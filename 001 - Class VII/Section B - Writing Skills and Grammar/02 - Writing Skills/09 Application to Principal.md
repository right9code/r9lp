# Application to Principal

## 📌 What is an Application to the Principal?
An **Application to the Principal** is a type of formal letter written by a student (or parent) to the head of the school requesting permission, leaves, fee concessions, or issuing certificates. The tone must be highly respectful, polite, and strictly to the point.

## 📝 Format / Structure 

The letter follows a strict left-aligned formal format:

1. **Receiver’s Designation and Address**: 
   - *The Principal,*
   - *Name of the School,*
   - *City/Address.*
2. **Date**: E.g., *15 September 2026*
3. **Subject**: A brief line stating the purpose of the application.
4. **Salutation**: *Respected Sir/Madam,*
5. **Body of the Letter**:
   - **Introduction**: Introduce yourself (Name, Class, Section) and state the reason for writing.
   - **Main Content**: Provide necessary details (dates of leave, reason for request, etc.).
   - **Conclusion**: A polite request for action and an expression of gratitude.
6. **Closing / Subscription**: *Thanking you, / Yours obediently,*
7. **Signature & Details**: Name, Class, and Roll Number.

## ⚖️ CBSE Marking Scheme (Typically 5 Marks)
- **Format**: 1.5 Marks
- **Content**: 2 Marks
- **Expression**: 1.5 Marks (Accuracy, polite tone)

## 💡 Language Tips & Key Rules
- **Tone**: Formal, humble, and persuasive. Never demand; always request.
- **Subject Line**: Keep it concise (e.g., *Subject: Application for three days sick leave.*).
- **Useful Phrases**:
  - *"With due respect, I would like to state that..."*
  - *"I request you to kindly grant me leave for..."*
  - *"I shall be highly obliged to you for this favor."*

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Using informal greetings like "Dear Sir". Always use "Respected Sir/Madam".
- Forgetting to write the Class and Section. The Principal needs to know who you are.
- Rambling and giving unnecessary personal details. Stick strictly to the reason.

## 🌟 Model Example

**Q: You are Kunal/Kavita, a student of Class VII A. Write an application to the Principal of your school requesting two days' leave to attend your sister's wedding.**

The Principal,  
Delhi Public School,  
New Delhi.  

10 November 2026  

Subject: Application for two days' leave to attend sister's wedding.

Respected Sir,  

With due respect, I, Kunal Sharma, a student of Class VII-A of your school, wish to state that my elder sister's wedding ceremony is scheduled to take place on 14th November 2026. Because of the preparations and the ceremony itself, I will not be able to attend school.

Therefore, I kindly request you to grant me leave for two days, i.e., from 13th November to 14th November 2026. I assure you that I will complete all my pending classwork as soon as I return.

I shall be highly obliged for your kindness.

Thanking you,  

Yours obediently,  
Kunal Sharma  
Class VII-A  
Roll No: 15
