# Informal Letter (Advice / Apology)

## 📌 What is this type of Informal Letter?
While informal letters are generally written to family and friends to share news, specific types of informal letters are frequently asked in exams:
1. **Giving Advice**: Writing to a younger sibling or friend to guide them about studies, health, or behavior.
2. **Apologizing**: Writing to a friend or relative to say sorry for a mistake or for missing an event.

## 📝 Format / Structure (Word Limit: 100-120 words)

The format remains the standard left-aligned informal format:

1. **Sender’s Address**: Top left corner.
2. **Date**: Below the address.
3. **Salutation**: E.g., *Dear [Name],*
4. **Body of the Letter**:
   - **Introduction**: Ask about their health. State why you are writing (to advise or to apologize).
   - **Main Content**: 
     - *For Advice*: Explain the benefits of good habits and give 2-3 specific tips.
     - *For Apology*: Clearly state what you did wrong, express genuine regret, and promise not to repeat it.
   - **Conclusion**: Warm closing remarks.
5. **Subscription**: *Yours affectionately, / Yours lovingly,*
6. **Name**: First name.

## ⚖️ CBSE Marking Scheme (Typically 5 Marks)
- **Format**: 1 Mark
- **Content**: 2 Marks
- **Expression**: 2 Marks (Emotional tone, grammar)

## 💡 Language Tips & Useful Phrases
- **For Advice**:
  - *"I heard from mother that you are neglecting your studies..."*
  - *"You must realize the importance of..."*
  - *"I suggest that you make a timetable and stick to it."*
- **For Apology**:
  - *"I am writing this letter to sincerely apologize for..."*
  - *"I feel terrible about what happened..."*
  - *"Please forgive me. I promise it won't happen again."*

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Sounding too angry (in advice)**: Even if you are advising a younger sibling, keep the tone encouraging and loving, not harsh.
- **Making excuses (in apology)**: If you are apologizing, take responsibility. Don't blame others.

## 🌟 Model Example

**Q: You are Sumit, living in a hostel at 14, Boys Hostel, Delhi. Write a letter to your younger brother advising him to participate in sports and not just focus on video games.**

14, Boys Hostel  
Delhi  

12 September 2026  

Dear Rohit,  

I hope you and mom are doing well. I received mom’s letter yesterday, and she mentioned that you have stopped going out to play and spend all your free time playing video games.

Rohit, while playing video games is fun, it is very harmful to sit in front of a screen all day. It damages your eyesight and makes you lazy. You must remember that "a healthy mind lives in a healthy body." Outdoor sports are essential to keep you physically fit and active. They also teach you teamwork and discipline. 

I strongly advise you to join the school football team or at least go cycling in the evening. I hope you will listen to my advice and change your routine. Take care of yourself.

Yours affectionately,  
Sumit

## ✍️ Practice Prompts

1. **Apology:** You borrowed your friend's favorite storybook and accidentally tore a few pages. Write a letter apologizing to him and offering to replace the book.
2. **Advice:** Write a letter to your friend advising her to read the newspaper daily to improve her general knowledge and vocabulary.
