# Informal Letter

## 📌 What is an Informal Letter?
An **informal letter** is written to close acquaintances, such as family members, friends, and relatives. The language used is casual, personal, and friendly, as opposed to the formal tone used in official correspondence.

## 📝 Format / Structure (Word Limit: 100-120 words)

The format of an informal letter generally follows the left-aligned layout:

1. **Sender’s Address**: Written at the top left corner.
2. **Date**: Written below the address (e.g., 24 May 2026).
3. **Salutation (Greeting)**: E.g., *Dear Mom, Dear Rahul,*
4. **Body of the Letter**:
   - **Paragraph 1 (Introduction)**: Ask about their well-being / state the purpose of the letter. (e.g., *Hope you are doing well.*)
   - **Paragraph 2 (Main Content)**: The actual message or details.
   - **Paragraph 3 (Conclusion)**: Closing remarks, sending love to other family members.
5. **Subscription (Closing)**: E.g., *Yours lovingly, Your affectionate friend,*
6. **Sender’s Name/Signature**: First name only.

## ⚖️ CBSE Marking Scheme (Typically 5 Marks)
- **Format**: 1 Mark (Address, Date, Salutation, Closing)
- **Content**: 2 Marks (Ideas, logic, relevance)
- **Expression**: 2 Marks (Grammar, vocabulary, spelling, coherence)

## 💡 Language Tips & Useful Phrases
- **Opening**: 
  - *"I hope this letter finds you in the best of health."*
  - *"It was great to hear from you."*
  - *"I am writing this letter to tell you about..."*
- **Closing**: 
  - *"Give my regards to Uncle and Aunt."*
  - *"Hope to see you soon."*
  - *"Write back soon!"*

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Using formal language**: Do not use "Respected" or "Yours faithfully" in an informal letter.
- **Punctuation errors in format**: Do not put commas after the address lines or date in the modern left-aligned format.
- **Forgetting the date**: The date is a crucial part of the format.

## 🌟 Model Example

**Q: You are Rohan/Riya living at 45, Model Town, Delhi. Write a letter to your friend inviting them to spend the summer vacation with you.**

45, Model Town  
Delhi  

15 May 2026  

Dear Amit,  

I hope this letter finds you in good health and high spirits. I am writing to invite you to spend the upcoming summer vacation with me in Delhi.

It has been a long time since we last met, and I have planned a lot of exciting activities for us. We can visit historical monuments like the Red Fort and Qutub Minar, and enjoy the famous street food of Chandni Chowk. We will also spend some time playing video games and reading our favorite comic books. My parents are also very eager to see you.

Please discuss this with your parents and let me know your arrival date so I can pick you up from the station. Give my warm regards to uncle and aunt.

Yours affectionately,  
Rohan
