# Story Writing

## 📌 What is Story Writing?
**Story writing** is a creative composition that involves narrating an event or a series of events. It requires imagination, a structured plot, characters, and a clear setting. In exams, you are usually given an opening line (prompt), an ending line, or a visual cue to build your story upon.

## 📝 Format / Structure (Word Limit: 150-200 words)

1. **Title**: Must be catchy and relevant to the plot.
2. **Introduction (Beginning)**: 
   - Introduce the main characters.
   - Establish the setting (time and place).
   - Use the provided starting prompt (if any).
3. **Body (Middle - The Plot & Conflict)**:
   - Develop the plot. What happens next?
   - Introduce a conflict, problem, or an unexpected twist.
   - Build suspense or interest.
4. **Conclusion (Ending)**:
   - Resolve the conflict.
   - State the moral of the story (optional, but good if the story dictates it).
   - Ensure the ending feels complete and logical.

## ⚖️ CBSE Marking Scheme (Typically 5-8 Marks)
- **Title & Moral**: 1 Mark
- **Content (Creativity, Plot, Characterization)**: 3-4 Marks
- **Expression (Vocabulary, Grammar, Sequencing, Coherence)**: 2-3 Marks

## 💡 Language Tips & Key Rules
- **Tense**: Always use the **Past Tense** unless there is dialogue in quotes.
- **Dialogue**: Use dialogue sparingly to make the characters feel alive, properly punctuated (e.g., *“Look out!” he shouted.*).
- **Transition Words**: Use sequence words to show the passage of time (e.g., *Meanwhile, suddenly, the next morning, eventually, to his surprise*).
- **Show, Don't Tell**: Instead of saying "He was scared," write "His hands trembled and a cold sweat broke out on his forehead."

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Ignoring the prompt**: If a starting line is given, you MUST start your story with those exact words.
- **Switching tenses**: Starting in the past tense and accidentally moving to the present tense mid-story.
- **Unrealistic endings**: "And then I woke up and it was all a dream" is considered a weak, cliché ending. Try to resolve the plot creatively.

## 🌟 Model Example

**Q: Write a story in 150-200 words beginning with the following line: "I was walking alone in the dark forest when suddenly..." Give it a suitable title.**

**The Mysterious Light**

I was walking alone in the dark forest when suddenly, I heard a sharp snapping sound behind me. My heart skipped a beat, and my palms grew sweaty. The dense canopy of trees blocked out the moonlight, leaving me entirely in the shadows. I quickened my pace, but the footsteps behind me seemed to match mine.

Just as panic began to set in, I saw a faint, warm, golden glow emerging from behind a large oak tree ahead. Cautiously, I crept towards it, hoping for safety. To my absolute astonishment, sitting by a small, magically glowing campfire, was a tiny, old man with a long silver beard. He looked up, smiled warmly, and gestured for me to sit down. 

"Lost, are we?" he chuckled, handing me a cup of sweet-smelling tea. The menacing footsteps behind me had vanished. I spent the rest of the night listening to his fascinating tales of the forest. When I woke up the next morning, the man was gone, leaving only cold ashes and a safe path leading straight to my village.
