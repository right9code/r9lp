# Diary Entry

## 📌 What is a Diary Entry?
A **diary entry** is a personal piece of writing where an individual records their thoughts, feelings, experiences, and daily events. It is treated as a secret confidante. Because it is personal, the language is informal, honest, and reflective.

## 📝 Format / Structure (Word Limit: 100-120 words)

The standard CBSE format for a diary entry is left-aligned:

1. **Date**: Written on the top left (e.g., 24 May 2026).
2. **Day**: Written directly below the date (e.g., Monday).
3. **Time**: Written below the day (e.g., 9:30 PM). *(Most diary entries are written at night).*
4. **Salutation**: E.g., *Dear Diary,* (Optional but recommended).
5. **Body**: 
   - A description of the event or incident.
   - Your emotional reaction or thoughts about it.
   - Future hopes or plans regarding the incident.
6. **Sign-off / Signature**: First name of the writer at the bottom left.

## ⚖️ CBSE Marking Scheme (Typically 5 Marks)
- **Format**: 1 Mark (Date, Day, Time, Salutation, Name)
- **Content**: 2 Marks (Relevance to the prompt, ideas)
- **Expression**: 2 Marks (Grammar, vocabulary, emotional tone)

## 💡 Language Tips & Key Rules
- **Pronouns**: ALWAYS use the **first-person perspective** ("I", "we", "my").
- **Tense**: Usually written in the **Simple Past Tense** (for events that happened that day) and **Present/Future Tense** (for feelings and future plans).
- **Tone**: Conversational, emotional, and expressive. You can use exclamation marks to express strong feelings!
- **Sentence Starters**:
  - *"Today was the most wonderful/terrible day..."*
  - *"I can't believe what happened today..."*
  - *"I am feeling so proud/guilty about..."*

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Writing a formal essay instead of pouring out feelings.
- Forgetting to write the time (usually night time).
- Exceeding the word limit by explaining unnecessary, irrelevant daily routines.

## 🌟 Model Example

**Q: You are Aman/Amita. Today, you rescued an injured puppy from the street and took it to the vet. Write a diary entry expressing your feelings about the incident.**

12 August 2026  
Wednesday  
9:00 PM  

Dear Diary,  

Today was an emotionally exhausting but ultimately fulfilling day. While walking back from school, I noticed a tiny puppy shivering near the pavement, whimpering in pain. Its hind leg was injured. My heart melted instantly. Without a second thought, I wrapped him in my jacket and rushed to the nearest veterinary clinic. 

The doctor treated his wound and told me that he would have been in severe danger if delayed. Seeing the relief in the puppy’s eyes made me feel incredibly happy and proud of my actions. I have brought him home for now, and after some convincing, Mom has agreed to let us keep him! I can't wait to play with him tomorrow.

Aman

## ✍️ Practice Prompts

1. You recently visited an old age home with your classmates. Write a diary entry expressing how the visit made you feel and what you learned.
2. You were caught cheating in a class test and the teacher scolded you in front of everyone. Write a diary entry expressing your guilt and your resolution never to do it again.
