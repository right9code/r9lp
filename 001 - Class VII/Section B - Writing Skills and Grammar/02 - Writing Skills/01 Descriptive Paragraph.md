# Descriptive Paragraph (Person)

## 📌 What is a Descriptive Paragraph?
A **descriptive paragraph** is a piece of writing that vividly describes a person, place, or thing in such a way that a picture is formed in the reader's mind. When describing a **person**, it focuses on their physical appearance, personality traits, habits, and your relationship or opinion about them.

## 📝 Format / Structure (Word Limit: 100-120 words)

1. **Heading/Title**: Catchy and relevant (Optional but recommended).
2. **Introduction (1-2 sentences)**: 
   - Introduce the person.
   - State who they are and how you know them.
3. **Body (Physical Appearance & Personality) (5-6 sentences)**:
   - **Physical appearance**: Age, height, build, face, eyes, hair, clothes.
   - **Personality traits**: Qualities, habits, behavior, talents, interests.
   - Give examples or anecdotes to support the traits.
4. **Conclusion (1-2 sentences)**:
   - Your final thoughts, feelings, or opinion about the person.
   - Why they are special to you.

## ⚖️ CBSE Marking Scheme (Typically 5 Marks)
- **Format (Title)**: 1 Mark
- **Content**: 2 Marks
- **Expression (Grammar, Vocabulary, Coherence)**: 2 Marks

## 💡 Language Tips & Useful Vocabulary
- **Physical Traits**: Tall, short, well-built, slim, plump, fair, wheatish, round-faced, sharp features, curly/straight/wavy hair, sparkling eyes.
- **Personality Traits**: Honest, hardworking, cheerful, optimistic, generous, strict but kind, punctual, humorous, creative, introverted, extroverted.
- **Sentence Starters**:
  - *"One of the most inspiring people I know is..."*
  - *"He/She is not only known for... but also for..."*
  - *"What I admire most about him/her is..."*

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Avoid just making a list of adjectives. Use them in proper, connected sentences.
- Don't jump back and forth between physical traits and personality; organize them logically.
- Exceeding the word limit significantly. Stick to 100-120 words.

## 🌟 Model Example

**Q: Write a descriptive paragraph in 100-120 words describing your favorite teacher.**

**My Favorite Teacher**
Mrs. Sharma, my English teacher, is one of the most inspiring people I have ever met. She is a graceful lady in her late thirties, of medium height, with a warm, welcoming smile that instantly puts students at ease. She usually dresses in elegant cotton sarees. Beyond her neat appearance, what makes her truly special is her patient and affectionate nature. She has a unique, engaging way of teaching that makes even the most difficult grammar concepts easy to understand. She is strict about discipline but always approachable if we have doubts. Her optimistic attitude and constant encouragement motivate us to do our best. I deeply respect her and look forward to her classes every day.
