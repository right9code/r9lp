# Acrostic Poem Writing

## 📌 What is an Acrostic Poem?
An **Acrostic poem** is a fun and creative type of poetry where the first letter of each line spells out a specific word or phrase when read vertically downward. The word spelled out is usually the subject or theme of the poem. 

## 📝 Format / Structure

1. **The Theme Word**: Choose the word you want to write about. Write it vertically down the left side of the page, using capital letters.
2. **The Lines**: Each line of the poem must begin with the corresponding letter of the theme word.
3. **Rhyme & Rhythm**: Acrostic poems do **not** need to rhyme! They also do not need a specific rhythm. They can be single words, short phrases, or complete sentences.

## 💡 How to Write an Acrostic Poem (Step-by-Step)
- **Step 1**: Write your topic word vertically. (e.g., S-U-N)
- **Step 2**: Brainstorm words or phrases that describe your topic.
- **Step 3**: Look at the letters in your vertical word and see if any of your brainstormed words fit those letters.
- **Step 4**: Fill in the remaining lines. Ensure the whole poem connects to the main theme.

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Choosing lines that do not make sense together. All lines must relate to the vertical theme word.
- Forgetting to capitalize the first letter of each line.
- Trying too hard to make it rhyme, which can force the poem to sound unnatural.

## 🌟 Model Example

**Example 1: Single Word/Short Phrase Style**
*(Theme: SPRING)*

**S**unshine warming the earth  
**P**etals opening wide  
**R**ainbows in the sky  
**I**nsects buzzing around  
**N**ew leaves on the trees  
**G**reen grass growing tall  

**Example 2: Full Sentence Style**
*(Theme: FRIEND)*

**F**inding someone who always listens to you,  
**R**eady to help when you are feeling blue.  
**I**nside jokes that only the two of you share,  
**E**very single day showing how much they care.  
**N**ever judging you for making a mistake,  
**D**oing whatever it takes for your friendship's sake.

## ✍️ Practice Prompts

1. Write an acrostic poem using the letters of your own **FIRST NAME**. Describe your personality traits.
2. Write an acrostic poem using the word **SCHOOL**. Use short phrases to describe what you do there.
