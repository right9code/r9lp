# Limerick Poem Writing

## 📌 What is a Limerick?
A **Limerick** is a short, humorous, and often nonsensical poem consisting of five lines. It has a very specific rhythm and rhyme scheme. Limericks are meant to be fun, lighthearted, and bouncy to read aloud.

## 📝 Format / Structure 

A limerick strictly follows these rules:
1. **Length**: It must be exactly five lines long.
2. **Rhyme Scheme**: It follows an **AABBA** rhyme scheme.
   - Line 1, Line 2, and Line 5 rhyme with each other (A).
   - Line 3 and Line 4 rhyme with each other (B).
3. **Rhythm (Meter)**: It has a specific bouncy rhythm.
   - Lines 1, 2, and 5 are longer (usually 8-9 syllables) and have 3 strong beats.
   - Lines 3 and 4 are shorter (usually 5-6 syllables) and have 2 strong beats.

## 💡 How to Write a Limerick (Step-by-Step)
- **Step 1**: Start with a subject, usually a person and where they are from. (e.g., *There once was a boy from Peru...*)
- **Step 2**: Think of rhyming words for the end of the first line (e.g., *shoe, blue, flew*). This will be your Line 2 and Line 5 ending.
- **Step 3**: Write Line 2, building on the story.
- **Step 4**: Think of a new rhyming pair for Lines 3 and 4 (e.g., *night/light*, *cat/hat*). Keep these lines short!
- **Step 5**: Write Line 5, resolving the funny situation, making sure it rhymes with Lines 1 and 2.

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Breaking the AABBA rhyme scheme.
- Making the poem too serious; limericks are meant to be silly!
- Making lines 3 and 4 too long. They must be noticeably shorter than the others.

## 🌟 Model Example

**Example 1: The Classic Limerick Rhythm**

There once was a fellow named Tim, **(A)** *(3 beats)*
Who never knew quite how to swim. **(A)** *(3 beats)*
He fell in the bay, **(B)** *(2 beats)*
On a cold winter's day, **(B)** *(2 beats)*
Now nobody's looking for him! **(A)** *(3 beats)*

**Example 2: An Animal Theme**

A wonderful bird is the pelican, **(A)**
His beak can hold more than his belly can. **(A)**
He can take in his beak, **(B)**
Enough food for a week, **(B)**
But I'm entirely unsure how the hellican. **(A)**
