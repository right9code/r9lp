# Formal Letter

## 📌 What is a Formal Letter?
A **formal letter** is written for official, professional, or business purposes. It is written to people in authority, such as editors, managers, principals, or municipal officers. The language must be strictly formal, polite, clear, and to the point.

## 📝 Format / Structure (Word Limit: 100-120 words)

The format follows a strict left-aligned layout:

1. **Sender’s Address**: Written at the top left.
2. **Date**: Written below the sender's address (e.g., 20 May 2026).
3. **Receiver’s Address**: Designation and address of the person receiving the letter.
4. **Subject**: A brief one-line summary of the letter's purpose.
5. **Salutation**: *Respected Sir/Madam* or *Dear Sir/Madam*.
6. **Body of the Letter**:
   - **Paragraph 1 (Introduction)**: Identify yourself and state the clear purpose of the letter.
   - **Paragraph 2 (Main Details)**: Explain the issue, provide facts, or give necessary information.
   - **Paragraph 3 (Conclusion)**: What action you expect the receiver to take.
7. **Closing**: *Thanking you,*
8. **Subscription**: *Yours faithfully,* or *Yours sincerely,*
9. **Signature & Name**: Your signature and full name.

## ⚖️ CBSE Marking Scheme (Typically 5-8 Marks)
- **Format**: 1-2 Marks (All 9 elements in correct order)
- **Content**: 3-4 Marks (Clear explanation of the issue/request)
- **Expression**: 2 Marks (Formal tone, grammar, accurate spelling)

## 💡 Language Tips & Useful Phrases
- **Tone**: Objective and respectful. Avoid contractions (don't, can't - use do not, cannot).
- **Opening Phrases**:
  - *"I am writing to bring to your attention..."*
  - *"On behalf of the residents of [Area], I would like to request..."*
- **Closing Phrases**:
  - *"I look forward to a prompt response."*
  - *"I request you to look into this matter urgently."*

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Using informal greetings like "Hey" or "Hi".
- Writing long, emotional stories. Stick to the facts.
- Mixing up the sender's and receiver's addresses. Sender is always on top!

## 🌟 Model Example

**Q: You are Ramesh, a resident of 12, Civil Lines, Kanpur. Write a formal letter to the Municipal Commissioner complaining about the irregular water supply in your locality.**

12, Civil Lines  
Kanpur  

24 May 2026  

The Municipal Commissioner  
Municipal Corporation  
Kanpur  

**Subject:** Complaint regarding irregular water supply in Civil Lines.

Respected Sir,  

I am writing on behalf of the residents of Civil Lines to bring to your attention the severe issue of irregular water supply in our locality for the past three weeks.

The water is supplied for barely twenty minutes a day, and the pressure is so low that the water tanks on the first floor do not get filled. Sometimes, the water supplied is muddy and unfit for consumption. This has caused immense hardship to the residents, especially during this peak summer season.

We have lodged several complaints at the local water office, but no action has been taken. I kindly request you to look into this matter urgently and ensure a regular and clean water supply to our area.

Thanking you,  

Yours faithfully,  
Ramesh Singh
