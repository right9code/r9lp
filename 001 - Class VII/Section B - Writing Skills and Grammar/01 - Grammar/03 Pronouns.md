### **C. Rewrite the sentences correctly.**

1. **They blamed themselves.** (Correct as is, or if meant to be reciprocal: *They blamed each other.*)
2. **I am shorter than him.**
   * I am shorter than **he**. (Implicitly: *than he is*)
3. **Which is the news that you bring?**
   * **What** is the news that you bring?
4. **It was him who should have protested.**
   * It was **he** who should have protested.
5. **I and Ram have been chosen to lead the team.**
   * **Ram and I** have been chosen to lead the team. (231 rule: mention others before yourself)
6. **Which is the purpose of this plan?**
   * **What** is the purpose of this plan?
7. **She and me are to blame for this mess.**
   * **She and I** are to blame for this mess. (Subject case)
8. **The members of the jury was divided in its opinion.**
   * The members of the jury **were** divided in **their** opinions.
9. **Between you and I, there is no truth in this.**
   * Between you and **me**, there is no truth in this. (Object of preposition)
10. **One must be careful about what he says.**
    * One must be careful about what **one** says.
11. **Anybody was there to save the drowning child.**
    * **Nobody** was there to save the drowning child. (Contextual correction)
12. **My uncle asked my sister and I to accompany him.**
    * My uncle asked my sister and **me** to accompany him. (Object case)
13. **My aunt and myself decided to see a film.**
    * My aunt and **I** decided to see a film.
14. **Any of the children are ready to participate in the programme tomorrow.**
    * **Each** of the children **is** ready to participate in the programme tomorrow.
15. **Our players were better prepared than that of China.**
    * Our players were better prepared than **those** of China. (Plural reference to players)
16. **The money has been stolen by anybody who entered the room when we were away.**
    * The money has been stolen by **someone** who entered the room when we were away.

### **E. Join these sets of sentences with relative pronouns.**

1. **The boy arrived late. He was scolded by his teacher.**
   * The boy who arrived late was scolded by his teacher.
2. **The tiger was pursuing a buck. The buck was injured.**
   * The tiger was pursuing a buck **which** was injured.
3. **Bring me the purse. It is on the bed.**
   * Bring me the purse **that/which** is on the bed.
4. **This is the prize. He won it for his essay.**
   * This is the prize **which/that** he won for his essay.
5. **This is the man. We were saved by his presence of mind.**
   * This is the man **whose** presence of mind saved us.
6. **Unfortunately, I missed the bus. It was to take me to Shimla.**
   * Unfortunately, I missed the bus **which** was to take me to Shimla.
7. **Here is the surgeon. He will operate on my friend tomorrow.**
   * Here is the surgeon **who** will operate on my friend tomorrow.
8. **Let us visit the old market. It was set up by the British.**
   * Let us visit the old market **which** was set up by the British.
