# Paper Boats - Class 7 Comprehensive Notes

**Poet:** Rabindranath Tagore
**(From the textbook *Poorvi*)**

---

## Complete Poem Text

Day by day I float my paper boats one by one down the running stream.
In big black letters I write my name on them and the name of the village where I live.
I hope that someone in some strange land will find them and know who I am.

I load my little boats with shiuli flowers from our garden,
and hope that these blooms of the dawn will be carried safely to land in the night.

I launch my paper boats and look up into the sky
and see the little clouds setting their white bulging sails.
I know not what playmate of mine in the sky sends them down the air to race with my boats!

When night comes I bury my face in my arms
and dream that my paper boats float on and on under the midnight stars.
The fairies of sleep are sailing in them,
and the lading is their baskets full of dreams.

**— Rabindranath Tagore**

---

## Detailed Summary

"Paper Boats" is a beautiful, imaginative poem by the legendary Indian poet Rabindranath Tagore. It captures the innocence, hope, and boundless creativity of childhood through the simple act of a child setting paper boats afloat on a stream.

### Stanza-wise Overview

*   **The Daily Ritual:** The poem opens by describing a young child’s daily ritual. Every day, the child lovingly crafts paper boats and releases them, one by one, to float down a running stream. For the child, this is not just play; it is an important daily mission.
*   **A Message to the World:** Before setting the boats on the water, the child carefully writes their own name and the name of their village in big black letters on the paper. The child harbors a hopeful, innocent fantasy that someone in a distant, unknown land will find these boats, read the names, and discover who the child is and where they come from.
*   **Gifts for the Journey:** To make the boats special, the child loads them with delicate *shiuli* flowers picked from their garden in the early morning. The child hopes that these fragile "blooms of the dawn" will survive the long journey and safely reach a faraway shore by nighttime.
*   **Racing the Clouds:** As the child watches the boats float away, they look up at the sky. Through the lens of imagination, the child sees the little white clouds in the sky as competing ships with bulging sails, racing alongside the paper boats on the water.
*   **The Dream World:** As night falls, the child goes to sleep, but their imagination continues to actively work. In their dreams, the child sees the paper boats still floating steadily under the midnight stars. The child dreams that the "fairies of sleep" are sailing in the boats, carrying baskets full of magical dreams.

---

## Central Themes

### 1. Childhood Innocence and Imagination
The poem beautifully highlights how a child’s mind operates without the boundaries of adult reality. A simple piece of folded paper becomes a grand vessel, and clouds become racing ships. The poem celebrates the pure, magical perspective of youth.

### 2. Yearning for Connection
By writing their name and village on the boats, the child expresses a universal human desire: the longing to reach out, to be known, and to make a connection with the wider, unknown world beyond their immediate surroundings.

### 3. Hope and the Journey of Life
The paper boats serve as a powerful metaphor for life itself. Just as the child sends the fragile boats down the unpredictable stream hoping they will reach a safe shore, humans send their hopes and dreams into the unknown future.

## Detailed Poetic Devices Analysis

**Rhyme Scheme:** Free Verse (prose poem) — no regular rhyme scheme or metre. The flowing, unstructured form mirrors the natural movement of water and the free-flowing imagination of a child.

**1. Symbolism**
The poem is rich in symbolic meaning:
*   **Paper Boats:** Symbolize the child's hopes, dreams, and innocent aspirations sent out into the unknown world.
*   **The Stream:** Symbolizes the continuous, unstoppable flow of time and life's journey into the future.
*   **Shiuli Flowers:** Symbolize the beauty, purity, and fragility of childhood itself. They are the child's gift to the unknown.
*   **Clouds with "bulging sails":** Symbolize the limitless scope of a child's imagination.
*   **Midnight Stars:** Symbolize the boundary between reality and the dream world.

**2. Imagery**
The poet uses vivid, multi-sensory imagery:
*   *Visual:* *"big black letters," "little clouds setting their white bulging sails," "under the midnight stars"* — creates a dreamlike, picturesque atmosphere.
*   *Tactile:* *"I bury my face in my arms"* — the physical gesture of falling asleep.
*   *Kinesthetic:* *"float on and on," "down the running stream"* — movement of the boats and water.

**3. Personification**
*   *"The fairies of sleep are sailing in them"* — sleep is personified as having magical fairy creatures.
*   *"the little clouds setting their white bulging sails"* — clouds are personified as ships with sails.
*   *"what playmate of mine in the sky sends them down"* — an invisible sky-being is imagined as a playmate.

**4. Metaphor**
*   *"blooms of the dawn"* — the shiuli flowers are compared to the dawn itself, linking them to new beginnings.
*   *Clouds as ships:* *"little clouds setting their white bulging sails"* — clouds are transformed into sailing vessels.
*   *"the lading is their baskets full of dreams"* — the cargo of the fairy ships is made of dreams, not goods.

**5. Contrast**
*   **Day vs. Night:** During the day, the child floats boats in reality; at night, the boats continue floating in dreams.
*   **Known vs. Unknown:** The child writes their village name (known) hoping someone in a "strange land" (unknown) will find it.
*   **Reality vs. Fantasy:** The act of floating paper boats is real; fairies sailing them under stars is fantasy.

**6. Repetition**
*   The phrase *"paper boats"* is repeated throughout, anchoring the poem's central image.
*   *"I hope"* appears in stanzas 1 and 2, emphasizing the child's persistent, innocent optimism.

**7. Tone**
The tone is **dreamy, innocent, and wonder-filled.** It is gentle and tender throughout, perfectly capturing the imaginative world of a child. There is a quiet melancholy in the child's solitary play, but it is overwhelmed by hope and magical thinking.

## Textbook Questions and Answers

### II. Analytical and Reflective Questions

**1. Why do you think the poet writes his personal details on the paper boats?**
**Ans:** The poet writes his personal details in the innocent hope that someone in a distant land will find them. By sending out these fragile vessels, he expresses a universal childhood desire to reach out, connect with strangers, and leave a mark on the wider world.

**2. The poet talks about the imagination of a child. Support this view with examples from the poem.**
**Ans:** The poem is deeply infused with a child's boundless imagination, particularly in how the boy visualizes the journey of his simple paper boats. He doesn't just see them floating down a stream; he imagines them surviving long journeys into strange, distant lands. Furthermore, he vividly imagines the "fairies of sleep" sailing in his boats, carrying baskets full of dreams under the moonlight. He also imagines that the little clouds in the sky are actually racing his boats as "white bulging sails," transforming ordinary reality into an extraordinary fantasy.

**3. How does the poet bring together imagination and reality to make the poem more interesting?**
**Ans:** The poet masterfully blends reality—the tactile act of folding paper and floating boats on a real stream—with dreamlike elements. The seamless transition from physical water to imaginary fairies seamlessly captures the unique, wonderful way a child's mind experiences the world.
