# My Dear Soldiers - Class 7 Comprehensive Notes

**Poet:** Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

## Complete Poem Text

My dear soldiers,
You are the defenders of our frontiers,
Guarding our land, our sea, our air,
With vigilance beyond compare.

When we are all asleep,
You still hold on to your deed.
Windy season or snowy days,
Or scorching sun's sweltering rays.

You are there guarding all the time,
Awake, treading the lonely expanses as yogis,
In mountains high and valleys deep,
In deserts wide where sand-storms creep.

Prime of your youth given to the nation!!
You are the great sons of my land,
Your selfless service, strong and true,
The nation owes its peace to you.

May God bless you,
My dear soldiers,
May your valour shine forever bright,
Guardians of our land, our pride, our light.

**— Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam**

---

## Detailed Summary

"My Dear Soldiers" is a deeply moving, patriotic poem written by the former President of India, Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam. It serves as a heartfelt tribute to the men and women of the Indian Armed Forces, honoring their immense sacrifices and unwavering dedication to protecting the nation.

### Stanza-wise Overview

*   **Defenders of the Borders:** The poem opens by highlighting the vast and diverse responsibility of the soldiers. The poet acknowledges that soldiers are the ultimate protectors of the nation, guarding its land borders, its sea coasts, and its airspace. They are the vigilant shield that keeps the country safe from external threats.
*   **Enduring Extreme Hardships:** Dr. Kalam draws attention to the incredibly harsh conditions under which soldiers operate. They perform their duties relentlessly, regardless of the extreme weather—whether it is freezing snow, strong freezing winds, or scorching heat. They serve in the most challenging terrains, including high mountains, deep valleys, dry deserts, and treacherous marshes.
*   **The Sacrifice of Youth:** The poem poignantly notes the personal cost of this service. The soldiers dedicate the "prime of their youth"—their most energetic, vibrant, and productive years—to the nation. Instead of enjoying a comfortable civilian life, they spend their best years facing danger and isolation.
*   **The Yogis of the Nation:** In a powerful symbolic comparison, the poet likens the soldiers to "yogis" (spiritual ascetics). Like yogis, soldiers possess immense self-discipline, inner strength, and the ability to endure extreme physical hardship and solitude, all for a higher, selfless purpose—the protection of their motherland.
*   **A Prayer of Gratitude:** In the concluding lines, the poet addresses the soldiers directly, calling them the "great sons of my land." He offers a collective expression of profound gratitude on behalf of all citizens and ends the poem with a sincere prayer to God, asking for the soldiers' safety, well-being, and blessings.

## Central Themes

### 1. Gratitude and Patriotism
The primary theme of the poem is cultivating a deep sense of respect, gratitude, and patriotism. The poem reminds the reader that the peace and security enjoyed by ordinary citizens are only possible because of the silent, vigilant sacrifices made by the armed forces.

### 2. Sacrifice and Selflessness
The poem heavily emphasizes the theme of sacrifice. Soldiers are portrayed as selfless individuals who give up the comforts of home, the safety of civilian life, and the very best years of their youth to serve a cause greater than themselves.

### 3. Unyielding Endurance
The text highlights the incredible physical and mental endurance required to be a soldier. By detailing the harsh climates (snow, wind, heat) and terrains (mountains, deserts), the poet showcases the remarkable resilience of the armed forces.

## Detailed Poetic Devices Analysis

**Rhyme Scheme:** Loosely structured with some couplet and alternate rhymes (*frontiers/compare [slant], asleep/deed [slant], days/rays, time/yogis [free], deep/creep, nation/land [free], you/true, bright/light*). The poem prioritises emotional sincerity over strict metre.

**1. Metaphor**
*   *"treading the lonely expanses as yogis"* — the most powerful metaphor. Soldiers are compared to yogis (spiritual ascetics), elevating their physical endurance to a spiritual level. Like yogis, soldiers possess immense self-discipline, inner strength, and the ability to endure extreme hardship and solitude for a higher purpose.
*   *"Prime of your youth given to the nation!!"* — youth is compared to a precious gift that soldiers voluntarily offer.

**2. Imagery (Visual and Tactile)**
Vivid pictures of the soldiers' harsh environments:
*   *"Windy season or snowy days, / Or scorching sun's sweltering rays"* — tactile imagery of extreme cold and heat.
*   *"mountains high and valleys deep, / In deserts wide where sand-storms creep"* — visual imagery of varied, treacherous terrains.
*   *"treading the lonely expanses"* — the vast, desolate loneliness of frontier duty.

**3. Antithesis / Contrast**
The poem is built on contrasts between civilian comfort and military sacrifice:
*   *"When we are all asleep, / You still hold on to your deed"* — sleeping citizens vs. awake, vigilant soldiers.
*   *"Windy season or snowy days, / Or scorching sun's sweltering rays"* — contrasting extremes of weather endured equally.
*   **Effect:** Makes the soldier's sacrifice more poignant by highlighting what civilians take for granted.

**4. Apostrophe (Direct Address)**
The entire poem is addressed directly to "My dear soldiers":
*   *"My dear soldiers," "You are the defenders," "May God bless you"*
*   **Effect:** Creates an intimate, personal, and emotionally charged tribute rather than a detached description.

**5. Alliteration**
*   ***s***corching ***s***un's ***s***weltering — the repeated 's' sound mimics the hissing, relentless heat.
*   ***d***ear... ***d***efenders... ***d***eed
*   ***g***uarding... ***g***reat... ***G***od
*   **Effect:** Adds intensity and musical rhythm.

**6. Exclamatory Expression**
*   *"Prime of your youth given to the nation!!"* — the double exclamation marks emphasize the enormity of the sacrifice. It is not a calm statement but an emotional outburst of gratitude and awe.

**7. Tone**
The tone is **reverent, patriotic, and deeply grateful.** It progresses from acknowledgment of sacrifice (stanzas 1–3) to emotional tribute (stanza 4) to a prayerful blessing (stanza 5). The poet speaks not as a politician but as a fellow citizen expressing heartfelt thanks.

## Textbook Questions and Answers

### II. Analytical and Reflective Questions

**1. Who is the speaker of this poem? How can you say so?**
**Ans:** The speaker is a grateful, patriotic civilian representing the collective voice of the nation. We know this because the tone is deeply reverent and appreciative, directly addressing the soldiers as "Defenders of borders" and offering a heartfelt prayer for their continued safety.

**2. How is the theme of the poem conveyed through imagery? Support your answer with examples from the poem.**
**Ans:** The theme of selfless sacrifice is powerfully conveyed through imagery of extreme, unforgiving environments like "freezing cold," "blinding snow," and "torrential rain." These vivid descriptions help the reader physically imagine the immense bodily suffering the soldiers voluntarily accept.

**3. How does the poet highlight the commitment of the soldier? Provide examples from the poem to support your answer.**
**Ans:** The poet highlights the soldier's unwavering commitment by emphasizing their constant vigilance and their willingness to endure severe hardships without complaint. The poet states that the soldiers are "always awake" and stand tall like an "impregnable wall," suggesting an absolute, unbroken dedication to their duty, regardless of the time of day. Furthermore, the commitment is highlighted by the fact that they face "enemies" and "terrorists" head-on, willingly putting themselves in the "front line" of extreme danger to ensure peace.

**4. What is the significance of the line ‘Prime of your youth given to the nation!!’ with reference to sacrifice and service?**
**Ans:** The line is deeply significant because it highlights the most profound, irreversible sacrifice a soldier makes. While civilians spend their twenties and thirties—the "prime" of their lives—building careers, enjoying families, and experiencing the carefree joys of youth, soldiers spend these peak years stationed in hostile, isolated environments, facing death. It emphasizes that their service is not just a temporary job, but a lifelong dedication of their best, most vital years to a higher cause, sacrificing their own personal youth for the country's future.

**5. The poem ends with a prayer for the soldier’s well-being and blessings. Why is it important for each one of us to pray for the well-being of soldiers?**
**Ans:** Praying for soldiers is an act of profound spiritual gratitude and national solidarity. Since civilians cannot physically stand on the borders, offering prayers is a way to bridge that distance, acknowledging that the peace we enjoy is bought by their continuous sacrifices.
