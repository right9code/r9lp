## SECTION A - GRAMMAR
 1. The beggar asked the emperor for **alms**.
   * **Explanation:** The word "alim" is incorrect in this context. The intended word is "alms" (money, food, or clothes given to poor people), which is always used in its plural form but can take a singular or plural verb depending on context.
 2. The Chinese are hardworking **people**.
   * **Explanation:** "People" is already the plural form when referring to a group of human beings. "Peoples" is only used when referring to multiple different ethnic groups, nations, or races collectively. Here, we are talking about a single nationality, so "people" is correct.
 3. Measles **is** a highly infectious disease.
   * **Explanation:** Although "Measles" ends in an '-s', it is the name of a single disease. Therefore, it is a singular collective noun and requires the singular verb "is" rather than the plural "are".
 4. The beautiful **scenery** was captured by the camera.
   * **Explanation:** "Scenery" is an uncountable (mass) noun and cannot be made plural as "sceneries". It always takes a singular form and a singular verb ("was").
 5. **Ten thousand** men marched to the Town Hall. *(Alternative: **Thousands of** men marched to the Town Hall.)*
   * **Explanation:** When a specific cardinal number (like "Ten") precedes words like dozen, hundred, thousand, or million, the noun remains singular in form ("ten thousand"). You only add "-of" and make it plural ("thousands of") when the exact number is indefinite.

### 2. Summary of "A Funny Man"
"A Funny Man" by Natalie Joan is a whimsical and lighthearted poem about an eccentric individual who does everything in an upside-down or unconventional way. The "funny man" lives in a strange manner—he wears his clothes incorrectly, walks in peculiar ways, and flips normal daily routines on their head. The poem uses simple, rhythmic verse to create a sense of innocent humor and amusement. Through his harmless antics, the character represents the joy of non-conformity and imagination. The core essence of the poem is to celebrate silliness and bring a smile to the reader's face by contrasting the man's absurd behavior with the rigid rules of the normal world.
### 3. Short Answer Questions (Attempted: Q1 and Q2)
 * **How Doctor Dolittle's readiness to follow Polynesia the parrot's advice highlights listening to different viewpoints:**
   Doctor Dolittle’s willingness to listen to Polynesia shows that wisdom and valuable insights can come from the most unexpected sources, transcending species or status. By setting aside human ego and acknowledging that a bird might understand the animal kingdom better than him, he demonstrates true open-mindedness. This highlights that respecting and acting upon diverse perspectives can lead to personal growth, better understanding, and breaking down traditional barriers of communication.
 * **Qualities that made Doctor Dolittle famous among animals:**
   Doctor Dolittle became famous because of his extraordinary empathy, kindness, and dedication to animal welfare. Unlike other doctors, he took the time to learn animal languages under the guidance of his parrot, allowing him to understand their specific pains and needs directly. His patience, lack of greed, and genuine respect for animals as equals created a deep sense of trust, causing animals from all over the world to seek his medical help and spread tales of his benevolence.
