# Diary Entry

## 📌 What is a Diary Entry?
A **diary entry** is a personal, informal piece of writing where you record your daily events, inner thoughts, secrets, and feelings. Because it is meant for your eyes only, the language is highly personal, emotional, and reflective. In exams, it tests your ability to express complex emotions creatively.

## 📝 Format / Structure (Word Limit: 100-120 words)

The format is strictly left-aligned:

1. **Date**: E.g., *24 May 2026*
2. **Day**: E.g., *Sunday*
3. **Time**: E.g., *10:00 PM* (Usually written at night, reflecting on the day).
4. **Salutation**: *Dear Diary,* (Optional, but highly recommended).
5. **Body of the Entry**:
   - Begin with a general statement about how the day was (e.g., *Today was the best day of my life!*).
   - Describe the main incident or event.
   - Focus heavily on your **feelings and reactions** regarding the event.
   - End with a future hope, resolution, or a concluding thought.
6. **Signature/Name**: First name only.

## ⚖️ CBSE Marking Scheme (Typically 5 Marks)
- **Format**: 1 Mark (Date, Day, Time, Salutation, Name)
- **Content**: 2 Marks (Relevance to the given situation, ideas)
- **Expression**: 2 Marks (Grammar, emotional vocabulary, coherence)

## 💡 Language Tips & Key Rules
- **Perspective**: Always use the **First Person** ("I", "my", "we").
- **Tense**: Mix of **Past Tense** (to describe what happened) and **Present/Future Tense** (for current feelings and future plans).
- **Tone**: Conversational, honest, and expressive. Do not be formal.
- **Vocabulary of Emotion**: Exhilarated, devastated, overwhelmed, ecstatic, remorseful, grateful.

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Writing a formal report of the day instead of sharing feelings.
- Forgetting the Time (essential for diary formats).
- Exceeding the word limit by explaining trivial details (e.g., what you ate for breakfast) that do not relate to the prompt.

## 🌟 Model Example

**Q: You are Rohan/Riya. Today, the final results of the Inter-School Debate Competition were announced, and you secured the first position. Write a diary entry expressing your joy and excitement.**

15 November 2026  
Sunday  
9:30 PM  

Dear Diary,  

I cannot even begin to describe how incredibly ecstatic I am feeling tonight! Today, the results of the Inter-School Debate Competition were finally announced in the morning assembly, and I won the first prize! 

When the Principal called out my name, my heart skipped a beat. Walking up to the stage to receive the golden trophy in front of the entire school was a surreal experience. All those late nights practicing in front of the mirror and researching my topic have finally paid off. My parents were so proud when I showed them the trophy; Mom even had tears of joy in her eyes. It feels amazing to see my hard work rewarded. I am motivated to participate in more such competitions now. I am so exhausted but too happy to sleep!

Goodnight.  
Rohan

## ✍️ Practice Prompts

1. You witnessed a terrible road accident today on your way to school because a driver was speeding. Write a diary entry expressing your shock and your thoughts on road safety.
2. You had a bitter argument with your best friend today over a misunderstanding. Write a diary entry expressing your sadness and how you plan to resolve the issue tomorrow.
