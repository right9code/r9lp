# Formal Letter (Suggestion/Opinion)

## 📌 What is a Formal Letter of Suggestion?
A **formal letter of suggestion** (or providing an opinion) is written to an authority, such as a municipal officer, school principal, or government body, offering constructive ideas to improve a system, solve a community problem, or implement a new beneficial policy. The tone must be respectful, objective, and solution-oriented.

## 📝 Format / Structure (Word Limit: 100-120 words)

The format is strictly left-aligned:

1. **Sender’s Address**: Top left corner.
2. **Date**: E.g., *24 May 2026*.
3. **Receiver’s Designation and Address**: E.g., *The Principal...*
4. **Subject**: A brief line stating the topic (e.g., *Subject: Suggestion for introducing a period for mental health.*)
5. **Salutation**: *Respected Sir/Madam* or *Dear Sir/Madam*.
6. **Body of the Letter**:
   - **Paragraph 1 (Introduction)**: State who you are and the purpose of your letter. Mention the current issue or the area of improvement.
   - **Paragraph 2 (The Suggestions)**: Clearly outline your suggestions or ideas. Explain *why* these ideas would be beneficial.
   - **Paragraph 3 (Conclusion)**: Politely request the authority to consider your suggestions.
7. **Closing**: *Thanking you,*
8. **Subscription**: *Yours faithfully,* / *Yours obediently,* (if to Principal).
9. **Signature & Name**.

## ⚖️ CBSE Marking Scheme (Typically 5-8 Marks)
- **Format**: 1-2 Marks
- **Content**: 3-4 Marks (Logical, practical suggestions)
- **Expression**: 2 Marks (Polite tone, vocabulary, grammatical accuracy)

## 💡 Language Tips & Useful Phrases
- **Opening phrases**:
  - *"I am writing to humbly suggest a few improvements regarding..."*
  - *"As a concerned citizen/student, I would like to draw your attention to..."*
- **Giving Suggestions**:
  - *"I would highly recommend that..."*
  - *"It would be highly beneficial if we could implement..."*
  - *"Another constructive step would be..."*
- **Closing phrases**:
  - *"I hope you will consider my suggestions favorably."*
  - *"I look forward to seeing positive changes in this regard."*

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Sounding arrogant or demanding. You are making suggestions, not giving orders.
- Providing vague complaints without offering any actual solutions.

## 🌟 Model Example

**Q: You are Sumit/Sunita, the Head Boy/Girl of your school. Write a letter to the Principal suggesting the introduction of a 'Zero Period' for extracurricular activities.**

12, School Hostel  
Modern School, Delhi  

10 August 2026  

The Principal  
Modern School  
New Delhi  

**Subject**: Suggestion regarding the introduction of a 'Zero Period'.

Respected Madam,  

I, Sumit Sharma, the Head Boy of the school, am writing to you on behalf of the student council to suggest the introduction of a 'Zero Period' in our daily academic schedule.

Currently, students are entirely focused on academics and find very little time to pursue extracurricular interests like debate, dramatics, or music. I suggest that we could shorten each academic period by just 5 minutes to create a 40-minute 'Zero Period' right after the morning assembly. During this time, students could participate in various club activities. 

This initiative would not only help in the overall personality development of the students but also reduce academic stress, leading to better concentration in classes.

I humbly request you to consider this suggestion for the benefit of the student community.

Thanking you,  

Yours obediently,  
Sumit Sharma  
Head Boy

## ✍️ Practice Prompts

1. Write a letter to the Municipal Commissioner suggesting measures to improve the maintenance of public parks in your city (e.g., installing dustbins, hiring guards).
2. Write a letter to the Director of the local library suggesting the addition of a 'Digital Reading Room' equipped with computers and e-books for students.
