# Conversation / Dialogue Writing

## 📌 What is Conversation Writing?
**Conversation writing** is the art of drafting a natural dialogue between two or more characters. It is used to test your ability to construct realistic speech, maintain a logical flow of thoughts, and use appropriate punctuation and tone.

## 📝 Format / Structure 

1. **Setting (Optional)**: A brief bracketed statement setting the scene (e.g., *[At a busy railway station]*).
2. **Speaker Name**: Followed by a colon ( : ).
3. **Dialogue**: The spoken words.
4. **Stage Directions (Optional)**: Words in brackets indicating the speaker's tone or action (e.g., *(excitedly)*, *(pointing to the map)*).

## ⚖️ CBSE Marking Scheme (Typically 4-5 Marks)
- **Format**: 1 Mark
- **Content (Logical progression, addressing the prompt)**: 2 Marks
- **Expression (Grammar, natural tone)**: 1-2 Marks

## 💡 Language Tips & Key Rules
- **Use Contractions**: Spoken English uses *I'm, don't, won't, let's*. Use them to make it sound authentic.
- **Interrupting/Pausing**: Use ellipses (...) to show someone trailing off, or dashes (—) to show someone being interrupted.
- **Maintain Balance**: Ensure both speakers have a roughly equal share of the dialogue.

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Forgetting the colon (:) after the name.
- Using quotation marks (" ") around the dialogue in this specific script format.

## 🌟 Model Example

**Q: Write a conversation between two friends discussing their plans for the upcoming summer vacation.**

*(In the school cafeteria during recess)*

**Ravi**: Hey, Amit! Only one week left until the summer vacations begin. I can't wait!  
**Amit**: *(smiling)* Me neither! The heat has been unbearable lately. Do you have any special plans?  
**Ravi**: Yes, my family is planning a trip to Manali for ten days. We are going to try trekking and maybe even river rafting! What about you?  
**Amit**: That sounds amazing! I am not going anywhere fancy. My grandparents are coming over from the village to stay with us for a month.  
**Ravi**: Oh, that’s great too. You must be excited to see them.  
**Amit**: Absolutely. My grandmother promised to teach me how to bake. Also, I am planning to join that new swimming camp at the sports complex.  
**Ravi**: A swimming camp is a brilliant idea to beat this heat. Maybe I'll join you after I get back from Manali.  
**Amit**: That would be perfect! We can practice together. Anyway, the bell is about to ring. Let's head back to class.
