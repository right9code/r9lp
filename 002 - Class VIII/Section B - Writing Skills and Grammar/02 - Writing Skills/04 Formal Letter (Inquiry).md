# Formal Letter (Inquiry)

## 📌 What is a Letter of Inquiry?
A **letter of inquiry** is a formal letter written to request information about a product, service, course, or event. It is typically addressed to institutions, businesses, or organizations. The tone must be polite, formal, and specific about the details required.

## 📝 Format / Structure (Word Limit: 100-120 words)

The standard left-aligned formal format is used:

1. **Sender’s Address**: Top left corner.
2. **Date**: E.g., *10 June 2026*.
3. **Receiver’s Designation and Address**: E.g., *The Director, XYZ Institute...*
4. **Subject**: A brief line stating the purpose (e.g., *Subject: Inquiry regarding summer coding courses.*)
5. **Salutation**: *Respected Sir/Madam* or *Dear Sir/Madam*.
6. **Body of the Letter**:
   - **Paragraph 1 (Introduction)**: State where you saw the advertisement/learned about the institution and why you are writing.
   - **Paragraph 2 (Main Details)**: Clearly list the specific information you need (e.g., fee structure, timings, batch size, transport). You can use bullet points for clarity.
   - **Paragraph 3 (Conclusion)**: Request a prompt reply, perhaps asking for a brochure or prospectus to be sent.
7. **Closing**: *Thanking you,*
8. **Subscription**: *Yours faithfully,* or *Yours sincerely,*
9. **Signature & Name**.

## ⚖️ CBSE Marking Scheme (Typically 5-8 Marks)
- **Format**: 1-2 Marks
- **Content**: 3-4 Marks (Asking relevant questions clearly)
- **Expression**: 2 Marks (Polite tone, grammatical accuracy)

## 💡 Language Tips & Useful Phrases
- **Opening phrases**:
  - *"With reference to your advertisement in 'The Daily Times' dated [Date]..."*
  - *"I am writing to inquire about the courses offered by your esteemed institute."*
- **Asking questions**:
  - *"I would be grateful if you could provide the following details:"*
  - *"Could you please let me know the fee structure for..."*
- **Closing phrases**:
  - *"I request you to send a detailed prospectus to the address mentioned above."*
  - *"I look forward to hearing from you soon."*

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Asking vague questions. Be very specific about what you want to know (timings, fees, eligibility, etc.).
- Using informal language or demanding the information aggressively.

## 🌟 Model Example

**Q: You are Ankit/Ankita living at 14, Rose Villa, Dehradun. You have seen an advertisement in the newspaper regarding a 'Personality Development Course' offered by Elite Institute. Write a letter of inquiry to the Director asking for necessary details.**

14, Rose Villa  
Dehradun  

15 May 2026  

The Director  
Elite Institute  
Mall Road, Dehradun  

**Subject**: Inquiry regarding the Personality Development Course.

Respected Sir/Madam,  

With reference to your advertisement published in 'The Times of India' dated 12th May 2026, I am writing to inquire about the 'Personality Development Course' offered by your esteemed institute during the upcoming summer vacations. 

I am currently a Class VIII student and am very keen on enrolling in this program to improve my communication and public speaking skills. Before joining, I would be grateful if you could provide me with the following details:

1. What is the total duration of the course?
2. What are the batch timings and class days?
3. What is the total fee structure and the mode of payment?
4. Is there a transport facility available for students coming from Rose Villa?

I kindly request you to send a detailed prospectus to my address or email the details at ankit123@email.com at the earliest so I can make my decision.

Thanking you,  

Yours sincerely,  
Ankit Sharma
