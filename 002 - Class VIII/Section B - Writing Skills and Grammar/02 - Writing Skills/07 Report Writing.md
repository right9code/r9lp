# Report Writing

## 📌 What is a Report?
A **report** is a factual description of an event or an incident. It is written to inform people about what happened, usually for a newspaper, a school magazine, or an official record. It relies strictly on facts and avoids personal opinions.

## 📝 Format / Structure (Word Limit: 120-150 words)

1. **Headline / Title**: Catchy, brief, and to the point.
2. **Byline**: Name of the reporter and their designation (e.g., *By Rahul, Staff Reporter*).
3. **Place and Date**: Written at the beginning of the first paragraph (e.g., *New Delhi, 14 May:*).
4. **Paragraph 1 (Introduction)**: The 5 Ws - *What* happened? *When* did it happen? *Where* did it happen? *Who* was involved?
5. **Paragraph 2 (Main Body/Details)**: 
   - Sequence of events.
   - Causes and consequences (if it's an accident/incident).
   - Details of the activities (if it's a school event).
   - Quotes from eyewitnesses or chief guests.
6. **Paragraph 3 (Conclusion)**:
   - Relief measures or police action (for accidents).
   - Prize distribution or vote of thanks (for events).

## ⚖️ CBSE Marking Scheme (Typically 5-8 Marks)
- **Format**: 1 Mark (Heading, Byline, Place & Date)
- **Content**: 3-4 Marks (Factual details, logical sequence)
- **Expression**: 2-3 Marks (Past tense, passive voice, formal tone)

## 💡 Language Tips & Key Rules
- **Tense**: Always use the **Past Tense** since the event has already occurred.
- **Voice**: Use **Passive Voice** to maintain a formal and objective tone (e.g., *The prizes were distributed* instead of *The Principal distributed the prizes*).
- **Tone**: Objective. NEVER use "I" or "Me" (except when quoting someone).
- **Be Factual**: Do not include your own feelings or opinions. Stick to the facts.

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Writing it like an essay or a personal story.
- Forgetting the Byline or the Place & Date at the start.
- Using the present or future tense.

## 🌟 Model Example

**Q: Your school recently organized a 'Tree Plantation Drive' on the occasion of World Environment Day. Write a report in 120-150 words for your school magazine. You are Ananya/Aryan, Head Girl/Boy.**

**Tree Plantation Drive Organised**  
*By Ananya, Head Girl*

**New Delhi, 6 June:** To mark the occasion of World Environment Day, a massive Tree Plantation Drive was organized by the Eco Club of XYZ Public School on 5th June 2026 within the school premises. The aim of the event was to raise awareness about environmental conservation and the importance of afforestation.

The event commenced at 9:00 AM with a special assembly. The Chief Guest, the local MLA Mr. R.K. Sharma, inaugurated the drive by planting a banyan sapling near the school gate. Following this, students from Classes VI to X, along with the teachers, planted over 500 saplings of neem, peepal, and ashoka trees along the boundary walls and in the neighborhood park. The students carried placards bearing slogans about saving the earth.

The Chief Guest addressed the gathering, emphasizing the urgent need to protect our planet for future generations. The event concluded with a vote of thanks proposed by the Principal and the distribution of refreshments to all participants. It was a highly successful and inspiring event.

## ✍️ Practice Prompts

1. **Newspaper Report:** You are a staff reporter for 'The Times of India'. Write a report on a major fire that broke out in a garment factory in your city, causing severe property damage but no casualties.
2. **Magazine Report:** Your school celebrated its Annual Sports Day last week. Write a report on the event for your school magazine, detailing the chief guest, the events, and the winning house.
