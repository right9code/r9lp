# Informal Letter

## 📌 What is an Informal Letter?
An **informal letter** is a personal letter written to friends, family members, or relatives. It is used to share personal news, express feelings, give advice, or invite someone to an event. The tone is relaxed, friendly, and conversational.

## 📝 Format / Structure (Word Limit: 120-150 words)

The format follows a left-aligned layout:

1. **Sender’s Address**: Written at the top left corner.
2. **Date**: Written below the address (e.g., 24 May 2026).
3. **Salutation (Greeting)**: E.g., *Dear Mom, Dear Rahul,*
4. **Body of the Letter**:
   - **Paragraph 1 (Introduction)**: Inquire about their health and well-being. State the reason for writing.
   - **Paragraph 2 (Main Content)**: Expand on the reason (e.g., describing a trip, sharing news, or inviting them). Give details.
   - **Paragraph 3 (Conclusion)**: Concluding remarks, sending love and regards to family members.
5. **Subscription (Closing)**: E.g., *Yours lovingly, Your affectionate friend,*
6. **Sender’s Name/Signature**: First name only.

## ⚖️ CBSE Marking Scheme (Typically 5-8 Marks)
- **Format**: 1-2 Marks (Address, Date, Salutation, Closing)
- **Content**: 3-4 Marks (Relevance, ideas, elaboration)
- **Expression**: 2 Marks (Grammar, vocabulary, friendly tone)

## 💡 Language Tips & Useful Phrases
- **Opening**: 
  - *"It was wonderful to receive your letter yesterday."*
  - *"I hope you are sailing in the same boat of good health."*
- **Closing**: 
  - *"Pay my warm regards to uncle and aunt, and love to little Neha."*
  - *"Eagerly waiting for your reply."*
- **Tone**: Use contractions (*I'm, don't, can't*) and exclamations to make it sound natural.

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Using formal language**: Do not write "Respected Friend" or end with "Yours faithfully".
- **Punctuation errors in format**: Do not use commas at the end of the address lines in modern format.
- **Forgetting the date**: The date is a crucial part of the format.

## 🌟 Model Example

**Q: You are Aman/Anjali living at 14, Mall Road, Shimla. Write a letter to your friend in Delhi describing the heavy snowfall in your city and your experiences during the winter.**

14, Mall Road  
Shimla  

15 January 2026  

Dear Rohit,  

I hope this letter finds you in the best of health and spirits. I received your letter last week but couldn't reply earlier as we had a major power cut here due to the extreme weather.

I am writing to tell you about the magical experience of winter here in Shimla. For the past three days, we have been experiencing heavy snowfall. The entire city is covered in a thick blanket of pristine white snow. The pine trees look like they are out of a fairy tale! Since the schools are closed, my friends and I spend our mornings having snowball fights and building snowmen in the park. In the evenings, we sit by the fireplace, drinking hot cocoa and roasting peanuts. 

It is incredibly beautiful, though quite freezing. You must plan a trip here next winter; I guarantee you will love it. Give my warm regards to your parents and love to your little sister.

Write back soon.

Yours lovingly,  
Aman

2. You recently moved to a new city and joined a new school. Write a letter to your old best friend describing your new school, classmates, and how much you miss them.
