# Formal Letter (Complaint)

## 📌 What is a Complaint Letter?
A **formal letter of complaint** is written to an authority, a business, or an official to report a grievance, a defective product, poor service, or a civic issue in a locality. The tone must be firm, polite, and strictly formal.

## 📝 Format / Structure (Word Limit: 100-120 words)

The standard left-aligned format is used:

1. **Sender’s Address**: Top left corner.
2. **Date**: E.g., *24 May 2026*.
3. **Receiver’s Designation and Address**: E.g., *The Manager, LG Electronics...*
4. **Subject**: A brief line stating the issue (e.g., *Subject: Complaint regarding defective washing machine.*)
5. **Salutation**: *Respected Sir/Madam* or *Dear Sir/Madam*.
6. **Body of the Letter**:
   - **Paragraph 1 (Introduction & Purchase details)**: Identify yourself. State clearly what you are complaining about. If it's a product, mention the date of purchase, bill number, and model.
   - **Paragraph 2 (The Problem)**: Explain the defect or the issue in detail. How is it causing inconvenience?
   - **Paragraph 3 (Resolution/Action required)**: State what action you expect (e.g., replacement, repair, swift action).
7. **Closing**: *Thanking you,*
8. **Subscription**: *Yours faithfully,* or *Yours sincerely,*
9. **Signature & Name**.

## ⚖️ CBSE Marking Scheme (Typically 5-8 Marks)
- **Format**: 1-2 Marks
- **Content**: 3-4 Marks (Clear details of the problem and desired action)
- **Expression**: 2 Marks (Polite but firm tone, correct grammar)

## 💡 Language Tips & Useful Phrases
- **Opening phrases**:
  - *"I am writing to express my dissatisfaction with..."*
  - *"I regret to bring to your notice that..."*
- **Detailing the issue**:
  - *"Despite careful usage according to the manual, the device stopped working..."*
  - *"This has caused me immense inconvenience."*
- **Demanding action**:
  - *"I request you to replace the defective item at the earliest..."*
  - *"I expect a prompt resolution to this issue."*

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Using abusive or overly emotional language**: Even if you are angry, the tone must remain polite and professional.
- **Forgetting receipt details**: If complaining about a product, you *must* invent and mention a bill number and date of purchase.

## 🌟 Model Example

**Q: You are Ramesh/Reena living at 45, MG Road, Pune. You recently purchased a mobile phone from 'Digital World' but it stopped working after a week. Write a letter of complaint to the Store Manager asking for a replacement.**

45, MG Road  
Pune  

15 October 2026  

The Store Manager  
Digital World  
MG Road, Pune  

**Subject**: Complaint regarding a defective mobile phone.

Respected Sir,  

I am writing this letter to express my deep dissatisfaction with a mobile phone I purchased from your store on 8th October 2026. The model is Samsung Galaxy A54, and the bill number is DW-7489. 

For the first few days, the phone worked perfectly. However, for the last two days, the battery has been draining within an hour of a full charge, and the screen frequently freezes, rendering the device completely unusable. This has caused me a great deal of inconvenience in my daily work.

Since the phone is still well within its one-year warranty period, I request you to kindly replace this defective handset with a new, fully functional one at the earliest. A copy of the receipt and the warranty card is enclosed with this letter.

I look forward to a prompt and positive response.

Thanking you,  

Yours sincerely,  
Ramesh Singh

2. Write a letter to the Municipal Commissioner of your city complaining about the overflowing garbage bins and unhygienic conditions in your locality.
