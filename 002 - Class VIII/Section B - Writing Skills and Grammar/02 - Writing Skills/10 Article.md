# Article Writing

## 📌 What is an Article?
An **article** is a piece of writing intended for publication in a newspaper, magazine, or journal. It is written to inform, persuade, or entertain a wide audience on a specific topic of interest, social issue, or current event. It presents facts, causes, effects, and the writer's opinions or suggestions.

## 📝 Format / Structure (Word Limit: 120-150 words)

1. **Title/Heading**: Catchy, relevant, and centered. Every major word should be capitalized.
2. **Byline**: The name of the writer, placed just below the title on the right side or center (e.g., *By Rahul*).
3. **Paragraph 1 (Introduction)**: 
   - A strong opening sentence to catch the reader's attention (a question, a quote, or a startling fact).
   - Clearly state the topic and its current relevance.
4. **Paragraph 2 (Body - Analysis)**: 
   - Explain the causes of the issue.
   - Describe the effects or consequences.
   - Provide examples or facts to support your points.
5. **Paragraph 3 (Conclusion)**:
   - Offer suggestions, solutions, or a call to action.
   - End with a strong concluding thought or a hope for the future.

## ⚖️ CBSE Marking Scheme (Typically 5-8 Marks)
- **Format**: 1 Mark (Heading and Byline)
- **Content**: 3-4 Marks (Relevance, logic, facts, examples)
- **Expression**: 2-3 Marks (Vocabulary, grammar, coherence, mature tone)

## 💡 Language Tips & Key Rules
- **Tone**: Formal, informative, and persuasive. 
- **Vocabulary**: Use mature vocabulary and transition words (e.g., *Furthermore, consequently, nevertheless, to mitigate this issue*).
- **Rhetorical Questions**: Use them to engage the reader (e.g., *Are we truly prepared to face the consequences of global warming?*).
- **Do NOT**: Do not use slang, overly casual language, or address a specific person (like you do in a letter).

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Forgetting the Byline (the writer's name).
- Writing the entire article in a single massive paragraph. Always divide into Intro, Body, and Conclusion.
- Going off-topic or presenting illogical arguments.

## 🌟 Model Example

**Q: You are Kavita/Karan. Write an article in 120-150 words for your school magazine on the topic "The Impact of Junk Food on Health".**

**The Impact of Junk Food on Health**  
*By Kavita*

In today’s fast-paced world, fast food has become an unavoidable part of our daily lives. With colorful advertisements and mouth-watering tastes, junk food easily attracts children and teenagers. However, are we paying a heavy price for this temporary satisfaction?

Junk food, though delicious, is severely devoid of essential nutrients. It is loaded with artificial preservatives, excessive salt, refined sugar, and unhealthy trans fats. Regular consumption of these foods leads to severe health issues such as childhood obesity, early-onset diabetes, and high blood pressure. Furthermore, relying heavily on fast food causes lethargy and affects a student's concentration and academic performance. The lack of fiber in these diets also leads to severe digestive problems.

It is high time we realize the catastrophic effects of our dietary choices. Parents and schools must actively promote the consumption of balanced, home-cooked meals rich in green vegetables and fresh fruits. Awareness campaigns about nutrition should be organized regularly. A healthy mind resides in a healthy body; therefore, choosing health over taste is the need of the hour.
