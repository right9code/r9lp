# Slogan Writing

## 📌 What is a Slogan?
A **slogan** is a short, striking, and memorable phrase used in advertising, political campaigns, or awareness drives. Its primary purpose is to convey a strong message quickly and effectively, making people remember a core idea or take action.

## 📝 Format / Structure 

A slogan does not have a rigid paragraph structure like an essay, but it should follow these visual rules:

1. **Length**: Usually just 1 to 2 lines (maximum 10-15 words).
2. **Visual Presentation**: It is often written in **bold, capital letters** or enclosed in a creative box or cloud shape to make it visually appealing.
3. **Punctuation**: Often uses an exclamation mark (!) to show emphasis.

## ⚖️ CBSE Marking Scheme (Typically 2-3 Marks)
- **Creativity and Originality**: 1 Mark
- **Relevance to the Topic**: 1 Mark
- **Rhyme/Rhythm/Impact**: 1 Mark

## 💡 Language Tips & Key Rules
- **Keep it Catchy**: Use rhymes (e.g., *Save Earth, For what it's Worth*).
- **Use Alliteration**: Repeating the same starting letter sounds pleasing (e.g., *Reduce, Reuse, Recycle*).
- **Action Verbs**: Start with strong verbs (e.g., *Stop, Save, Protect, Join*).
- **Contrasts**: Use contrasting ideas to make a point (e.g., *Don't be Mean, Keep it Clean*).

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Writing a full paragraph instead of a short phrase.
- Using difficult, complex vocabulary. The best slogans are simple and easy to understand for everyone.
- Forgetting to align the slogan centrally on the page.

## 🌟 Model Examples

**Topic: Water Conservation**
> **SAVE WATER, SAVE LIFE!**
> *(Every Drop Counts, Don't Let It Bounce!)*

**Topic: Anti-Bullying**
> **BE A BUDDY, NOT A BULLY!**
> *(Lift Each Other Up, Don't Tear Each Other Down!)*

**Topic: Deforestation**
> **PLANT A TREE, MAKE THE EARTH DEBT-FREE!**
> *(Cut a Tree, Cut Your Life!)*
