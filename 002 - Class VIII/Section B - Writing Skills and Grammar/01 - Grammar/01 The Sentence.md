### **C. Rearranging Words and Phrases**

1. **young / worried / the / man / how / looks (exclamatory)**
   * **How worried the young man looks!**

2. **how many / are there / do you know / in our lives / sources of stress (interrogative)**
   * **Do you know how many sources of stress there are in our lives?**

3. **are four / of stress / sources / our lives / there / in / common (assertive)**
   * **There are four common sources of stress in our lives.**

4. **pressure / external / the / or internal / can be / first one (assertive)**
   * **The first one can be external or internal pressure.**

5. **cause / stress / can / goals and expectations / internal / unreal (interrogative)**
   * **Can unreal goals and expectations cause internal stress?**

6. **due to / did you know / factors / takes place / that / outside / external stress (interrogative)**
   * **Did you know that external stress takes place due to factors outside?**

7. **of / is / another / anxiety / source / stress (assertive)**
   * **Anxiety is another source of stress.**

8. **panic / goes wrong / when / do not / any little thing (imperative)**
   * **Do not panic when any little thing goes wrong.**

### **D. Transformation of Sentences**

1. **I remained quiet. (to negative)**
   * I did not speak.

2. **What a curt reply! (to assertive)**
   * It was a very curt reply.

3. **I will not reach early. (to affirmative)**
   * I shall be late.

4. **No man is immortal. (to interrogative)**
   * Is any man immortal?

5. **It is a very cold night. (to exclamatory)**
   * What a cold night it is!

6. **Who does not know this? (to assertive)**
   * Everyone knows this.

7. **It is a good idea. (to interrogative)**
   * Isn't it a good idea?

8. **He rejected all our proposals. (to negative)**
   * He did not accept any of our proposals.

9. **To think we should meet again! (to assertive)**
   * It is strange to think that we should meet again.

10. **You should not break rules. (to imperative)**
    * Do not break rules.

11. **I wish I had come sooner. (to exclamatory)**
    * If only I had come sooner!

12. **Why worry about such small issues? (to assertive)**
    * It is useless to worry about such small issues.

13. **That was not the correct thing to say. (to interrogative)**
    * Was that the correct thing to say?

14. **As soon as he saw the policeman, he ran. (to negative)**
    * No sooner did he see the policeman than he ran.

15. **Was it for this that we worked so hard? (to assertive)**
    * We did not work so hard for this.

16. **You are instructed to sit down immediately. (to imperative)**
    * Sit down immediately.

17. **There was no one present who did not reply. (to affirmative)**
    * Everyone present replied.

18. **In the opposition too, the orthodox fought against the bill. (to negative)**
    * In the opposition too, the orthodox did not fail to fight against the bill.

19. **Was there ever anything so exquisitely beautiful before? (to assertive)**
    * There was never anything so exquisitely beautiful before.

20. **I shall never forget those glorious days in school. (to interrogative)**
    * Shall I ever forget those glorious days in school?

21. **You must solve all the sums in this exercise before turning on the television. (to negative)**
    * Do not turn on the television until you have solved all the sums in this exercise.

22. **You must not run or shout in the corridor. (to imperative)**
    * Do not run or shout in the corridor.

23. **This carpet is an exquisite work of art. (to exclamatory)**
    * What an exquisite work of art this carpet is!

24. **Who thought it would rain so heavily today? (to assertive)**
    * No one thought it would rain so heavily today.

25. **The minister whispered in the emperor's ear. (to interrogative)**
    * Did the minister whisper in the emperor's ear?
