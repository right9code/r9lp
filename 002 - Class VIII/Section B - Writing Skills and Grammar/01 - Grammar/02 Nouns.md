### **B. Correct the collective nouns**

1. **I saw a pride of calves when I went out this morning.**
   * I saw a **herd** of calves when I went out this morning.
2. **I need to buy a set of tables.**
   * I need to buy a **suite** of furniture (or a **set** of chairs/stools).
3. **Check out the chest of shelves.**
   * Check out the **flight** or **stack** of shelves.
4. **A squadron of cars stood ready.**
   * A **fleet** of cars stood ready.
5. **A mob of children gathered near the gate.**
   * A **troop** or **group** of children gathered near the gate.
6. **I bought a board of flowers for my mother.**
   * I bought a **bouquet** of flowers for my mother.
7. **A troupe of ducks stood ready for the show.**
   * A **brace** or **team** of ducks stood ready for the show.
8. **A swarm of lizards were visible at a distance.**
   * A **lounge** of lizards was visible at a distance.
9. **A regiment of men stormed into the premises.**
   * A **band** or **crowd** of men stormed into the premises.
10. **The bevy of fans were eager to meet the popular author.**
    * The **crowd** or **throng** of fans was eager to meet the popular author.
11. **A troop of buffaloes descended into the lake.**
    * A **herd** of buffaloes descended into the lake.
12. **A herd of owls hooted all night.**
    * A **parliament** of owls hooted all night.
13. **A rack of hounds attacked the stag.**
    * A **pack** of hounds attacked the stag.
14. **Indonesia is a heap of islands in southeast Asia.**
    * Indonesia is an **archipelago** or **chain** of islands in southeast Asia.
15. **A spring of thieves robbed the house.**
    * A **gang** or **pack** of thieves robbed the house.

There were two **possessions** (*Common*) of which James and Della both took a mighty **pride** (*Abstract*). One was Jim’s gold watch that had been his father’s and his grandfather’s. Della visited a hundred **stores** (*Common*) before she found the perfect **gift** (*Common*) for Jim. It was a platinum fob chain, simple and chaste in **design** (*Common*), properly proclaiming its **value** (*Abstract*) by **substance** (*Material*) alone and not by ornamentation. It was like him. Quietness and value—the **description** (*Common*) applied to both. With that **strap** (*Common*) on his watch Jim might be properly anxious about the **time** (*Abstract*) in any company. Grand as the gold **watch** (*Common*) was, he sometimes looked at it on the sly on account of the old leather that he used in place of a chain.
