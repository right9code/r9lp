### A. Underline the verbs in these sentences and pick out the objects, if any. State if the verbs are transitive or intransitive.

1. The teacher **gave** me a book.
   * **Type:** Transitive
   * **Objects:** me (Indirect), a book (Direct)

2. The children **sang** a lovely song.
   * **Type:** Transitive
   * **Object:** a lovely song

3. The birds **sang** sweetly.
   * **Type:** Intransitive

4. The men **declared** a truce.
   * **Type:** Transitive
   * **Object:** a truce

5. He **felled** the trees.
   * **Type:** Transitive
   * **Object:** the trees

6. The boy **narrated** a tale.
   * **Type:** Transitive
   * **Object:** a tale

7. He **fell** asleep.
   * **Type:** Intransitive

8. Can you **spare** a few minutes?
   * **Type:** Transitive
   * **Object:** a few minutes

9. He **spoke** rudely.
   * **Type:** Intransitive

10. The car **halted** suddenly.
    * **Type:** Intransitive

11. I **burnt** my hand.
    * **Type:** Transitive
    * **Object:** my hand

12. The fire **raged** through the forest.
    * **Type:** Intransitive

13. They **laughed** at the joke.
    * **Type:** Intransitive

14. The storm **destroyed** the house.
    * **Type:** Transitive
    * **Object:** the house

15. The farmer **sold** me some potatoes.
    * **Type:** Transitive
    * **Objects:** me (Indirect), some potatoes (Direct)

16. The boat **floated** down the river.
    * **Type:** Intransitive

17. I **moved** the bowl.
    * **Type:** Transitive
    * **Object:** the bowl

18. The glass **broke**.
    * **Type:** Intransitive

19. He **taught** me dancing.
    * **Type:** Transitive
    * **Objects:** me (Indirect), dancing (Direct)

20. The officer **arrested** the thief.
    * **Type:** Transitive
    * **Object:** the thief

21. She **sleeps** in the next bedroom.
    * **Type:** Intransitive

22. The dog **chased** the white mouse.
    * **Type:** Transitive
    * **Object:** the white mouse

23. The door **opened**.
    * **Type:** Intransitive

24. He **promised** me a book.
    * **Type:** Transitive
    * **Objects:** me (Indirect), a book (Direct)

### B. Underline the verbs in these sentences and pick out the subjective and objective complements, if any.

1. We **felt** cold.
   * **Subjective Complement:** cold

2. My dog **is** lazy.
   * **Subjective Complement:** lazy

3. We **became** tired.
   * **Subjective Complement:** tired

4. I **feel** miserable.
   * **Subjective Complement:** miserable

5. He **became** a writer.
   * **Subjective Complement:** a writer

6. They **are** Buddhists.
   * **Subjective Complement:** Buddhists

7. The building **is** to let.
   * **Subjective Complement:** to let

8. We **named** the child Sita.
   * **Objective Complement:** Sita

9. He **was elected** prefect.
   * **Subjective Complement:** prefect (Note: Passive voice)

10. The night **grew** cold.
    * **Subjective Complement:** cold

11. The girl **seems** gentle.
    * **Subjective Complement:** gentle

12. The pill **tastes** bitter.
    * **Subjective Complement:** bitter

13. The machine **is** of no use.
    * **Subjective Complement:** of no use

14. We **found** the child asleep.
    * **Objective Complement:** asleep

15. Exercise **has made** him sturdy.
    * **Objective Complement:** sturdy

16. I **found** the child weeping.
    * **Objective Complement:** weeping

17. The committee **found** him guilty.
    * **Objective Complement:** guilty

18. His account **filled** us with amazement.
    * **Objective Complement:** with amazement
