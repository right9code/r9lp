### **A. Fill in the blanks with suitable personal or possessive pronouns.**

1. **I am taller than ___________ is.**
   * I am taller than **he** is. (Subjective case required before the verb 'is')

2. **He is as lazy as that brother of ___________.**
   * He is as lazy as that brother of **yours** (or **his/hers/mine**).

3. **They are far better players than ___________.**
   * They are far better players than **we** (or **us**).

4. **My sister’s hair is longer than ___________.**
   * My sister’s hair is longer than **mine** (or **hers**).

5. **None as blind as ___________ that will not see.**
   * None as blind as **those** that will not see.

6. **Rohit has found a diary of ___________ that ___________ had thought was lost.**
   * Rohit has found a diary of **his** that **he** had thought was lost.

7. **I am sure that nobody will help you but ___________.**
   * I am sure that nobody will help you but **me**.

8. **She knows her responsibilities as well as ___________.**
   * She knows her responsibilities as well as **I** (or **mine**).

9. **My father is furious with ___________. I have lost a favourite book of ___________.**
   * My father is furious with **me**. I have lost a favourite book of **his**.

10. **‘Who did this?’ Mother asked. ‘It was ___________. But the idea was ___________,’ replied Akash.**
    * ‘It was **me**. But the idea was **hers** (or **theirs**),’ replied Akash.

11. **Reeta is very happy; the essay that won the competition is ___________. ___________ is a wonderful piece of writing.**
    * Reeta is very happy; the essay that won the competition is **hers**. **It** is a wonderful piece of writing.

12. **Mr Rathore is a nominee for the elections. Between you and ___________, ___________ is a truly deserving candidate.**
    * Between you and **me**, **he** is a truly deserving candidate.

### **B. Fill in the blanks with suitable demonstrative or interrogative pronouns.**

At dawn, we started to climb the mountain road. ‘**This** is an ancient road. It leads to a monastery and a temple. **Those** were built about two hundred years ago,’ said my guide Kabu, pointing to some low huts by the roadside.

On either side of the narrow track grew many unfamiliar flowers. ‘**What** are these flowers called?’ I asked.

‘**These** are orchids and **those** in the distance are rhododendrons,’ said a local youth who had joined us.

‘**This** is proving to be an even better trek than I had expected,’ I said to my friend.

‘Yes, indeed,’ he replied. ‘**Who** is that fellow racing up at a great pace while we’re panting behind him?’

I looked at the man in front wondering at his incredible speed. Suddenly, there was a sudden shout.

‘Look at **that**,’ said my guide pointing at a distant peak. ‘**There** lies Mount Everest. In our country, we also call it Sagarmatha. **Which** of you would like to try to climb that peak?’

We looked at each other and shook our heads. **That** seemed like an impossible dream.

### **C. Correct the errors in the use of pronouns and identify their kind.**

1. **I did not buy neither of the two pens.**
   * I did not buy **either** of the two pens. (**Distributive**)
2. **I filled up the forms herself.**
   * I filled up the forms **myself**. (**Emphasising**)
3. **One must be careful about what he says.**
   * One must be careful about what **one** says. (**Indefinite**)
4. **'You will only do well if you help one,' the coach told the team.**
   * 'You will only do well if you help **each other/one another**,' the coach told the team. (**Reciprocal**)
5. **Jaya himself invited her friends for the party.**
   * Jaya **herself** invited her friends for the party. (**Emphasising**)
6. **Some of the boys was given a balloon.**
   * **Each** of the boys was given a balloon. (**Distributive**)
7. **Raj didn't tell someone that he needed help.**
   * Raj didn't tell **anyone** that he needed help. (**Indefinite**)
8. **Every of the students in the class passed the exam.**
   * **Each** of the students in the class passed the exam. (**Distributive**)
9. **Any of the company's business is done in India.**
   * **All** (or **Some**) of the company's business is done in India. (**Indefinite**)
10. **I had to hold on to the chair to steady itself.**
    * I had to hold on to the chair to steady **myself**. (**Reflexive**)
11. **None doesn't know what to say in such a situation.**
    * **No one/Nobody** knows what to say in such a situation. (**Indefinite**)
12. **Although he is an Indian, either of his companies is set in India.**
    * Although he is an Indian, **neither** of his companies is set in India. (Contextually implies negative; **Distributive**)
13. **The criminals gave himself up to the police.**
    * The criminals gave **themselves** up to the police. (**Reflexive**)
14. **Sam and his brother have stopped talking to one other after a silly fight.**
    * Sam and his brother have stopped talking to **each other** after a silly fight. (**Reciprocal**)

### **D. Correct the errors in the use of relative pronouns.**

1. **She is the girl that was selected to play the protagonist's role.**
   * She is the girl **who** was selected...
2. **Buy strawberry ice cream. It is the only flavour what he likes.**
   * It is the only flavour **that** he likes.
3. **That is the boy to who the judges gave the prize.**
   * That is the boy to **whom** the judges gave the prize.
4. **This is the puppy who we rescued from the street.**
   * This is the puppy **that/which** we rescued...
5. **The car which headlights were on has broken down.**
   * The car **whose** headlights were on...
6. **These are the mangoes that we bought from the market.**
   * (Correct as is, or use **which**)
7. **This is the road what leads to the hospital.**
   * This is the road **that/which** leads...
8. **The forms which I had to fill up yesterday were very complicated.**
   * (Correct as is, or use **that**)
9. **This is the boy which father is a mountaineer.**
   * This is the boy **whose** father is a mountaineer.
10. **She collected all the items which she needed for the experiment.**
    * She collected all the items **that** she needed...

### **E. Join these sets of sentences with a relative pronoun.**

1. **Mr Roy is an architect. His designs have won him many awards.**
   * Mr Roy is an architect **whose** designs have won him many awards.
2. **The medicines have been withdrawn by the medical council. The medicines were used to treat bacterial infection.**
   * The medicines **which/that** were used to treat bacterial infection have been withdrawn by the medical council.
3. **I stopped the ambulance. It was speeding down the road.**
   * I stopped the ambulance **which/that** was speeding down the road.
4. **The diary was preserved by the family. It belonged to Grandfather.**
   * The diary **which/that** belonged to Grandfather was preserved by the family.
5. **The hurricane is heading towards the next town. The hurricane has caused extensive damage in our town.**
   * The hurricane, **which** has caused extensive damage in our town, is heading towards the next town.
6. **The writer will visit us next week. I admire the writer.**
   * The writer **whom** I admire will visit us next week.
7. **We received a good offer for the house. We accepted it.**
   * We accepted the good offer **which/that** we received for the house.
8. **We went to a hotel. It was recommended by you.**
   * We went to a hotel **which/that** was recommended by you.
9. **This lunch box is broken. It belongs to my sister.**
   * This lunch box, **which** belongs to my sister, is broken.
10. **He took me to see the dilapidated building. He is repairing it.**
    * He took me to see the dilapidated building **which/that** he is repairing.
