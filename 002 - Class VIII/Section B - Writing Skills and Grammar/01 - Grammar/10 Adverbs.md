### A. Choose adverbs from the box to complete the passage given below. Identify the kind of adverb you have used in each case.

**Word Box:** usually, around, never, consequently, enormously, quite, early, too, always, anywhere, so, yesterday, invariably, violently, heavily, hardly, why, every day.

**Answer:**
My family **invariably** (Frequency) needs an excuse to travel. We're **usually** (Frequency) ready to travel **anywhere** (Place). I get **so** (Degree) excited before a trip that I can **hardly** (Degree) sleep in anticipation of the event. If we travel by car, we **always** (Frequency) try to set off **early** (Time) so that we can reach our destination quickly.

**Yesterday** (Time) I was looking at the pictures of our trip to Goa. It rained **heavily** (Manner) **every day** (Frequency) of that trip. Although we couldn't go swimming, we enjoyed ourselves **enormously** (Degree). **Why** (Interrogative) couldn't we go swimming? The waves were **too** (Degree) high. I remember the sea pounding **violently** (Manner) against the beach. **Consequently** (Reason), we just relaxed **around** (Place) in the rain.

### B. Rewrite these sentences using adverbs in place of the highlighted adjectives. The meaning of the sentences should not change.

1.  **Q:** I am a careful driver.
    **A:** I drive **carefully**.
2.  **Q:** Suman is not a fast runner.
    **A:** Suman does not run **fast**.
3.  **Q:** Gargi is a brilliant dancer.
    **A:** Gargi dances **brilliantly**.
4.  **Q:** He is quick to take offence.
    **A:** He takes offence **quickly**.
5.  **Q:** Sita's behaviour is more courteous than Mira's.
    **A:** Sita behaves **more courteously** than Mira.
6.  **Q:** Of the two, Harish is the more articulate speaker.
    **A:** Of the two, Harish speaks **more articulately**.
7.  **Q:** She has a very good pronunciation of the English vowels.
    **A:** She pronounces the English vowels **very well**.
8.  **Q:** Mr Sen approaches the subject in a far less scientific manner.
    **A:** Mr Sen approaches the subject **far less scientifically**.
9.  **Q:** There has been a rapid increase in the number of train accidents.
    **A:** The number of train accidents has increased **rapidly**.
10. **Q:** Last night's performance left a considerable impression upon me.
    **A:** Last night's performance impressed me **considerably**.

### F. Rewrite the paragraphs correcting the use of adverbs wherever required.

1.  **Original:** He was much sorry for his behaviour. He seldom or ever told a lie. He was very much surprised by his own conduct. He had behaved fairly badly. Just not like him.
    **Corrected:** He was **very** sorry for his behaviour. He **seldom** (or **seldom if ever**) told a lie. He was **very** surprised by his own conduct. He had behaved **rather** badly. It was just not like him.

2.  **Original:** I am too tired, but not very tired to walk to the market. Do not worry, I shall walk so slowly. The weather is surely pleasant. It is not too humid today. I surely will manage. Remember that I go to the market alone always.
    **Corrected:** I am **very** tired, but not **too** tired to walk to the market. Do not worry, I shall walk **very** slowly. The weather is **fairly** pleasant. It is not too humid today. I **surely** will manage. Remember that I **always** go to the market alone.

3.  **Original:** Don't you think she talks very much? She is a rather clever girl, but fairly talkative.
    **Corrected:** Don't you think she talks **too much**? She is a **fairly** clever girl, but **rather** talkative.
