
Poetic Devices

* **Personification:** "Mighty Himavant," "Generous Ganga," "She’s peerless." 
  * Reasoning: Assigns human virtues and a "Mother" identity to the land to evoke emotional devotion.

* **Imagery:** "The sunny golden land." 
  * Reasoning: Uses vivid color to symbolize prosperity, warmth, and divine light.

* **Alliteration:** "**g**enerous **G**anga," "**h**as been **h**eard **h**ere." 
  * Reasoning: Repeats initial sounds to create a melodic, lyrical rhythm.

* **Anaphora:** "The... **is ours**," "This... **is ours**." 
  * Reasoning: Repeats specific phrases at the start of lines to reinforce collective national pride.

* **Hyperbole:** "There’s no equal anywhere on earth." 
  * Reasoning: Uses intentional exaggeration to emphasize the incomparable greatness of the nation.

* **Rhetorical Question:** "Which other river can match her grace?" 
  * Reasoning: Asks a question with an implied "none" to emphasize uniqueness.

* **Metaphor:** "Brahma-knowledge has **taken root**." 
  * Reasoning: Compares abstract wisdom to a growing plant, suggesting it is organic and deep-seated.

* **Allusion:** "Upanishads," "Buddha," "Dhamma." 
  * Reasoning: References specific historical and religious milestones to ground the poem in heritage.

* **Refrain:** "She’s peerless, let’s praise her!" 
  * Reasoning: A repeated chorus that anchors the central message and builds emotional momentum.

* **Epithet:** "**Gallant** warriors," "**Hoary** antiquity." 
  * Reasoning: Uses descriptive adjectives to highlight the permanent, essential qualities of the subjects.

* **Catalogue:** Listing mountains, rivers, scriptures, warriors, and sages. 
  * Reasoning: Piles up diverse examples of greatness to show the nation’s multifaceted richness.
