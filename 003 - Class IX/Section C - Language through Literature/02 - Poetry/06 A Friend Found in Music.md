# A Friend Found in Music - Class 9 Comprehensive Notes

**Poet:** Bryanna T. Perkins
**(From the textbook *Kaveri*)**

---

## Complete Poem Text

Music is the ocean that pulls me to shore,
Music is the rhythm that moves me to the core,
Music is my therapy when I'm feeling blue,
Music is the strength that helps me pull through.

When no one seems to listen,
When no one seems to care,
Music is the needed friend
That is always, always there.

It lifts me when I'm falling,
It holds me when I break,
It sings to me so softly
When no other sound I'd take.

Music is the ocean that pulls me to shore,
Music is the rhythm that moves me to the core,
A friend I've found in music,
Forever, and ever more.

**— Bryanna T. Perkins**

---

## Detailed Summary

"A Friend Found in Music" is a deeply personal and emotional poem that explores the profound connection between a human being and music. The poet portrays music not merely as a collection of sounds or a form of entertainment, but as a reliable, comforting, and transformative companion in life.

The poem begins by describing music as a powerful force, similar to an ocean, that draws the poet toward a place of peace and comfort. The poet emphasizes that music is a constant presence, acting as a loyal friend during both moments of joy and times of deep sorrow. Whenever the poet feels "blue" (sad or lonely), music serves as a form of therapy. It has the power to lift her spirits and give her the emotional strength needed to endure hardships and "pull through." 

The poem concludes by highlighting that music transcends language; it is a rhythm that touches the very "core" of the human heart. It acts as a dependable, much-needed friend who remains present even when human company is unavailable or insufficient.

---

## Central Themes

### 1. Music as Emotional Therapy
A central theme of the poem is the healing power of music. It highlights music's ability to act as a therapeutic agent during difficult times. When the poet is feeling low, music provides solace, comfort, and the strength to overcome emotional struggles.

### 2. Constant Companionship
Music is personified as a loyal, unwavering friend. Unlike human companions who might be absent or fail to understand, music is always there. It provides a constant source of support, acting as an anchor that keeps the poet grounded regardless of her emotional state.

### 3. Emotional Resonance
The poem explores how music synchronizes with human emotions. It transcends mere sound to resonate with the listener's internal state, touching the very core of their being and mirroring their innermost feelings.

## Detailed Poetic Devices Analysis

**Rhyme Scheme:** AABB in the opening and closing stanzas (*shore/core, blue/through*); ABCB in middle stanzas (*listen/care/friend/there, falling/break/softly/take*). The consistent rhyme creates a song-like, musical quality that mirrors the poem's subject.

**1. Personification**
This is the most prominent device. Music is given human qualities throughout:
*   *"Music is the needed friend / That is always, always there"* — music is a loyal companion.
*   *"It lifts me when I'm falling"* — music physically supports her.
*   *"It holds me when I break"* — music embraces and comforts her.
*   *"It sings to me so softly"* — music becomes a gentle voice.
*   **Effect:** Transforms music from an abstract concept into a tangible, living friend.

**2. Extended Metaphor**
The poem is built on the central metaphor of **music as a friend/companion:**
*   The title itself — *"A Friend Found in Music"* — establishes this.
*   Throughout the poem, music is described as doing things only a friend would: listening, caring, lifting, holding.

**3. Metaphor**
*   *"Music is the ocean that pulls me to shore"* — music is compared to a vast, powerful ocean. The "shore" symbolizes a safe, peaceful place. Music draws her toward safety.
*   *"Music is my therapy"* — directly equates music to a healing process.
*   *"Music is the rhythm that moves me to the core"* — the "core" represents her deepest emotions.

**4. Anaphora**
The repetition of *"Music is the…"* at the beginning of consecutive lines:
*   *"Music is the ocean…"*
*   *"Music is the rhythm…"*
*   *"Music is my therapy…"*
*   *"Music is the strength…"*
*   **Effect:** Creates a rhythmic, chant-like quality. Emphasizes music's multifaceted role in her life.

**5. Repetition / Refrain**
*   The opening couplet (*"Music is the ocean… Music is the rhythm…"*) is repeated in the final stanza, creating a **circular structure** that mirrors the endless, cyclical nature of music.
*   *"always, always there"* — the word repetition emphasizes permanence.

**6. Imagery**
*   *Tactile:* *"It lifts me when I'm falling, / It holds me when I break"* — the reader can feel the physical support.
*   *Auditory:* *"It sings to me so softly"* — evokes the gentle sound of music.
*   *Kinesthetic:* *"pulls me to shore," "moves me to the core"* — creates a sense of physical movement.

**7. Tone**
The tone is **intimate, grateful, and comforting.** The poem reads like a love letter or tribute to music, expressing deep personal gratitude for its constant emotional support.

## Textbook Questions and Answers

1. How is music able to move the poet ‘to the core’?
**Ans:** The poet means that music doesn't just touch her ears; it resonates with her deepest emotions and soul. It has a rhythmic power that can physically and mentally shift her from sadness to a place of inner strength.

2. Why does the poet compare music to a ‘needed friend’?
**Ans:** The poet compares music to a "needed friend" because it provides a level of constant, non-judgmental support that human companions often fail to offer. When "no one seems to listen" or "care," music remains a reliable presence that is "always, always there" to provide solace. Much like a true friend, music understands and mirrors the listener’s inner emotional state, whether they are feeling "blue" or need the "strength to pull through." It offers a form of silent therapy that can "lift" a person when they are falling and "hold" them when they feel broken. This personification highlights music's unique ability to act as an emotional anchor, providing a safe sanctuary of peace and understanding in a lonely world.

3. Explain the poet’s attitude towards music.
**Ans:** The poet’s attitude towards music is one of deep reverence, intense gratitude, and profound love. She does not view music merely as a source of entertainment or a background sound; instead, she describes it as a vital life-force, comparable to an "ocean" that possesses an irresistible "pull" toward safety and peace. Her attitude reflects a spiritual connection, as she sees music as her "therapy" and her primary source of internal "strength." This sense of awe and dependency suggests that the poet views music as an essential part of her identity, a "rhythm" that moves her to her very "core" and serves as her most trusted companion in navigating the emotional complexities of life.

4. Support the opinion that this poem has a universal appeal.
**Ans:** The poem has universal appeal because everyone has experienced sadness or loneliness. Music is a global language of emotion, and anyone who has ever found comfort in a song can relate to the poet's feelings.

5. Compare your experience of music with the feelings expressed in the poem.
**Ans:** (Personal Ans) Like the poet, I find that when I'm overwhelmed with schoolwork or feeling stressed, music acts as my "therapy." It helps me stay calm and gives me the "strength" to keep going.
