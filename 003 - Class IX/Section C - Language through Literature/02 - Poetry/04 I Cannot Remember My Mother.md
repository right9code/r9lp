# I Cannot Remember My Mother - Class 9 Comprehensive Notes

**Poet:** Rabindranath Tagore
**(From the textbook *Kaveri*)**

---

## Complete Poem Text
I cannot remember my mother,  
only sometime in the midst of my play  
a tune seems to hover over my playthings,  
the tune of some song that she used to hum  
while rocking my cradle.  

I cannot remember my mother,  
but when in the early autumn morning  
the smell of the shiuli flowers floats in the air,  
the scent of the morning service in the temple  
comes to me as the scent of my mother.  

I cannot remember my mother,  
only when from my bedroom window I send  
my eyes into the blue of the distant sky,  
I feel that the stillness of my mother's gaze  
on my face has spread all over the sky.  

---

## Detailed Summary

"I Cannot Remember My Mother" is an intensely emotional and nostalgic poem by Rabindranath Tagore, who lost his mother when he was very young. 

### Stanza 1: The Memory of Sound
The poet begins by explicitly stating that he cannot remember his mother's physical features. However, her memory is triggered by sound. While he is playing with his toys, he suddenly feels a familiar tune hovering over his playthings. He realizes that this is the same tune his mother used to hum while rocking him to sleep in his cradle when he was a baby. 

### Stanza 2: The Memory of Smell
In the second stanza, the poet again asserts that he cannot remember his mother. However, the memory is triggered by scent. During the early mornings of autumn, the sweet fragrance of the *shiuli* (night-flowering jasmine) flowers floats in the air. This fragrance gets mixed with the scent of incense burning during the morning prayer service in the temple. This specific combination of smells reminds him of his mother, suggesting that she used to gather these flowers for her daily morning prayers.

### Stanza 3: The Memory of Sight/Feeling
In the final stanza, the poet mentions for the third time that he cannot remember his mother. Here, the memory is triggered by sight and a profound feeling of being watched over. When he looks out of his bedroom window into the vast, clear, blue distant sky, he feels a deep sense of peace. He feels as if the still, loving, and protective gaze of his mother is spread all across the sky, looking down at him from heaven.

---

## Central Themes

### 1. The Enduring Power of Love and Memory
The primary theme is that a mother's love is eternal and transcends death. Even though the poet was too young to remember her face, the deep emotional bond they shared remains intact. Her presence is not physical but is felt through his senses—sound, smell, and feeling.

### 2. Nostalgia and Loss
The poem is heavily steeped in nostalgia. The repeated phrase "I cannot remember my mother" highlights a deep sense of loss and the poet's yearning for the mother he barely knew.

### 3. Nature as a Connecting Force
Tagore beautifully uses elements of nature to connect the physical world with the spiritual world of his mother. The hovering tune, the smell of the *shiuli* flowers, and the vast blue sky act as bridges connecting the poet to his mother's memory.

## Detailed Poetic Devices Analysis

**Rhyme Scheme:** Free Verse — no fixed rhyme scheme or metre. The lack of structure reflects the free-flowing, unforced nature of the poet's memories and the organic, spontaneous way they arise.

**1. Anaphora / Refrain**
The line *"I cannot remember my mother"* is repeated at the beginning of each stanza.
*   **Effect:** Emphasizes the poet's deep, persistent sense of loss. It also creates a paradox: while he claims he cannot remember her, each stanza proves that her memory is deeply embedded in his senses.

**2. Sensory Imagery (Multi-sensory)**
The poet uses a different sense in each stanza to evoke his mother's presence:
*   *Auditory (Sound):* *"a tune seems to hover over my playthings"* — the echo of her lullaby lingers.
*   *Olfactory (Smell):* *"the smell of the shiuli flowers floats in the air"* — fragrance triggers her memory.
*   *Visual (Sight):* *"I send my eyes into the blue of the distant sky"* — the vastness of the sky reflects her presence.

**3. Synesthesia**
The blending of sensory experiences across stanzas creates a holistic portrait of the mother:
*   Sound → Smell → Sight: The progression from one sense to another suggests that the mother's presence permeates *all* of the poet's sensory experiences, not just one.

**4. Metaphor**
*   *"the stillness of my mother's gaze on my face has spread all over the sky"* — the sky becomes a metaphor for the mother's protective, omnipresent love. Her gaze is so vast and all-encompassing that it covers the entire heavens.
*   *"the heart is a pilgrim"* (implied) — the poet's emotional journey through life in search of his mother's memory.

**5. Symbolism**
*   **Shiuli flowers:** Symbolize the morning prayers and spiritual devotion of the mother. In Indian culture, these flowers are gathered for worship at dawn, linking the mother to purity, faith, and the sacred.
*   **The blue sky:** Symbolizes eternity, peace, and the infinite nature of maternal love that transcends death.
*   **The cradle:** Symbolizes infancy, innocence, and the earliest bond between mother and child.

**6. Personification**
*   *"a tune seems to hover over my playthings"* — the tune is given the ability to hover, as if it has a will of its own, lingering like a spirit.
*   *"the smell of the shiuli flowers floats in the air"* — the scent is personified as something that floats gently, carrying the mother's essence.

**7. Tone**
The tone is **nostalgic, melancholic, yet deeply tender.** There is a quiet sadness in each repetition of "I cannot remember," but this is balanced by the warmth and love that radiates through each sensory memory. The poem ends on a note of peaceful acceptance rather than despair.

---

## Word Meanings & Vocabulary

*   **Hover:** To remain in one place in the air; to linger.
*   **Playthings:** Toys.
*   **Shiuli:** A type of small, fragrant white and orange flower (Night-flowering Jasmine) that blooms in autumn in India, heavily associated with the Durga Puja festival and morning prayers.
*   **Stillness:** Calmness; lack of movement or sound.
*   **Gaze:** A steady, intent look.

## Textbook Questions and Answers

1. What is the emotional impact of the refrain, 'I cannot remember my mother'?
**Ans:** The refrain "I cannot remember my mother" has a profound emotional impact, creating a rhythmic and melancholic pulse that underscores the poet's deep sense of loss. By repeating this line at the beginning of each stanza, Tagore highlights the paradox of his memory: while he cannot recall her physical face or form, her presence is so deeply embedded in his subconscious that it is triggered by everyday sensory experiences. The refrain emphasizes that maternal love is a spiritual force that transcends death and physical absence. It transforms the poem from a simple lament into a powerful exploration of how the soul retains the essence of a person even when the mind loses their image.

2. Interpret the connection between the poet's mother and the following:
(i) shiuli flowers: Their scent during autumn morning prayers reminds the poet of his mother's own devotion and her habit of gathering flowers for worship.
(ii) humming tune: The sound hovering over his toys triggers a subconscious memory of her lullabies from his infancy in the cradle.

3. What role does nature play in the poet's description of the memory of his mother?
**Ans:** Nature plays the role of a divine bridge that connects the poet’s earthly existence with the spiritual memory of his mother. Tagore does not find his mother in photographs or stories, but in the "smell of the shiuli flowers" during an autumn morning and the "stillness of the distant blue sky." By intertwining her memory with natural phenomena, the poet suggests that her love has become omnipresent and eternal, much like nature itself. The vastness of the sky and the fragrance of the morning service act as sensory vessels that carry her essence to him, proving that nature serves as a sanctuary where the bond between a mother and child remains forever intact.

4. What can be inferred about the poet's perception of the mother-child relationship?
**Ans:** The poet perceives the relationship as spiritual and eternal. It is a bond that transcends death and physical memory, existing in the senses and the soul forever.
