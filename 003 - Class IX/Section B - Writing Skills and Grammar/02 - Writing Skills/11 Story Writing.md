# Story Writing

## 📌 What is Story Writing?
**Story writing** is an exercise in imagination, creativity, and structured narration. At the Class IX level, a story must have a well-developed plot, clear characterization, an engaging setting, and a logical conclusion. You are usually provided with a visual cue, an opening line, or an outline to build upon.

## 📝 Format / Structure (Word Limit: 150-200 words)

1. **Title**: Must be catchy and related to the central theme of the story.
2. **Introduction (The Setup)**: 
   - Introduce the main characters.
   - Establish the setting (time, place, atmosphere).
   - If a starting prompt is given, you MUST begin exactly with those words.
3. **Body (The Conflict & Climax)**:
   - Introduce a problem, a mystery, or an unexpected twist.
   - Build tension. Show how the characters react to the conflict.
   - Use sensory details to make the scene vivid.
4. **Conclusion (The Resolution)**:
   - Resolve the conflict logically.
   - The ending should leave an impact. A twist ending is often appreciated, provided it makes sense.

## ⚖️ CBSE Marking Scheme (Typically 5-8 Marks)
- **Title**: 1 Mark
- **Content (Originality, plot development, characterization)**: 3-4 Marks
- **Expression (Grammar, vocabulary, sequencing, coherence)**: 2-3 Marks

## 💡 Language Tips & Key Rules
- **Tense**: Always use the **Past Tense** for narration. Present tense is only allowed inside quotation marks for direct dialogue.
- **Show, Don't Tell**: Don't say "Rahul was terrified." Say "Rahul's knees buckled and a cold sweat drenched his shirt."
- **Use Dialogue Sparingly**: A few lines of dialogue can bring characters to life, but do not turn the story into a script.

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Ignoring the Prompt**: Starting your own story without using the provided opening line.
- **The "Dream" Ending**: Concluding with "And then I woke up, it was all a dream" is considered a weak and clichéd ending.
- **Switching Tenses**: Accidentally jumping between past and present tense.

## 🌟 Model Example

**Q: Write a story in 150-200 words beginning with the line: "The old grandfather clock in the hallway struck midnight, and suddenly the lights went out..." Give it a suitable title.**

**The Midnight Visitor**

The old grandfather clock in the hallway struck midnight, and suddenly the lights went out. I was alone in the house, my parents having gone to attend a wedding in the next town. The sudden darkness was absolute, thick, and suffocating. Outside, the wind howled, rattling the windowpanes. 

I fumbled in the dark for my phone to use its flashlight. Just as my fingers brushed against the cold screen, I heard it—a slow, deliberate creak from the wooden stairs leading to my room. My heart hammered against my ribs. I held my breath, listening intently. Footstep. Pause. Footstep. Someone was coming up.

Terrified, I grabbed a heavy cricket bat from the corner and hid behind the bedroom door, raising the bat. The doorknob turned slowly. The door swung open, and a tall figure stepped into the room. 

With a yell, I swung the bat, only to hear a familiar, panicked shout. "Woah, hold on! It's me!" 

The flashlight clicked on, revealing my elder brother, Rahul, soaking wet and holding a spare key. His train had been cancelled due to the storm, so he had taken a cab back. I dropped the bat, collapsing onto the bed in relief, the terror instantly replaced by nervous laughter.

## ✍️ Practice Prompts

1. **Complete the story**: "It was a bright, sunny morning. Neha was walking to school when she noticed a shiny, metallic box hidden in the bushes..."
2. **Develop a story based on the outline**: Two friends traveling through a forest - encounter a bear - one climbs a tree - the other lies down dead - bear sniffs and leaves - moral.
