# Reflective Writing

## 📌 What is Reflective Writing?
**Reflective writing** is an analytical piece of writing where you describe an experience, an event, or a concept, and then deeply reflect on what it means, what you learned from it, and how it has changed your perspective or behavior. It requires introspection and critical thinking.

## 📝 Format / Structure (Word Limit: 120-150 words)

1. **Title**: Catchy and relevant to the reflection.
2. **Introduction (The Event/Concept)**: 
   - Describe the specific event, experience, or idea that triggered your reflection.
   - Keep the description brief; the focus should be on the *reflection*.
3. **Body (The Reflection)**:
   - How did you feel during the experience?
   - What went well? What went wrong?
   - **The "So What?":** Why was this experience important? What did it reveal about you or society?
4. **Conclusion (The Outcome)**:
   - What is the ultimate lesson learned?
   - How will you act differently in the future based on this realization?

## ⚖️ CBSE Marking Scheme (Typically 5 Marks)
- **Format (Title)**: 1 Mark
- **Content (Depth of thought, logical progression)**: 2 Marks
- **Expression (Vocabulary, coherence, mature tone)**: 2 Marks

## 💡 Language Tips & Key Rules
- **First-Person Perspective**: Use "I", "my", "me" as it is a personal reflection.
- **Evaluative Language**: Use phrases that show analysis, not just description (e.g., *Looking back, I realize that...*, *This experience taught me...*, *I initially felt... but then I understood...*).
- **Honesty**: A good reflection is honest. Do not be afraid to admit mistakes or failures, as long as you show growth from them.

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Over-describing**: Spending 80% of the essay describing what happened and only 20% on the actual reflection/learning. The ratio should be balanced.
- **Being superficial**: Writing clichés like "I learned to be good" instead of detailing a specific, mature realization.

## 🌟 Model Example

**Q: Write a reflective paragraph on a time you failed at a task or competition, and what you learned from the experience.**

**The Value of Defeat**

Last month, I participated in the Inter-School Science Fair with a project I had hurriedly put together in just two days. Unsurprisingly, I did not win any prize. My initial reaction was one of intense embarrassment and frustration. I blamed the judges, the unfair time constraints, and my lack of resources.

However, after the anger subsided, I was forced to look inward. Reflecting on the event, I realized that my failure was entirely my own doing. I had underestimated the competition and succumbed to procrastination. This experience was a harsh but necessary wake-up call. It taught me that there are no shortcuts to success and that true achievement requires meticulous planning, dedication, and consistent effort. Looking back, I am grateful for that defeat. It stripped away my overconfidence and taught me the value of discipline—a lesson that will undoubtedly guide my future endeavors much more effectively than an unearned victory ever could.

## ✍️ Practice Prompts

1. Write a piece of reflective writing on **how the COVID-19 pandemic and lockdowns changed your perspective on family and freedom**.
2. Reflect on a time **you prejudged someone based on their appearance or background**, and how getting to know them proved you wrong.
