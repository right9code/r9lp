# Descriptive Paragraph (Factual Description)

## 📌 What is Factual Description?
A **factual description** is a piece of writing that describes an event, a process, a place, or an object strictly based on facts. Unlike a creative descriptive essay, a factual description does not include personal feelings, exaggerated adjectives, or opinions. It is similar to a report but focuses entirely on describing "how things are" or "how things happened."

## 📝 Format / Structure (Word Limit: 100-120 words)

1. **Title**: A clear and precise heading.
2. **Introduction**: 
   - State what is being described.
   - Give a brief overview.
3. **Body Paragraph**:
   - Provide sequential or logical details.
   - If describing a process (e.g., how to open a bank account), list the steps chronologically.
   - If describing an object/place, list dimensions, materials, and facts.
4. **Conclusion**:
   - A concluding factual statement summarizing the utility or final state of the subject.

## ⚖️ CBSE Marking Scheme (Typically 5 Marks)
- **Format (Title)**: 1 Mark
- **Content (Accuracy of facts, logical sequence)**: 2 Marks
- **Expression (Formal vocabulary, grammar)**: 2 Marks

## 💡 Language Tips & Key Rules
- **Tone**: Strictly objective and formal.
- **Pronouns**: Never use first-person pronouns (I, me, my). Write in the third person.
- **Voice**: The **Passive Voice** is highly preferred (e.g., *The mixture is heated* instead of *You must heat the mixture*).
- **Transition Words**: Use sequencing words for processes (e.g., *Firstly, subsequently, thereafter, finally*).

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Including personal opinions (e.g., *I think the new library is very beautiful*). Stick to facts (*The new library covers an area of 500 sq ft and contains 10,000 books*).
- Using informal language or exclamation marks.

## 🌟 Model Example

**Q: Write a factual description of the process of making a cup of tea.**

**The Process of Making Tea**

Making a cup of traditional Indian tea is a simple process that requires water, tea leaves, sugar, milk, and spices. First, half a cup of water is poured into a saucepan and placed over a medium flame. Once the water begins to boil, a teaspoon of tea leaves is added to it. To enhance the flavor, crushed ginger and cardamom pods are subsequently added to the boiling water. The mixture is allowed to simmer for about two minutes until the water absorbs the dark color and aroma of the tea leaves. Thereafter, half a cup of milk and two teaspoons of sugar are added to the pan. The concoction is boiled for another three to four minutes until it attains a rich, golden-brown hue. Finally, the hot tea is strained through a sieve into a cup and served immediately.
