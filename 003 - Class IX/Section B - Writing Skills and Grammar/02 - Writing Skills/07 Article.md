# Article Writing

## 📌 What is an Article?
An **article** is a comprehensive piece of writing published in newspapers or school magazines. In Class IX, an article expects a mature analysis of a topic—usually a social issue, environmental concern, or a teenage-centric problem. It must present a balanced view, including causes, effects, and pragmatic solutions.

## 📝 Format / Structure (Word Limit: 120-150 words)

1. **Title/Heading**: Centered, catchy, and relevant. (Capitalize main words).
2. **Byline**: Just below the title on the right (e.g., *By Amit, Class IX-A*).
3. **Paragraph 1 (Introduction - The Hook)**: 
   - Start with a striking fact, a quote, or a rhetorical question.
   - Introduce the topic and its current relevance.
4. **Paragraph 2 (Body - Analysis)**: 
   - **Causes**: Why is this issue happening?
   - **Effects**: How does it impact society, students, or the environment?
   - Include statistics or real-life examples to strengthen your point.
5. **Paragraph 3 (Conclusion - The Solution)**:
   - Suggest practical, actionable solutions.
   - Conclude with a strong, thought-provoking sentence or a call to action.

## ⚖️ CBSE Marking Scheme (Typically 5-8 Marks)
- **Format (Title + Byline)**: 1 Mark
- **Content (Ideas, logic, relevance)**: 3-4 Marks
- **Expression (Grammatical accuracy, mature vocabulary, cohesion)**: 2-3 Marks

## 💡 Language Tips & Key Rules
- **Formal Tone**: Do not use slang, abbreviations, or conversational language.
- **Linkers / Connectors**: Use words like *Furthermore, consequently, paradoxically, inevitably, therefore* to connect your sentences smoothly.
- **Third Person Perspective**: Generally, use the third person ("It is evident that..."). You can use "we" in the conclusion when calling for collective action.

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Writing the article without paragraphs. It MUST be broken into Intro, Body, and Conclusion.
- Providing problems without suggesting any solutions at the end.
- Straying away from the given topic.

## 🌟 Model Example

**Q: You are Rohan/Riya. Write an article for your school magazine on the topic: "The Menace of Cyberbullying Among Teenagers."**

**The Menace of Cyberbullying Among Teenagers**  
*By Riya*

The digital age has brought the world to our fingertips, but it has also unleashed a silent, invisible monster: cyberbullying. With the exponential rise in the use of social media among teenagers, the internet has unfortunately become a breeding ground for harassment and intimidation. 

Unlike traditional bullying, cyberbullying does not stop at the school gates; it follows the victim home. Hiding behind the veil of anonymity, bullies use fake profiles to post derogatory comments, spread malicious rumors, or share embarrassing photographs. The consequences are devastating. Victims often suffer from severe anxiety, depression, and a steep decline in academic performance. In extreme cases, it leads to tragic outcomes like self-harm.

Eradicating this menace requires a collective effort. Parents and teachers must cultivate an environment where teenagers feel safe discussing their online experiences without fear of losing their internet privileges. Furthermore, schools should conduct regular awareness workshops on cyber ethics. As responsible netizens, teenagers must learn to block and report toxic behavior, ensuring the internet remains a space for learning, not torment.

## ✍️ Practice Prompts

1. Write an article on **"The Importance of Mental Health Awareness in Schools."** Highlight how academic pressure affects students and what can be done.
2. Write an article on **"Water Scarcity: The Looming Crisis."** Detail the causes and the urgent need for water conservation.
