# Speech Writing

## 📌 What is a Speech?
A **speech** is a formal address delivered to an audience (like in a school assembly). It is meant to express opinions, share information, or persuade the listeners regarding a specific topic. Because it is spoken aloud, it should be engaging, interactive, and clearly structured.

## 📝 Format / Structure (Word Limit: 120-150 words)

1. **Heading/Title**: Catchy and relevant. (Optional, but good practice).
2. **Introduction (The Greeting & Hook)**: 
   - Greet the audience formally.
   - Introduce yourself and the topic.
3. **Body (The Core Message)**:
   - Present facts, causes, and effects related to the topic.
   - Use rhetorical questions to keep the audience engaged.
   - Give examples.
4. **Conclusion (The Call to Action & Sign-off)**:
   - Summarize the main point.
   - End with a strong appeal or a hope for the future.
   - **MUST end with a formal "Thank you."**

## ⚖️ CBSE Marking Scheme (Typically 5-8 Marks)
- **Format**: 1 Mark (Greeting, topic introduction, "Thank you" at the end)
- **Content**: 3-4 Marks (Logical arguments, relevance)
- **Expression**: 2-3 Marks (Persuasive tone, vocabulary, grammatical accuracy)

## 💡 Language Tips & Key Rules
- **Formal Greetings to Memorize**: *"Respected Principal, honorable teachers, and my dear friends. A very good morning to all of you. I am [Name], and today I stand before you to express my views on..."*
- **Interactive Tone**: Use pronouns like "we", "us", and "you" to make the audience feel involved (e.g., *Don't you agree that we need to change our habits?*).
- **Transitions**: *Moving on, furthermore, imagine for a moment, let us consider.*

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Writing it exactly like an essay or an article. A speech MUST have a greeting at the beginning and a "Thank you" at the end.
- Using overly complex sentences that would be hard for a speaker to actually say out loud without losing breath.

## 🌟 Model Example

**Q: You are Rohan/Riya. Write a speech to be delivered in the morning assembly on the topic "The Importance of Discipline in a Student's Life".**

**The Importance of Discipline**

Respected Principal, honorable teachers, and my dear friends. A very warm good morning to you all. I, Rohan of Class IX, stand before you today to share my thoughts on a topic that is the foundation of all success: "The Importance of Discipline in a Student's Life."

When we hear the word 'discipline', we often think of strict rules and punishments. But what is true discipline? It is simply the bridge between our goals and our accomplishments. As students, we have multiple responsibilities: academics, sports, and personal hobbies. Without the discipline of a timetable, managing these becomes impossible. It is discipline that makes us wake up early when we want to sleep, and study when we want to play.

Look at any successful person—a scientist, an athlete, or an artist. The one common trait they all share is unwavering discipline. It builds character, teaches time management, and prepares us for the harsh realities of the adult world. 

Therefore, my friends, let us not view discipline as a burden, but as a stepping stone to achieving our dreams. Let us pledge to be more disciplined from today onwards. 

Thank you, and have a wonderful day ahead!
