# Diary Entry

## 📌 What is a Diary Entry?
A **diary entry** is a deeply personal piece of writing reflecting a person's inner thoughts, feelings, and reactions to a specific event. At the Class IX level, your diary entry should demonstrate emotional maturity, introspective vocabulary, and a clear narrative of the day's events.

## 📝 Format / Structure (Word Limit: 100-120 words)

The format is strictly left-aligned:

1. **Date**: E.g., *24 May 2026*
2. **Day**: E.g., *Sunday*
3. **Time**: E.g., *10:30 PM* (Usually written at night).
4. **Salutation**: *Dear Diary,*
5. **Body**:
   - Begin with an exclamation or a summary statement of the day's emotion.
   - Narrate the incident briefly.
   - Spend the majority of the word limit on **how the incident made you feel** and **what you learned**.
   - Conclude with a hope, a resolution, or a final thought before sleeping.
6. **Signature/Name**: First name only.

## ⚖️ CBSE Marking Scheme (Typically 5 Marks)
- **Format**: 1 Mark (Date, Day, Time, Salutation, Name)
- **Content**: 2 Marks (Relevance to prompt, creativity, emotional depth)
- **Expression**: 2 Marks (First-person perspective, grammar, vocabulary)

## 💡 Language Tips & Key Rules
- **Perspective**: Always use the **First Person** ("I", "my", "we").
- **Tense**: Use the **Simple Past Tense** to describe the event, and **Present/Future Tense** for your current reflections and future resolutions.
- **Show Emotion**: Use strong adjectives (e.g., *devastated, elated, overwhelmed, anxious, relieved*).

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Writing it like an objective news report. It *must* contain your personal feelings.
- Forgetting the Time.
- Wasting words on your morning routine (e.g., "I woke up, brushed my teeth...") unless it is directly relevant to the prompt.

## 🌟 Model Example

**Q: You are Aman/Amita. You recently visited a slum area as part of a school social service project. The harsh living conditions deeply moved you. Write a diary entry expressing your feelings and your resolution to help.**

12 October 2026  
Monday  
9:00 PM  

Dear Diary,  

Today was an eye-opening and deeply unsettling day for me. Our school organized a visit to the Dharavi slum area for a social service project. I am still struggling to process the stark contrast between my comfortable life and the harsh reality those children face daily.

Walking through the narrow, unpaved alleys, I saw families living in cramped, tin-roofed shanties with minimal access to clean water or sanitation. Yet, despite their extreme poverty, the children greeted us with the most radiant, hopeful smiles. Seeing their joy over the simple notebooks and pencils we distributed broke my heart and filled me with immense guilt for taking my privileges for granted.

This visit has changed my perspective completely. I have resolved to stop complaining about trivial things. Furthermore, I plan to start a weekend book collection drive in my society to help those children. I truly hope I can make a small difference in their lives.

Amita

## ✍️ Practice Prompts

1. You participated in an inter-school debate and forgot your lines on stage. Write a diary entry expressing your embarrassment and how you plan to overcome your stage fright.
2. Your parents surprised you with a pet dog on your birthday, something you have wanted for years. Write a diary entry expressing your excitement.
