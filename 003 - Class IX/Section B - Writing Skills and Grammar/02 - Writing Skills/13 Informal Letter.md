# Informal Letter

## 📌 What is an Informal Letter?
An **informal letter** is a personal letter written to friends, family members, or relatives to share personal news, express feelings, ask for advice, or describe an experience. At the Class IX level, the letter should display emotional depth, mature vocabulary, and well-structured paragraphs despite the casual tone.

## 📝 Format / Structure (Word Limit: 120-150 words)

The format follows a left-aligned layout:

1. **Sender’s Address**: Top left corner.
2. **Date**: Below the address (e.g., *24 May 2026*).
3. **Salutation**: E.g., *Dear Rahul, Dear Mom,*
4. **Body of the Letter**:
   - **Paragraph 1 (Introduction)**: Inquire about their health and state the reason for writing the letter.
   - **Paragraph 2 (Main Content)**: Expand on the topic. Give vivid details of the experience, problem, or news you are sharing. Show your feelings.
   - **Paragraph 3 (Conclusion)**: Concluding remarks, hopes for a reply, and sending love to other family members.
5. **Subscription**: *Yours lovingly, / Yours affectionately,*
6. **Name**: First name only.

## ⚖️ CBSE Marking Scheme (Typically 5-8 Marks)
- **Format**: 1-2 Marks (Address, Date, Salutation, Closing)
- **Content**: 3-4 Marks (Relevance, ideas, elaboration)
- **Expression**: 2 Marks (Grammar, vocabulary, friendly but mature tone)

## 💡 Language Tips & Key Phrases
- **Opening**: 
  - *"I was delighted to read your last letter..."*
  - *"I hope this letter finds you in the pink of health."*
- **Tone**: Conversational and warm. You can use contractions (*I've, couldn't, haven't*).
- **Elaboration**: Don't just state facts. If you went on a trip, describe how it made you *feel*.

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Using formal sign-offs**: Never use "Yours faithfully" or "Yours sincerely" in an informal letter.
- **Missing the Date**: Ensure the date is written in the correct format (15 May 2026, not 15/05/26).

## 🌟 Model Example

**Q: You are Sneha/Sumit studying in a boarding school in Dehradun. Write a letter to your mother telling her about your experience of participating in a grueling 3-day adventure camp organized by the school.**

Room No. 42, Ganga Hostel  
Doon Public School  
Dehradun  

12 October 2026  

Dear Mom,  

I hope you and dad are doing wonderful back home. I am writing this letter to share my thrilling experience at the 3-day adventure camp our school organized in Rishikesh last weekend. 

Honestly, mom, when I first saw the itinerary, I was terrified. The camp included rock climbing, valley crossing, and white-water rafting. On the first day during the rock climbing session, my hands were shaking, and I almost gave up halfway. However, our instructor and my friends cheered me on, and when I finally reached the top, the feeling of accomplishment was indescribable! The river rafting the next day was the highlight—the freezing water splashing against our faces was both scary and exhilarating. 

This trip pushed me out of my comfort zone and taught me that I am much stronger than I thought. I can't wait to show you all the photographs during the winter break. 

Give my love to dad and little Rohan. Miss you all!

Yours lovingly,  
Sneha

## ✍️ Practice Prompts

1. Write a letter to your friend apologizing for your rude behavior during an argument last week, and asking for their forgiveness.
2. You recently read a highly inspiring book. Write a letter to your cousin recommending the book to them and explaining why you liked it so much.
