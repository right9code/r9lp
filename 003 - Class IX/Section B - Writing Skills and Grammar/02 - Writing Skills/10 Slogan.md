# Slogan Writing

## 📌 What is a Slogan?
A **slogan** is a memorable, punchy motto or phrase used in campaigns, posters, and advertisements to draw attention to a specific cause or issue. The goal is to convey a deep message in the fewest words possible.

## 📝 Format / Structure 

Slogans are visually and structurally unique:
1. **Length**: Extremely short. Usually 1 to 2 lines (5-12 words maximum).
2. **Visual Presentation**: They should be written in **bold, capital letters**. You can draw a box or a cloud around them in the exam to make them stand out.
3. **Punctuation**: Often end with an exclamation mark (!) to show strong emotion or urgency.

## ⚖️ CBSE Marking Scheme (Typically 2-3 Marks)
- **Creativity and Originality**: 1 Mark
- **Relevance to the Topic**: 1 Mark
- **Impact/Rhythm**: 1 Mark

## 💡 Language Tips & Key Rules
- **Rhyming**: A rhyming slogan is easier to remember (e.g., *Don't be a fool, follow the rule*).
- **Alliteration**: Repeated starting sounds create a nice rhythm (e.g., *Reduce, Reuse, Recycle*).
- **Action Words (Imperatives)**: Tell the reader what to do (e.g., *Stop, Save, Protect, Speak up*).
- **Pun/Wordplay**: Clever use of words scores high marks (e.g., *Water is life, don't let it go down the drain*).

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Writing a full sentence or a paragraph. A slogan is a phrase, not a story.
- Using complex, difficult vocabulary. It needs to be catchy and easily understood by the general public.

## 🌟 Model Examples

**Topic: Blood Donation**
> **DONATE BLOOD, BE A HERO!**
> *(Your Droplets Can Create an Ocean of Life!)*

**Topic: Say No to Junk Food**
> **EAT RIGHT, BE BRIGHT!**
> *(Junk the Fast Food, Fast the Junk Food!)*

**Topic: Girl Child Education**
> **EDUCATE A GIRL, EMPOWER A NATION!**
> *(Give Her a Pen, Watch Her Rewrite the Future!)*
