# Letter to the Editor

## 📌 What is a Letter to the Editor?
A **Letter to the Editor** is a formal letter written to the editor of a newspaper or magazine. Its purpose is to highlight a social issue, express an opinion on a current event, or draw the attention of the concerned authorities towards a public grievance. *It is important to remember that the editor does not solve the problem; they only publish the letter to spread awareness.*

## 📝 Format / Structure (Word Limit: 120-150 words)

The format strictly follows the left-aligned formal layout:

1. **Sender’s Address**: Top left corner.
2. **Date**: E.g., *24 May 2026*.
3. **Receiver’s Designation and Address**: 
   - *The Editor,*
   - *Name of the Newspaper (e.g., The Times of India),*
   - *City.*
4. **Subject**: A brief, catchy line stating the main issue (e.g., *Subject: Menace of stray dogs in our locality.*)
5. **Salutation**: *Respected Sir/Madam* or *Dear Sir/Madam*.
6. **Body of the Letter**:
   - **Paragraph 1 (Introduction)**: Start with a standard opening phrase stating the purpose of the letter.
   - **Paragraph 2 (Main Details)**: Explain the issue, its causes, and its effects on the public. Give specific examples.
   - **Paragraph 3 (Conclusion)**: Offer suggestions and request the editor to publish the letter to draw the attention of the authorities.
7. **Closing**: *Thanking you,*
8. **Subscription**: *Yours truly,* or *Yours sincerely,*
9. **Signature & Name**.

## ⚖️ CBSE Marking Scheme (Typically 5 Marks)
- **Format**: 1 Mark
- **Content**: 2 Marks (Identification of issue, logic, suggestions)
- **Expression**: 2 Marks (Grammar, formal vocabulary, coherence)

## 💡 Language Tips & Key Phrases
- **Opening phrases (Memorize these)**:
  - *"Through the columns of your esteemed newspaper, I wish to draw the attention of the concerned authorities towards..."*
  - *"I am writing to your reputed daily to express my deep concern regarding..."*
- **Closing phrases**:
  - *"I hope my letter finds adequate space in your widely circulated newspaper so that the concerned authorities wake up from their slumber and take necessary action."*
- **Tone**: Formal, objective, and persuasive. Avoid aggressive or abusive language.

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Asking the editor to fix the problem (e.g., *"Please fix the roads"*). The editor is a journalist, not a politician. Ask them to *publish* the issue so authorities see it.
- Writing a story instead of focusing on the facts of the public issue.

## 🌟 Model Example

**Q: You are Aman/Aditi living at 15, Vasant Kunj, New Delhi. Write a letter to the Editor of 'The Hindu' drawing attention to the frequent traffic jams in your area and suggesting measures to improve the situation.**

15, Vasant Kunj  
New Delhi  

10 October 2026  

The Editor  
The Hindu  
New Delhi  

**Subject**: Frequent traffic jams in Vasant Kunj area.

Respected Sir,  

Through the columns of your esteemed newspaper, I wish to draw the attention of the traffic authorities towards the severe issue of frequent traffic jams in the Vasant Kunj area.

For the past few months, the main intersection has become a nightmare for commuters. The primary cause of this chaos is the unauthorized parking of vehicles on both sides of the road by the local shopkeepers. Furthermore, the traffic lights at the main crossing have been dysfunctional for over two weeks. This results in hour-long delays, causing immense hardship to office-goers, school children, and even ambulances.

It is high time the authorities take strict action. I suggest that traffic police be deployed at the intersection during peak hours. Additionally, heavy fines should be imposed on illegal parking. I hope this letter finds a place in your widely circulated daily so that prompt action is taken.

Thanking you,  

Yours truly,  
Aman Sharma
