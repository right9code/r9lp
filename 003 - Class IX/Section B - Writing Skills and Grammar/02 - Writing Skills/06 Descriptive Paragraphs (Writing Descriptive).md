# Descriptive Paragraph (Writing Descriptive)

## 📌 What is Descriptive Writing?
At the Class IX level, **descriptive writing** goes beyond just listing physical attributes. Whether describing a person, an object, or an event, the goal is to use rich, sensory language and precise vocabulary to paint a vivid picture and evoke an emotional response from the reader. 

## 📝 Format / Structure (Word Limit: 100-120 words)

1. **Title**: An engaging heading relevant to the subject.
2. **Introduction (1-2 sentences)**: 
   - Introduce the subject (person, event, or object).
   - Hook the reader and state why this subject is noteworthy.
3. **Body (Details & Impressions) (6-8 sentences)**:
   - **Physical Details**: Appearance, structure, or visual aspects.
   - **Sensory Details**: Sights, sounds, smells, and textures.
   - **Personality/Action**: For a person, describe their habits and traits. For an event, describe the action and atmosphere.
4. **Conclusion (1-2 sentences)**:
   - Your final, lasting impression or emotional connection to the subject.

## ⚖️ CBSE Marking Scheme (Typically 5 Marks)
- **Format (Title)**: 1 Mark
- **Content (Sensory details, observation, relevance)**: 2 Marks
- **Expression (Grammar, descriptive vocabulary, flow)**: 2 Marks

## 💡 Language Tips & Key Rules
- **Metaphors and Similes**: Enhance your writing by comparing things (e.g., *Her smile was as warm as the morning sun*).
- **Strong Verbs**: Instead of "He walked fast," use "He strode hastily."
- **Avoid Clichés**: Do not use overused phrases like "heart of gold" or "busy as a bee." Try to be original.

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Exceeding the word limit. Be concise but descriptive.
- Creating a list of adjectives (e.g., *He is tall, smart, funny, and brave*) instead of weaving them into well-structured sentences.

## 🌟 Model Example (Describing a Person)

**Q: Write a descriptive paragraph describing an interesting person you met during a recent train journey.**

**The Mysterious Fellow Traveler**

During my recent train journey to Jaipur, I shared a compartment with a profoundly intriguing elderly man. He sat quietly by the window, an aura of serene wisdom surrounding him. He was a frail man with deep wrinkles etched across his forehead, telling tales of a long, eventful life. His thick, silver-white beard rested against his faded, olive-green woolen shawl. What struck me most were his eyes; they were a sharp, piercing hazel, sparkling with a youthful curiosity that contradicted his age. He spent hours meticulously sketching the passing landscape in a leather-bound journal with a charcoal pencil, his fingers moving with practiced grace. Though we barely spoke, his quiet passion and dignified presence left a lasting, peaceful impression on me long after the journey ended.

## ✍️ Practice Prompts

1. Write a descriptive paragraph about a **street vendor** you observe every day near your school, focusing on their hard work and interactions with customers.
2. Describe an **antique clock** kept in your living room, focusing on its intricate design, the sound of its ticking, and its emotional value to your family.
