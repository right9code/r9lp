## SECTION A - GRAMMAR
 1. **was reading**
   * **Explanation:** This sentence uses the past continuous tense. It describes an ongoing, longer action in the past ("was reading") that was interrupted by a sudden, completed past event ("the lights suddenly went out"), which is in the simple past tense.
 2. **has been working**
   * **Explanation:** This sentence requires the present perfect continuous tense because the action started in the past (at sunrise) and is still continuing or has a direct impact on the present ("She is exhausted"). The keyword "since" indicates the duration of the action up to the present moment.
 3. **am eating**
   * **Explanation:** The phrase "right now" indicates that the action is happening at the exact moment of speaking. Therefore, the present continuous tense ("am eating") is used to show an ongoing current activity.
 4. **have flown**
   * **Explanation:** The phrase "so far this year" indicates a period of time continuing up to the present. The present perfect tense ("have flown") is used here to describe an action that has happened multiple times in an uncompleted time frame leading up to now.
 5. **snows**
   * **Explanation:** This is a first conditional sentence, which is used to talk about a future possibility and its consequence. In a first conditional clause introduced by "if," we use the simple present tense ("snows") for the condition, while the main clause uses "will" ("will have to cancel").
## SECTION B - LITERATURE
### 2. Summary of "Bharat Our Land"
"Bharat Our Land" is a patriotic poem by Subramania Bharati that celebrates the grandeur, rich heritage, and spiritual wealth of India (Bharat). The poet proudly describes the mighty Himalayas, the sacred river Ganges, and the profound wisdom of the Upanishads, establishing that no other land can match Bharat's greatness. He extols the country’s ancient warriors, sages, and its culture of chivalry, divine music, and truth. The poem shifts from praising past glory to inspiring the youth, urging them to banish poverty, fear, and lethargy. It concludes on a triumphant note, emphasizing that Bharat will overcome all obstacles and continue to enlighten the world with its timeless values.
### 3. Short Answer Questions (Attempted: Q1 and Q2)
 * **Why the narrator initially laughs at her grandmother:**
   The narrator (from Sudha Murty's *How I Taught My Grandmother to Read*) laughs because she finds the idea of a sixty-two-year-old woman starting to learn the alphabet unusual and amusing. At that age, traditional notions suggested it was too late to begin schooling, making the grandmother's sudden academic ambition seem childlike and unexpected to the young narrator.
 * **Significance of the story *Kashi Yatre*:**
   *Kashi Yatre* serves as the core catalyst for the grandmother's transformation. In the story, an old woman sacrifices her savings to help an orphaned girl, mirroring the grandmother’s own selflessness and her ultimate struggle with dependency when she cannot read the next episode herself. It highlights the pain of illiteracy, bridging the gap between fiction and reality, and ultimately drives the grandmother to become independent by learning to read.
