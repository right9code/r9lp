# CBSE Class IX English Prose - 5-Mark Extract-Based Questions

This question bank contains **four distinct sets** of CBSE Class IX English Literature (Prose) 5-mark extract-based questions. Each set follows the official CBSE board exam pattern: **Attempt ANY ONE from A and B**, consisting of a mix of MCQs, True/False, vocabulary-based, and short-answer questions. 

You can choose any two of these sets for your midterm exam.

## SET 1

**VI. Attempt ANY ONE from A and B given below.**                                          **5 MARKS**

**A.** *"When I came back to my village, I saw my grandmother in tears. I was surprised, for I had never seen her cry even in the most difficult situations. What had happened? I was worried. “Avva, is everything all right? Are you okay?” I used to call her Avva, which means mother in the Kannada spoken in north Karnataka. She nodded but did not reply. I did not understand and forgot about it. In the night, after dinner, we were sleeping in the open terrace of our house. It was a summer night and there was a full moon. Avva came and sat next to me. Her affectionate hands touched my forehead. I realised she wanted to speak."*

**(i) Who does "I" refer to in this extract?**
(a) Triveni
(b) Krishtakka
(c) Sudha Murty
(d) Arenla

**(ii) The phrase "never seen her cry even in the most difficult situations" implies that the grandmother was:**
(a) fragile and easily discouraged
(b) strong-willed and resilient
(c) indifferent and unfeeling
(d) dependent and demanding

**(iii) State whether the following statement is TRUE or FALSE:**
The narrator immediately figured out the reason behind her grandmother's tears and comforted her.

**(iv) Find a word in the extract that is a synonym of "loving".**

**(v) What is the meaning of the word "Avva" in the context of the story?**

## SET 2

**VI. Attempt ANY ONE from A and B given below.**                                          **5 MARKS**

**A.** *"The word punkha or pankha originates from the word ‘pankh’, which means feather of a bird. Pankha is used to refer to all fans, whereas the word pankhi denotes a small plumed fan used in ancient India. Evidence of the existence and use of pankhi in India can be found in Buddhist wall paintings at Ajanta. These wall paintings date back to the 2nd century CE. Other representations of the punkha can be found in ancient sculptures and wood carvings."*

**(i) The term "pankhi" in ancient India specifically referred to:**
(a) a massive rope-pulled ceiling fan
(b) a small plumed fan made from feathers
(c) a metallic fan used by kings
(d) a clay vessel used for cooling

**(ii) Where can we find the earliest visual evidence of the use of hand fans in India?**
(a) Buddhist wall paintings at Ajanta
(b) Intricate carvings of Ellora Caves
(c) Royal paintings of the Mughal court
(d) Literary descriptions in the Vedas

**(iii) State whether the following statement is TRUE or FALSE:**
The word "pankha" has its linguistic roots in a word meaning a bird's feather.

**(iv) Find a word in the extract that means "indicates" or "stands as a name for."**

**(v) Apart from wall paintings, where else can representations of the punkha be found?**

## SET 3

**A.** *"“I have decided I want to learn the Kannada alphabet from tomorrow onwards. I will work very hard. I will keep Saraswati Puja day during Dassara as the deadline. Yet I laughed at her. “Avva, at this age of sixty-two you want to learn the alphabet? All your hair is grey, your hands are wrinkled, you wear spectacles and you work so much in the kitchen...” Childishly I made fun of the old lady. But she just smiled."*

**(i) Why does the speaker want to learn the alphabet?**

**(ii) The grandmother's determination shows that learning has no:**
(a) gender bias
(b) age limit
(c) cultural barrier

**(iii) State whether the following statement is TRUE or FALSE:**
The narrator laughed at the grandmother because she was impressed by her ambition.

**(iv) Find a word in the extract that means "a time or date by which something must be finished."**

**(v) Which physical signs of aging did the narrator point out to make fun of the grandmother?**

When she reached the threshold, she found her mother lying on the floor. She was not breathing."*

The girl finished making more pots than her mother's tally.

**(iv) Find a word in the extract that means "the level or point at which you enter a room or building (doorway)."**

## ANSWER KEY & MARKING SCHEME

### SET 1

*   **A. "When I came back to my village..." (How I Taught My Grandmother to Read)**
    *   **(i)** (c) Sudha Murty *(1 Mark)*
    *   **(ii)** (b) strong-willed and resilient *(1 Mark)*
    *   **(iii)** FALSE *(1 Mark)* [Explanation: The narrator did not understand her grandmother's tears at the time and actually forgot about it until night.]
    *   **(iv)** affectionate *(1 Mark)*
    *   **(v)** In North Karnataka Kannada, "Avva" means mother (used here by the granddaughter to affectionately address her grandmother). *(1 Mark)*
*   **B. "It takes months to bring out..." (The Pot Maker)**
    *   **(i)** (b) Arenla *(1 Mark)*
    *   **(ii)** (b) It takes less time, is less messy, can be done indoors in all seasons, and offers better returns. *(1 Mark)*

    *   **(iv)** batch *(1 Mark)*
    *   **(v)** handsome *(1 Mark)*
