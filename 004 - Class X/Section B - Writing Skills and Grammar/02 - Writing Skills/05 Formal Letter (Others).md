# Formal Letter: Institutional / Other Letters

This category generally covers letters written to heads of educational institutions (Principals, Headmasters, Directors of Institutes, Librarians) and other official bodies not covered under Business or Civic authorities. These are written to request leave, ask for fee concessions, suggest improvements in school facilities, or apply for specialized courses.

*(Note: The general format of all formal letters is the same. Refer to the 'Letter to Editor' notes for the universal 10-step layout).*

## I. Details for Exam
*   **Word Limit:** 100–120 words (Body).
*   **Marks:** 5 Marks (Format 1 + Content 2 + Expression 2).
*   **Tone:** Highly respectful, formal, and polite.

---

## II. Types of Institutional Letters

### 1. Letter to Principal (Suggestions / Facilities)
Written by a student or a parent to suggest improvements or complain about a lack of facilities in the school.

**Body Structure:**
*   **Para 1 (Introduction):** Introduce yourself (e.g., "I am [Name], a student of Class X-A / a parent of [Student Name] who studies in Class X-A."). State the purpose of the letter.
*   **Para 2 (Details):** Explain the current issue and why the new facility or improvement is needed. (e.g., "The library currently lacks updated reference books for the upcoming board exams...").
*   **Para 3 (Conclusion):** Request favorable action. "I request you to kindly look into this matter and sanction the purchase of new books."

### 2. Letter to Institute Director (Inquiry / Application)
Written to inquire about a course, diploma, or workshop. (This is very similar to a Business Inquiry letter).

**Body Structure:**
*   **Para 1 (Introduction):** Refer to the advertisement and state your interest.
*   **Para 2 (Details):** Ask about course duration, fee structure, eligibility, and placement opportunities.
*   **Para 3 (Conclusion):** Request a prospectus and prompt reply.

---

## III. Key Phrases to Memorize

**For Principal:**
*   *With due respect, I would like to state that...*
*   *On behalf of the students of Class X, I wish to bring to your attention...*
*   *We would be highly obliged if you could...*
*   *I request you to kindly consider my application favorably.*

**For Institute Director:**
*   *Apropos to your advertisement in [Newspaper Name]...*
*   *I am writing to seek information regarding the [Course Name]...*
*   *Please send me the detailed brochure and enrollment form.*

---

## IV. Sample Practice Question

**Question:** You are Namita/Namit, a student of Class X at XYZ Public School. Write a letter to the Principal of your school in 100-120 words, requesting the organization of special remedial classes for Mathematics after school hours, as many students are finding the new syllabus difficult.

**Sample Answer:**

XYZ Public School  
New Delhi

15 August 2026

The Principal  
XYZ Public School  
New Delhi

**Subject:** <ins>Request for organizing remedial classes in Mathematics.</ins>

Respected Sir/Ma'am,

With due respect, I am Namit, a student of Class X-A. On behalf of my classmates, I am writing this letter to request the organization of special remedial classes for Mathematics after regular school hours.

With the introduction of the new syllabus, many students are finding it difficult to grasp advanced concepts in Algebra and Trigonometry during the standard 40-minute periods. As the Board examinations are approaching, there is a growing anxiety among the students regarding our preparation. Extra classes dedicated to doubt-clearing and intensive practice would immensely benefit us and help improve the overall academic performance of the class.

I earnestly request you to consider our situation and kindly arrange for remedial Mathematics classes for Class X students. We shall be highly obliged for your support.

Thanking you

Yours obediently,
*(Signature)*
Namit  
(Class X-A)
