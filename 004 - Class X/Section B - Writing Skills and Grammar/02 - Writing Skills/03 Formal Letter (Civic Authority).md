# Formal Letter: Civic Authority Letters

Civic Authority Letters are written to public officials (Municipal Commissioner, District Magistrate, SHO, Traffic Police Commissioner, RWA President) to draw attention to civic issues, lodge complaints, or request the implementation of new community initiatives.

*(Note: The general format of all formal letters is the same. Refer to the 'Letter to Editor' notes for the universal 10-step layout).*

## I. Details for Exam
*   **Word Limit:** 100–120 words (Body).
*   **Marks:** 5 Marks (Format 1 + Content 2 + Expression 2).
*   **Tone:** Respectful, firm, objective, and solution-oriented. Avoid aggressive or abusive language.
*   **Key Authorities & Their Domains:**
    *   *Municipal Commissioner:* Sanitation, garbage, bad roads, parks, stray animals, street lights.
    *   *SHO (Station House Officer) / Police Commissioner:* Law and order, thefts, lack of security, illegal parking.
    *   *Traffic Commissioner:* Traffic jams, missing signals, reckless driving.
    *   *RWA (Resident Welfare Association) President:* Internal society matters (water supply, gym, community events).

---

## II. Body Structure of a Civic Authority Letter

### Para 1: Introduction (The Purpose)
Identify yourself (as a resident/citizen) and clearly state the purpose of the letter.
*   *Example:* "I am a resident of [Colony Name] and I am writing to bring to your urgent attention the deteriorating condition of the main road connecting our society to the highway."

### Para 2: Details of the Problem (Cause & Effect)
Explain the issue in detail and how it is affecting the residents.
*   *Example:* "For the past three months, the road has been riddled with massive potholes. During the monsoon, these potholes fill with water, becoming invisible to drivers. This has led to severe traffic congestion during peak hours and several minor accidents involving two-wheelers."

### Para 3: Conclusion & Suggestions (Action Required)
Suggest a practical solution and request immediate action.
*   *Example:* "I request you to kindly deploy a repair team at the earliest to relay the damaged stretches. Fixing the road will ensure the safety of daily commuters and prevent fatal accidents. We hope for your prompt intervention."

---

## III. Key Phrases to Memorize

**Opening Lines:**
*   *I am writing this letter to bring to your notice the pressing issue of...*
*   *On behalf of the residents of [Colony], I wish to draw your attention to...*

**Describing the Impact:**
*   *This has become a major cause of concern for the residents because...*
*   *It poses a severe threat to the safety and well-being of the community.*
*   *Despite repeated complaints to the local office, no action has been taken so far.*

**Closing Lines:**
*   *I earnestly request you to look into this matter urgently and take necessary remedial action.*
*   *We hope that the authorities will awaken from their slumber and resolve this issue.*
*   *Looking forward to your prompt and positive response.*

---

## IV. Sample Practice Question

**Question:** You are Rohan / Reema, a resident of 45, Greenfield Colony, Pune. The park in your locality, which was once a beautiful place, is now in a state of utter neglect. It has become a dumping ground for garbage and a haven for anti-social elements. Write a letter to the Municipal Commissioner of Pune in 100-120 words, complaining about the issue and requesting its immediate restoration.

**Sample Answer:**

45, Greenfield Colony  
Pune

10 April 2026

The Municipal Commissioner  
Municipal Corporation of Pune  
Pune

**Subject:** <ins>Complaint regarding the miserable condition of the local park.</ins>

Sir,

On behalf of the residents of Greenfield Colony, I am writing to draw your urgent attention to the deplorable condition of the public park in our locality. 

Once a lush green space used by children for playing and elderly residents for morning walks, the park is now in a state of utter neglect. The boundary walls are broken, and it has practically become a dumping ground for the neighborhood's garbage. The foul stench makes it impossible to even walk past it. Furthermore, due to the lack of working streetlights, the park has become a safe haven for anti-social elements at night, posing a severe security threat to the residents.

Despite several complaints filed at the local ward office, no action has been taken. I earnestly request you to direct the concerned department to clean the park, repair the lights, and restore it to its former glory. We hope for your prompt intervention.

Thanking you

Yours faithfully,
*(Signature)*
Rohan
