# Analytical Paragraph Writing

An analytical paragraph is a form of descriptive writing which is written on the basis of a given chart, graph, data, outline, clues, table, etc. When writing an analytical paragraph, one has to remember to describe the facts in the best possible manner and cover the information provided comprehensively.

## I. Details for Exam
*   **Word Limit:** 100–120 words.
*   **Marks:** 5 Marks.
*   **Marks Distribution:** Content + Expression (Coherence/Cohesion & Accuracy) = 2 + 3 = 5 Marks.
*   **Tone:** Formal, objective, and analytical. Use passive voice where applicable. Do not use first-person pronouns (I, me, my, we).
*   **Format:** A single continuous paragraph. No bullet points.

## II. Format of an Analytical Paragraph

An analytical paragraph shall be divided into three parts:

### 1. Introduction (1-2 lines)
*   The introduction should explain what the graph/chart/table/data illustrates or shows.
*   Do not copy the exact question. Paraphrase it.
*   *Useful Phrases:* "The given bar graph illustrates...", "The provided pie chart depicts...", "The table suggests that...", "The line graph shows the relationship between..."

### 2. Body of the Paragraph (4-5 lines)
*   This part should contain the main analysis of the data. 
*   Highlight the **maximum** and **minimum** values.
*   Identify **trends**, comparisons, and contrasts. 
*   Group similar data together to make logical comparisons.
*   Mention exact figures (percentages, fractions, numbers) to support your analysis.
*   *Useful Phrases for Quantities:* "exactly one-third", "almost a quarter of", "a striking majority", "a negligible amount".
*   *Useful Phrases for Comparisons:* "In contrast to...", "On the other hand...", "Similarly...", "As opposed to...".
*   *Useful Phrases for Trends:* "A steep rise", "A gradual decline", "Remained constant", "Fluctuated widely".

### 3. Conclusion (1-2 lines)
*   The conclusion should summarize the overall trend or the most striking feature of the data.
*   Never include your personal opinion or any new information not present in the given data.
*   *Useful Phrases:* "Overall, it can be concluded that...", "To sum up...", "In a nutshell, the chart reveals that...".

## III. Common Mistakes to Avoid
1.  **Adding Personal Opinions:** Do not write "I think", "In my opinion", or provide solutions unless explicitly asked.
2.  **Using Bullet Points:** An analytical paragraph must be a single cohesive paragraph.
3.  **Ignoring the Big Picture:** Do not just list data point after data point. Group the data and show the relationships (e.g., "While X increased, Y decreased").
4.  **Incorrect Tense:** If the data refers to a past year (e.g., 2015), strictly use the Past Tense. If it is general data, use the Present Tense.

## V. Practice Questions (PYQ Based)

### Question 1 (Data Based)
The given pie chart shows the proportion of people from different households living in poverty in the UK in 2002. Write an analytical paragraph to describe the information in 100-120 words.
*(Data context: Sole parents: 26%, Single without children: 24%, Couples with children: 15%, Couples without children: 9%, Single aged persons: 7%, Aged couples: 5%, All households: 14%)*

**Sample Answer:**
The provided pie chart illustrates the percentage of various household types living in poverty in the United Kingdom during the year 2002. Overall, it is evident that single-parent households and individuals without children were the most susceptible to poverty, whereas aged couples were the least affected. Specifically, sole parents constituted the largest demographic living in poverty at 26%, closely followed by single people without children at 24%. Couples with children also formed a significant portion, making up 15% of the impoverished population. In contrast, couples without children experienced a much lower poverty rate at 9%. The older population was the most financially secure, with single aged persons and aged couples accounting for only 7% and 5% of the poverty demographic, respectively. In a nutshell, younger singles and single-parent families faced the highest economic hardship in the UK in 2002 compared to older demographics.

### Question 2 (Argumentative/Clue Based)
Read the following excerpt from an article: *"The ban on single-use plastic is impractical. Alternatives are too expensive for local vendors, and the transition period is too short."* Write a paragraph analyzing the given argument. You may either agree or disagree with the statement. (100-120 words)

**Sample Answer:**
The given excerpt argues against the ban on single-use plastic, citing high costs of alternatives and an insufficient transition period as major hurdles for local vendors. While the economic concerns of small-scale vendors are valid in the short term, the argument overlooks the devastating, long-term environmental consequences of single-use plastics. Continuing their use leads to irreversible damage to marine life and soil quality, which ultimately incurs higher economic costs in environmental cleanup and healthcare. Furthermore, government subsidies and phased implementations can ease the financial burden on vendors during the transition. Eco-friendly alternatives, once mass-produced, will inevitably become cost-effective. Therefore, while the immediate implementation may pose logistical challenges, the ban on single-use plastic is an absolute necessity for sustainable development, making the argument against it fundamentally short-sighted.
