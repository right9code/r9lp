# Reported Speech (Direct & Indirect Speech)

There are two ways of relating what a person has said:
1. **Direct Speech:** Quoting the exact words of the speaker. The words are enclosed in quotation marks (" ").
   - *Example:* Ram said, "I am very busy now."
2. **Indirect Speech (Reported Speech):** Reporting what the speaker said without quoting their exact words. Quotation marks are removed, and a conjunction (like 'that') is introduced.
   - *Example:* Ram said that he was very busy then.

---

## Basic Terminology

- *He said, "I am going to school."*
- **Reporting Verb:** *said* (The verb used to introduce the spoken words).
- **Reported Speech:** *"I am going to school"* (The exact words spoken).

---

## Rules for Changing Direct to Indirect Speech

When converting Direct Speech into Indirect Speech, three main changes occur:
1. Change of Tense
2. Change of Pronouns
3. Change of Time and Place Expressions

### 1. Change of Tense

**Rule A:** If the Reporting Verb is in the **Present** or **Future** tense, the tense of the Reported Speech does NOT change.
- *Direct:* He says, "I **am** unwell."
- *Indirect:* He says that he **is** unwell.
- *Direct:* She will say, "I **like** tea."
- *Indirect:* She will say that she **likes** tea.

**Rule B:** If the Reporting Verb is in the **Past** tense (e.g., said), the tense of the Reported Speech changes to the corresponding past tense.

| Direct Speech (Tense) | Indirect Speech (Tense) | Example (Direct -> Indirect) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Simple Present | Simple Past | "I **play**" -> he said that he **played**. |
| Present Continuous | Past Continuous | "I **am playing**" -> he said that he **was playing**. |
| Present Perfect | Past Perfect | "I **have played**" -> he said that he **had played**. |
| Present Perfect Cont. | Past Perfect Cont. | "I **have been playing**" -> he said that he **had been playing**. |
| Simple Past | Past Perfect | "I **played**" -> he said that he **had played**. |
| Past Continuous | Past Perfect Cont. | "I **was playing**" -> he said that he **had been playing**. |
| Past Perfect | No Change | "I **had played**" -> he said that he **had played**. |

**Modals Change as follows:**
- will -> would
- shall -> should
- can -> could
- may -> might
- (must, should, ought to, could, would, might usually do NOT change).

⚠️ **EXCEPTION:** If the Reported Speech states a **Universal Truth, General Fact, or Habitual Action**, its tense remains unchanged even if the Reporting Verb is in the Past tense.  
e.g. The teacher said, "The earth revolves around the sun."  
The teacher said that the earth revolves around the sun.

### 2. Change of Pronouns

Pronouns change according to the **SON Rule** (Subject, Object, No Change):
- **First Person (I, we, me, us, my, our):** Changes according to the **Subject** of the Reporting Verb.
  - *Direct:* **He** said, "**I** like the book."
  - *Indirect:* **He** said that **he** liked the book.
- **Second Person (You, your):** Changes according to the **Object** of the Reporting Verb.
  - *Direct:* He said to **me**, "You are late."
  - *Indirect:* He told **me** that **I** was late.
- **Third Person (He, she, it, they, him, her, them):** **No change**.
  - *Direct:* I said, "She is a good girl."
  - *Indirect:* I said that she was a good girl.

### 3. Change of Time and Place Expressions

Words expressing nearness in time or place are changed into words expressing distance.

| Direct Speech | Indirect Speech |
| :--- | :--- |
| Here | There |
| This | That |
| These | Those |
| Now | Then |
| Today | That day |
| Tonight | That night |
| Yesterday | The previous day / The day before |
| Tomorrow | The next day / The following day |
| Ago | Before |
| Thus | So |

## Rules for Different Types of Sentences

### A. Assertive Sentences (Statements)
- Remove quotation marks and use the conjunction **that**.
- *said to* changes to *told*.
- *Direct:* Rahul said to me, "I am reading a book now."
- *Indirect:* Rahul told me that he was reading a book then.

### B. Interrogative Sentences (Questions)
- The reporting verb changes to *asked, inquired, or demanded*.
- The sentence structure changes from Question to Statement (Subject + Verb).
- **Wh- questions (What, Why, Where, How, etc.):** No conjunction is used. The question word acts as the joining word.
  - *Direct:* He said to me, "What are you doing?"
  - *Indirect:* He asked me what I was doing.
- **Yes/No questions (starting with auxiliary verbs like Do, Is, Are, Have):** Use the conjunction **if** or **whether**.
  - *Direct:* She said to him, "Are you coming with us?"
  - *Indirect:* She asked him if he was coming with them.

### C. Imperative Sentences (Commands, Requests, Advice)
- The reporting verb changes to *ordered, requested, advised, commanded, forbade*, etc., depending on the tone.
- The conjunction **to** is used before the base verb (to + V1).
- For negative commands, use **not to**.
- *Direct:* The teacher said to the boy, "Sit down."
- *Indirect:* The teacher ordered the boy to sit down.
- *Direct:* He said to me, "Please help me."
- *Indirect:* He requested me to help him.
- *Direct:* The doctor said, "Do not smoke."
- *Indirect:* The doctor advised me not to smoke. (OR: The doctor forbade me to smoke).

⚠️ **EXCEPTION:** Sentences starting with "Let's" or "Let us" suggest a proposal. The reporting verb changes to "proposed" or "suggested", and we use the conjunction "that" followed by "they should" or "we should".  
e.g. He said to his friends, "Let's go to the cinema."  
He suggested to his friends that they should go to the cinema.

### D. Exclamatory Sentences (Joy, Sorrow, Surprise)
- The reporting verb changes to *exclaimed with joy, exclaimed with sorrow, exclaimed with surprise*, etc.
- The conjunction **that** is used.
- The sentence becomes a statement (remove exclamation marks and words like alas, hurrah, wow).
- *Direct:* The boy said, "Hurrah! We have won the match."
- *Indirect:* The boy exclaimed with joy that they had won the match.
- *Direct:* She said, "Alas! I am undone."
- *Indirect:* She exclaimed with sorrow that she was undone.

# PRACTICE QUESTIONS

## I. Change the following sentences into Indirect Speech.

**A. Assertive Sentences**
1. He said, "I have passed the examination."
2. The teacher said, "The earth goes around the sun."
3. She said to me, "I am going to your house today."

**B. Interrogative Sentences**
1. He said to me, "Are you a student?"
2. She asked him, "Where do you live?"
3. The police said to the man, "Have you seen the thief?"

**C. Imperative Sentences**
1. The teacher said to the boys, "Do not make a noise."
2. The captain said to the soldiers, "March forward."
3. The beggar said to the lady, "Please give me some food."

**D. Exclamatory Sentences**
1. They said, "Alas! The great leader is dead."
2. The boy said, "What a beautiful flower it is!"

---

## Answers

**I. A.**
1. He said that he had passed the examination.
2. The teacher said that the earth goes around the sun.
3. She told me that she was going to my house that day.

**I. B.**
1. He asked me if I was a student.
2. She asked him where he lived.
3. The police asked the man if he had seen the thief.

**I. C.**
1. The teacher forbade the boys to make a noise. (Or: The teacher ordered the boys not to make a noise.)
2. The captain commanded the soldiers to march forward.
3. The beggar requested the lady to give him some food.

**I. D.**
1. They exclaimed with sorrow that the great leader was dead.
2. The boy exclaimed with joy that it was a very beautiful flower. (Or: The boy exclaimed with wonder that it was a beautiful flower.)
