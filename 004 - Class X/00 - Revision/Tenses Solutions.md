1. She **was watching** TV when her father came home.  
   → *Past Continuous tense*: used for an action in progress at a specific time in the past, interrupted by another past event.

2. They **go** to school every day by bus.  
   → *Present Simple tense*: used for habitual or regular actions (daily routine).

3. By next month, he **will have finished** his project.  
   → *Future Perfect tense*: used for an action that will be completed before a future time.

4. I **have seen** this film before.  
   → *Present Perfect tense*: used for a past action that has a result or connection to the present.

5. The train **had left** by the time we reached the platform.  
   → *Past Perfect tense*: used for the earlier of two past actions (the train left before we reached).

6. When I met her, she **was looking** for a job.  
   → *Past Continuous tense*: used for a background action in progress at a point in the past.

7. He **has been living** in this city since 2020.  
   → *Present Perfect Continuous tense*: used for an action that started in the past and is still continuing.

8. If you **arrive** late, you will miss the class.  
   → *Present Simple tense in a conditional clause*: used in “if” clauses for future condition (main clause is in Future Simple).

9. The students **are writing** quietly in the exam hall now.  
   → *Present Continuous tense*: used for an action happening at the moment of speaking.

10. We **visited** our grandparents last week.  
    → *Past Simple tense*: used for a completed action in the past with a clear time reference.

11. By the time you come, they **will have eaten** all the food.  
    → *Future Perfect tense*: used for an action that will be finished before a future moment.

12. She **was** very happy yesterday.  
    → *Past Simple tense*: used for a state or condition that existed in the past.

13. He **has completed** his exercises already.  
    → *Present Perfect tense*: used for a recently finished action that is relevant now (“already” is a clue).

14. Look! The bird **is flying** into the sky.  
    → *Present Continuous tense*: used for an action happening right now (emphasized by “look!”).

15. The workers **had been repairing** the road for two months when the inspector visited.  
    → *Past Perfect Continuous tense*: used for an action that had been going on over a period before another past event.






16. **Have you finished** your homework yet?  
   → *Present Perfect tense (interrogative)*: used to ask if a past action is already completed and still relevant now (clue: “yet”).

17. She **did not arrive** late for school yesterday.  
   → *Past Simple tense (negative)*: used for a completed action in the past; negative form uses “did not + base verb”.

18. **Will they come** to the party next week?  
   → *Future Simple tense (interrogative)*: used to ask about a future plan or event (time clue: “next week”).

19. I **have not found** my phone anywhere in the room.  
   → *Present Perfect tense (negative)*: shows that up to now, the action has not happened (“not found”).

20. **Was he playing** cricket when you met him?  
   → *Past Continuous tense (interrogative)*: asks about an action that was in progress at a specific time in the past.

21. They **had watched** the movie before the lights went out.  
   → *Past Perfect tense*: shows that one past action (watching) was completed before another past event (lights going out).

22. **Did you leave** your keys at home this morning?  
   → *Past Simple tense (interrogative)*: used to ask about a completed action in the past with a specific time (“this morning”).

23. He **was** very careful while crossing the road.  
   → *Past Simple tense*: describes a past state or condition at a specific time (implied in the context).

24. **Have the students understood** the instructions clearly?  
   → *Present Perfect tense (interrogative)*: asks if the students have completed the action of understanding, and it is still relevant now.

25. We **have been waiting** for the bus since 8 a.m.  
    → *Present Perfect Continuous tense*: used for an action that started in the past and is still continuing (time clue: “since 8 a.m.”).
