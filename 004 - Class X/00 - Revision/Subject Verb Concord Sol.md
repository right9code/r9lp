## Answer Key and Explanations

1. **is** — The subject is "quality" (singular), not "mangoes".
2. **have** — In "neither/nor" constructions, the verb agrees with the nearest subject ("students").
3. **was** — The title of a book is treated as a singular noun.
4. **is** — "Bread and butter" here expresses a single idea/concept (a meal).
5. **was** — When used with "a pair of", plural nouns like binoculars take a singular verb.
6. **are** — When a collective noun shows internal disagreement or division, it takes a plural verb.
7. **is** — Academic subjects ending in 's' are treated as singular.
8. **was** — Nouns qualified by "every" or "each" take a singular verb even when joined by "and".
9. **is** — A specific amount of money is treated as a single collective unit.
10. **has** — When a subject is joined by "along with", the verb agrees with the first subject ("leader").
