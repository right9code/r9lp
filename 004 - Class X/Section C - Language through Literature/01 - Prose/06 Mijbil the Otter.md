# Mijbil the Otter - Class 10 Comprehensive Notes

**Author:** Gavin Maxwell
**(From the textbook *First Flight*)**

---

## Detailed Summary

**Phase I: The Arrival (The Exposition)**
The story begins in Basra, Iraq, in 1956. Maxwell’s dog, Jonnie, had died, and feeling too lonely to keep another dog, he decides to keep an otter. His friend suggests the Tigris marshes as the best source. Later, two Arabs arrive at his room with a sack containing a small, mud-caked creature that looks like a "medievally conceived dragon."

**Phase II: Domestication (Rising Action)**
It takes Maxwell days of hard work to remove the mud and reveal that Mijbil is actually a chocolate-brown "Maxwell’s Otter" (*Lutrogale perspicillata maxwelli*). When taken to the bathroom, Mijbil goes wild with joy in the bathtub. Maxwell learns that for an otter, water must be kept in motion; if static, it must be provoked into splashing.

**Phase III: The Nightmare Flight (The Climax)**
British Airways refuses to carry animals, so Maxwell books a flight to Paris via Air France, and then to London. The airline insists the otter be in a small box. Before leaving, Maxwell finds Mijbil has torn the zinc lining of the box and is bleeding. During the flight, Mijbil escapes the box, causing chaos and screams. The helpful Air Hostess assists Maxwell, and eventually, Mijbil returns to his lap.

**Phase IV: Life in London (Falling Action)**
Maxwell stays in a flat in London for a month, taking Mijbil for walks on a lead like a dog. Mijbil invents games, like rolling a ping-pong ball on a broken suitcase, and learns to jump onto the low wall of a primary school, distracting students.

**Phase V: The Public Reaction (Resolution)**
Londoners, unfamiliar with otters, make wild guesses about Mijbil's species, calling him a "baby seal," a "squirrel," a "walrus," a "beaver," and even a "brontosaurus." A laborer digging a hole ultimately asks, "Here, Mister—what is that supposed to be?" highlighting the disconnect between urban life and wild nature.

## Character Analysis

### A. Gavin Maxwell (The Narrator)
*   **Compassionate & Patient**: He goes to great lengths to care for Mijbil, transforming his bathtub into a playground and enduring the chaos of a transatlantic flight.
*   **Observant**: He meticulously records Mijbil’s behaviors, such as his "thraldom to static water" and his inventive games with marbles.
*   **Adventurous**: Willing to take the risk of bringing an otter from the marshes of Iraq to the Scottish Highlands.

### B. Mijbil (The Otter)
*   **Intelligent & Inventive**: He creates his own games, such as balancing a ping-pong ball on the slanting lid of a suitcase.
*   **Affectionate**: Eventually develops a deep bond with Maxwell, sleeping in his bed and seeking his company.
*   **Playful & Energetic**: His life is a "continuous series of diversions," from plunging into water to "dribbling" a marble like a soccer player.
*   **Initially Aloof**: Described as "neither hostile nor friendly," he was simply indifferent during the first twenty-four hours.

### C. The Air Hostess
*   **Empathetic & Professional**: Maxwell calls her "the very queen of her kind." She understands the narrator's anxiety and suggests he keep the pet on his knee.

---

## Central Themes

### 1. The Bond Between Man and Nature
The story explores the deep emotional connection that can form between different species, transcending the need for verbal communication.

### 2. The Challenge of Displacement
It highlights the ethical and physical difficulties of removing an animal from its natural habitat (the Iraqi marshes) to a human, urban environment (London).

### 3. Curiosity and Ignorance
The various names Londoners give Mijbil show how humans struggle to categorize things that fall outside their daily experience, reflecting an urban disconnect from nature.

## Exam-Style Value Points (6-Mark Questions)

1.  **Maxwell’s Responsibility**: He didn't just "own" a pet; he became a guardian who prioritized the animal's comfort over his own convenience, even during a terrifying flight.
2.  **Mijbil’s Intelligence**: His ability to open a tap and his invention of the suitcase game prove that animals possess high cognitive functions and a need for mental stimulation.
3.  **The "Maxwell's Otter" Classification**: The fact that the species was named after the author adds a layer of scientific significance to a personal memoir.
4.  **Social Commentary**: The reaction of the London public reflects the "urban-rural divide," where city dwellers are completely detached from the natural world.
5.  **The Role of the Air Hostess**: Her presence is a "Value Point" for questions regarding human kindness; she represents the ideal response to an unexpected crisis.

## Question & Answers (NCERT Solutions)

### I. Oral Comprehension Check

**1. What ‘experiment’ did Maxwell think Camusfearna would be suitable for?**
**Ans:** When Maxwell travelled to Southern Iraq, a thought came to his mind that he should keep an otter instead of a dog. He thought that Camusfearna, ringed by water, would be an eminently suitable spot for this experiment.

**2. Why does he go to Basra? How long does he wait there, and why?**
**Ans:** He went to the Consulate-General in Basra to collect and answer his mail from Europe. He waited there for over a week. When he found that his mail hadn’t arrived, he cabled England. It remained unresponsive for three days. He finally received his mail after five days.

**3. How does he get the otter? Does he like it? Pick out the words that tell you this.**
**Ans:** On the day he received his mail, he went to his bedroom to read it. There he found two Arabs with the otter in a sack. They handed him a note from his friend and the otter. Yes, he liked it. The phrase "an otter fixation" and calling it an "otter thraldom" tells us this.

**4. Why was the otter named ‘Maxwell’s otter’?**
**Ans:** The otter was named ‘Maxwell’s otter’ because it was of a race previously unknown to science and was eventually christened by zoologists as *Lutrogale perspicillata maxwelli* or Maxwell’s otter.

**5. Tick the right answer. In the beginning, the otter was • aloof and indifferent • friendly • hostile**
**Ans:** In the beginning, the otter was aloof and indifferent.

**6. What happened when Maxwell took Mijbil to the bathroom? How did it behave two days after that?**
**Ans:** When Maxwell took Mijbil to the bathroom, he went wild with joy in the water for half an hour. He plunged and rolled in it, making enough slosh and splash for a hippo. Two days later, he escaped from the bedroom and fumbled at the chromium taps with his paws, turning on the tap from a trickle to a full flow.

**7. How was Mij to be transported to England?**
**Ans:** Mij was to be transported to England by a flight to Paris, and from there to London, because British Airlines didn’t permit animals to fly. Another airline insisted on packing Mij into a square box not bigger than eighteen inches to be kept on the floor at his feet.

**8. What did Mij do to the box?**
**Ans:** The author put Mij into the box and left for a hurried meal. In his attempt to get out of the box, Mij tore the zinc lining of the box to shreds. He got badly wounded during his attempt and was bleeding.

**9. Why did Maxwell put the otter back in the box? How do you think he felt when he did this?**
**Ans:** Maxwell put the otter back in the box because he was getting late for the flight. It was just ten minutes until the flight time, and the airport was five miles away. He felt miserable and horrified when he did this because Mij was in a pitiful, bloody condition.

**10. Why does Maxwell say the airhostess was ‘the very queen of her kind’?**
**Ans:** Maxwell said the airhostess was ‘the very queen of her kind’ because she listened to Maxwell’s events of the last half-hour, sympathized with him, and kindly suggested that he could keep his pet on his knees.

**11. What happened when the box was opened?**
**Ans:** When the box was opened, Mij was out of it in a flash and disappeared at high speed down the aircraft. He caused squawks and shrieks. A woman stood up on her seat screaming, "A rat! A rat!"

**12. What game had Mij invented?**
**Ans:** Mij had invented a game of rolling a ping-pong ball. He would keep the ball on the raised, sloping end of a damaged suitcase and allow it to run down its length. He would then dash around to the other end to ambush its arrival.

**13. What are ‘compulsive habits’? What does Maxwell say are the compulsive habits of? (i) school children (ii) Mij?**
**Ans:** Habits impossible to control are compulsive habits. 
(i) School children must place their feet squarely on the center of each paving block; must touch every seventh upright of the iron railings, or pass to the outside of every second lamp post. 
(ii) Mij would tug the author to a primary school wall, jump onto it, and gallop the full length of its thirty yards, distracting both pupils and staff.

**14. What group of animals do otters belong to?**
**Ans:** According to the author, otters belong to a comparatively small group of animals called mustellines, shared by the badger, mongoose, weasel, stoat, mink, and others.

**15. What guesses did the Londoners make about what Mij was?**
**Ans:** Londoners guessed that Mij was a baby seal, a squirrel, a walrus, a hippo, a beaver, a bear cub, a leopard that had changed its spots, and even a brontosaur. Nobody guessed Mij was an otter.

### II. Thinking About the Text

**1. What things does Mij do which tell you that he is an intelligent, friendly and fun-loving animal who needs love?**
**Ans:** Mij was an intelligent animal. He invented his own game with a ping-pong ball on a suitcase. He learned how to screw the tap until water began to flow so he could play. Though aloof at first, he soon became friendly and formed a special attachment to Maxwell, responding to his name and sleeping by his side. When Maxwell left him in the box and he got hurt, he clung to Maxwell's feet upon being let out. He was fun-loving, enjoying various toys like marbles and a terrapin shell, and clearly thrived on love and affection.

**2. What are some of the things we come to know about otters from this text?**
**Ans:** We learn that otters belong to a small group of animals called mustellines. They are small creatures with dark brown fur and are found in large numbers in marshes. Otters live near water, enjoy playing in it, and require water to be in motion; static water must be splashed. They are highly active, intelligent animals that can be tamed and love playing games with small objects.

**3. Why is Mij’s species now known to the world as Maxwell’s otter?**
**Ans:** Maxwell’s otter was of a race previously unknown to science. When it was finally cataloged and named by zoologists, it was given the scientific name *Lutrogale perspicillata maxwelli*, or Maxwell’s otter, in honor of the author who brought it from Iraq.

**4. Maxwell in the story speaks for the otter, Mij. He tells us what the otter feels and thinks on different occasions. Complete the column to say what Maxwell says about what Mij feels and thinks.**
**Ans:** 
- **What Mijbil does:** Plunges, rolls in the water, and makes the water splosh and splash. 
  **How Mijbil feels or thinks:** Mij feels that every drop of water should be enjoyed and extended; static water is a waste and must be made dynamic.
- **What Mijbil does:** Screws the tap in the wrong way. 
  **How Mijbil feels or thinks:** Mij feels irritated and chittered with irritation and disappointment when the tap does not cooperate.
- **What Mijbil does:** Nuzzles Maxwell’s face and neck in the aeroplane. 
  **How Mijbil feels or thinks:** Mij feels happy, secure, and comforted by his owner's presence after a traumatic experience.
