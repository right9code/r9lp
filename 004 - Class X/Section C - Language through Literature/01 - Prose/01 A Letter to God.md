# A Letter to God - Class 10 Comprehensive Notes

**Author:** G.L. Fuentes
**(From the textbook *First Flight*)**

---

## Detailed Summary

"A Letter to God" by G.L. Fuentes is a story of extreme faith and the irony of human nature.

**Phase 1: The Hope**
Lencho’s house sits on the crest of a low hill, overlooking his field of ripe corn. All the crop needs is a "downpour or at least a shower." During dinner, big drops of rain begin to fall. Lencho is ecstatic, calling the raindrops "new coins"—the big drops are ten-cent pieces and the little ones are fives.

**Phase 2: The Disaster**
Suddenly, a strong wind blows, and the rain turns into huge hailstones. The hail falls for an hour, leaving the field "white, as if covered with salt." Not a leaf remains on the trees; the corn is totally destroyed. Lencho’s soul is filled with sadness. He tells his sons that even a "plague of locusts" would have left more than the hail did.

**Phase 3: The Letter**
Lencho has only one hope: help from God. He writes a letter addressed to God, asking for 100 pesos to sow his field again and live until the next crop. He goes to town, places a stamp on the letter, and drops it into the mailbox.

**Phase 4: The Postmaster's Gesture**
A postman finds the letter and shows it to the Postmaster. They laugh at first, but the Postmaster soon turns serious, admiring Lencho's "immense faith." To keep Lencho's faith alive, the Postmaster collects money from his employees and gives part of his own salary. He manages to gather slightly more than half (70 pesos). He puts the money in an envelope with a letter containing only the signature: GOD.

**Phase 5: The Reaction and Irony**
On Sunday, Lencho arrives at the post office. When he receives the letter, he is not at all surprised to see the money—such was his confidence. His confidence turns to anger when he counts the money and finds only 70 pesos. He believes "God could not have made a mistake." Lencho immediately writes another letter to God. He asks for the remaining 30 pesos but tells God not to send it through the mail. He claims that the post-office employees are "a bunch of crooks" who must have stolen the missing 30 pesos.

---

## Character Analysis

### A. Lencho
*   **Hardworking Farmer:** He is described as an "ox of a man," working tirelessly in his fields. He has a deep, intimate knowledge of his land.
*   **Unwavering Faith:** His faith in God is absolute and literal. He believes God sees everything and will not let him go hungry.
*   **Simple and Naive:** While he can read and write, he lacks a complex understanding of how the world works.
*   **Strong-Willed:** Even after his crops are destroyed, he immediately seeks a solution.

### B. The Postmaster
*   **Kind and Generous:** Initially amused by Lencho's letter, he soon becomes moved by the man's faith.
*   **Empathetic Leader:** He organizes a collection among his employees and friends to help a stranger.
*   **Humble:** He doesn't take credit for the money, signing it "God" to protect Lencho's belief.

---

## Central Themes

### 1. Extreme Faith
The story explores the power of faith, but also its "blindness." Lencho's faith is so strong that he cannot see the human kindness right in front of him.

### 2. Irony of Situation
The greatest irony is that the people who acted as "God’s hands" (the postal staff) are accused of being thieves.

### 3. Conflict between Humans and Nature
While nature (the hail) destroys Lencho’s livelihood, human empathy tries to restore it, yet the two forces remain misunderstood by Lencho.

## Question & Answers (NCERT Solutions)

### I. Oral Comprehension Check

**1. What did Lencho hope for?**
**Ans:** Lencho hoped for rains as the only thing that his field of ripe corn needed was a shower.

**2. Why did Lencho say the raindrops were like 'new coins'?**
**Ans:** Lencho's crops were ready for harvest. After a downpour, he could sell the harvest and get money. That is why he compared the raindrops to 'new coins'.

**3. How did the rain change? What happened to Lencho's fields?**
**Ans:** The rain was pouring down. But suddenly, a strong wind began to blow and very large hailstones began to fall. The hail rained on the valley for an hour, destroying Lencho's fields completely. There was not a single leaf left on the trees and the flowers were gone.

**4. What were Lencho's feelings when the hail stopped?**
**Ans:** When the hail stopped, Lencho's soul was filled with sadness. He said that even a plague of locusts would have left more than the hailstorm. He feared they would have no corn that year and would go hungry.

**5. Who or what did Lencho have faith in? What did he do?**
**Ans:** Lencho had absolute faith in God. He believed God's eyes see everything. Therefore, he wrote a letter to God expressing his need for a hundred pesos to sow his field again.

**6. Who read the letter?**
**Ans:** When the postman saw the letter addressed to God, he laughed and took it to the postmaster, who then read it.

**7. What did the postmaster do then?**
**Ans:** In order to keep Lencho's faith alive, the postmaster decided to answer the letter. He collected money from his employees and gave part of his salary. He gathered seventy pesos, put it in an envelope addressed to Lencho, and signed it 'God'.

**8. Was Lencho surprised to find a letter for him with money in it?**
**Ans:** No. Lencho was not at all surprised. His confidence and faith in God were so absolute that he expected that reply.

**9. What made him angry?**
**Ans:** He got angry when he counted the money. There were only seventy pesos in the envelope. He was confident God could not make a mistake, so he concluded the post office employees must have stolen the remaining thirty pesos.

### II. Thinking About the Text

**1. Who does Lencho have complete faith in? Which sentences in the story tell you this?**
**Ans:** Lencho had complete faith in God. The sentences showing this are:
(i) But in the hearts of all who lived in that solitary house in the middle of the valley, there was a single hope: help from God.
(ii) All through the night, Lencho thought only of his one hope: the help of God, whose eyes, as he had been instructed, see everything.
(iii) "God," he wrote, "if you don't help me, my family and I will go hungry this year."
(iv) God could not have made a mistake, nor could he have denied Lencho what he had requested.

**2. Why does the postmaster send money to Lencho? Why does he sign the letter 'God'?**
**Ans:** The postmaster sent money to keep Lencho's profound faith in God alive. He was deeply moved by Lencho's letter. He signed it 'God' so that Lencho would truly believe God had answered him, preventing his faith from being shaken.

**3. Did Lencho try to find out who had sent the money to him? Why / Why not?**
**Ans:** No, Lencho did not try to find out who had sent the money. His faith in God was so strong that he never suspected it could be anyone else but God who sent him the money.

**4. Who does Lencho think has taken the rest of the money? What is the irony in the situation?**
**Ans:** Lencho thought that the post office employees had taken the rest of the money. The irony of the situation is that the employees whom he called a "bunch of crooks" were the very same people who had selflessly contributed their own money to help him.

**5. There are two kinds of conflict in the story: between humans and nature, and between humans themselves. How are these conflicts illustrated?**
**Ans:** The conflict between humans and nature is illustrated by the destruction of Lencho's crops by the hailstorm. Despite his hard work, nature destroyed his livelihood. The conflict between humans themselves is illustrated when Lencho blames the post office employees for stealing his money. Even though they performed an act of immense kindness, his lack of trust in his fellow humans led him to accuse them, highlighting the tragic lack of understanding between people.
