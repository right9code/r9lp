# Two Stories about Flying - Class 10 Comprehensive Notes

**Authors:** Liam O'Flaherty (Part 1) & Frederick Forsyth (Part 2)
**(From the textbook *First Flight*)**

---

## Detailed Summary

### Part 1: His First Flight
1.  **The Failed Attempt:** The young seagull runs to the brink of the ledge but becomes terrified by the vast expanse of the sea. He retreats to his "little hole" in shame.
2.  **Social Isolation:** His parents, two brothers, and sister fly away. His parents "upbraid" (scold) him and threaten to let him starve unless he takes flight.
3.  **The 24-Hour Wait:** He watches his brothers catching their first herring and his parents circling with pride. He stands on one leg, pretending to sleep to get attention, but his family ignores him.
4.  **The Hunger Trigger:** The sun rises, and he feels the heat intensely because he hasn't eaten. He sees his mother tearing at a piece of fish on a distant plateau.
5.  **The Deception:** He begs for food. His mother picks up a piece of fish and flies across to him. Just as she reaches the ledge, she halts in mid-air, dangling the food just out of reach.
6.  **The Leap of Faith:** Maddened by hunger, he dives at the fish. He falls into the void. For a "monstrous moment," his heart stands still.
7.  **The Flight:** Suddenly, he feels his wings spread outward. The wind rushes against his breast feathers. He realizes he is no longer falling but soaring.
8.  **The Family Celebration:** His family joins him, "curvetting" and "banking" around him. He lands on the green sea; his legs sink at first, but then he floats, finally having "made his first flight."

### Part 2: Black Aeroplane
1.  **The Clear Night:** The narrator is flying his old Dakota (DS 088) from France to England at 1:30 AM. The moon is up, and stars are shining.
2.  **The First Check-in:** He contacts Paris Control. They advise him to turn 12 degrees west. Everything is going smoothly.
3.  **The Storm Clouds:** 150 km from Paris, he sees "Black Mountains" of clouds. He doesn't have enough fuel to go around or over them.
4.  **The Fatal Decision:** Driven by the thought of an "English breakfast," he flies the Dakota straight into the storm.
5.  **The Technical Failure:** Inside the clouds, everything is black. The plane jumps and twists. His compass spins round and round (dead). The radio is dead. He is lost.
6.  **The Savior Appears:** He sees another aeroplane. It has no lights on its wings. The pilot waves his hand, signaling the narrator to "Follow me."
7.  **The Descent:** The narrator follows the black plane like an "obedient child." He grows worried because he only has fuel for 5 or 10 more minutes.
8.  **The Landing:** Suddenly, he comes out of the clouds and sees two straight lines of lights—a runway. He lands safely.
9.  **The Unsolved Mystery:** He goes to the Control Center to ask who the other pilot was. The woman in the tower looks at him strangely and says, "No other aeroplanes were flying tonight. Yours was the only one I could see on the radar."

## Character Analysis

### Part 1: His First Flight
#### 1. The Young Seagull
* **Insecure and Fearful:** Unlike his siblings, he is convinced his wings are too weak to support him. He represents the "fear of the unknown."
* **Observant:** He spends 24 hours watching his family from his ledge, feeling the sting of isolation.
* **Driven by Instinct:** While fear holds him back, his biological need (hunger) eventually overrides his mental barrier.
* **Triumphant:** Once he overcomes the initial shock of falling, he discovers his innate ability to soar and float.

#### 2. The Mother Seagull
* **The Strategist:** She is the primary motivator. She recognizes that sympathy will only make her son weaker.
* **Determined and Stern:** She ignores his cries to teach him the value of self-reliance.
* **Clever:** She uses the "bait" (the fish) to lure him into a situation where he has no choice but to fly.

### Part 2: Black Aeroplane
#### 1. The Narrator (The Pilot)
* **Home-sick:** His desire to be with his family for breakfast influences him to make a dangerous decision.
* **Risk-taker:** He chooses to fly into the storm rather than going back to Paris, showing a mix of bravery and recklessness.
* **Grateful:** He is deeply affected by the help he receives and seeks to thank the mysterious pilot.

#### 2. The Mysterious Pilot (The Black Aeroplane)
* **Enigmatic/Ghostly:** He appears in a plane with no lights in the middle of a storm.
* **Calm and Guiding:** He signals the narrator like a friend, leading him out of the darkness.
* **Vanishing Act:** He disappears without a trace as soon as the danger is over.

## Question & Answers (NCERT Solutions)

### Part 1: His First Flight

**1. Why was the young seagull afraid to fly? Do you think all young birds are afraid to make their first flight, or are some birds more timid than others? Do you think a human baby also finds it a challenge to take its first step?**
**Ans:** The young seagull was afraid to fly because it was his first flight and he feared falling and hurting himself. He thought that his wings would not support him while flying. Yes, it is natural that doing something for the first time is a bit challenging and fearful. All birds must be afraid to make their first flight. Similarly, a human baby is also afraid of taking its first step and finds it challenging when learning to walk without support.

**2. "The sight of the food maddened him." What does this suggest? What compelled the young seagull to finally fly?**
**Ans:** The young seagull was very hungry. It was this hunger that ultimately compelled it to fly. Its hunger intensified when it saw its mother tearing at a piece of fish that lay at her feet. It cried to her, begging her to get some food. When its mother came towards it with food in her beak, it screamed with joy and anticipation. However, she stopped midway. Wondering why she did not come nearer, and not being able to resist or control its hunger any longer, it dived at the food in its mother's beak. At that moment, his hunger overpowered his fear of the great expanse of sea beneath the cliff. Finally, this plunge was followed by the natural reaction of its body, i.e., to fly.

**3. "They were beckoning to him, calling shrilly." Why did the seagull's father and mother threaten him and cajole him to fly?**
**Ans:** The seagull's parents had tried everything, but he was reluctant to fly due to fear of falling down. He looked at his brothers and sister but wouldn't make any effort. That's why the whole family had left him alone and threatened and cajoled him to come out, as they wanted him to become self-reliant and independent.

### Part 2: Black Aeroplane

**1. "I'll take the risk". What is the risk? Why does the narrator take it?**
**Ans:** A huge storm was brewing up and the narrator was keen to reach his home to spend his holiday with his family. The risk was flying his old Dakota airplane straight into the dark storm clouds. He decided to fly through the storm because he did not want to miss the chance to meet his family and have a good English breakfast. Thus, he took the risk even when the visibility was almost zero.

**2. Describe the narrator's experience as he flew the aeroplane into the storm.**
**Ans:** As the pilot entered the storm, his plane started jumping and twisting in the air. He could not see anything outside the plane as it was completely black. When he looked at the compass and other instruments, they had stopped functioning due to the storm. It was a terrible and fearsome experience for him. The fuel tank was almost empty, and he could not fly for more than ten minutes. Then he saw another black aeroplane by his side, and the pilot of the plane signaled him to follow. It was a surprise for the narrator as the other black plane had no lights on its wings. He followed him without any choice and landed safely on the runway.

**3. Why does the narrator say, "I landed and was not sorry to walk away from the old Dakota…"?**
**Ans:** The narrator says so because the old Dakota had nearly carried him to the verge of death with a dead compass, a dead radio, and dead instruments. He had a horrific experience in the storm, and he was simply relieved to be safely on the ground. Moreover, he wanted to immediately go to the control tower to ask about the other pilot who had saved him.

**4. What made the woman in the Control Centre look at the narrator strangely?**
**Ans:** The woman in the Control Centre looked at the narrator strangely because the narrator asked her about the "other" black aeroplane and the pilot who saved him. She found this strange because she had seen no other plane except the narrator's in the sky during the fierce storm. The radar showed only his plane that night.

**5. Who do you think helped the narrator to reach safely? Discuss this among yourselves and give reasons for your answer.**
**Ans:** It is very difficult to definitively say who the unknown pilot was. But most likely, it was the narrator himself that helped him to overcome the fear in the storm, as no other plane was seen on the radar. In that extremely stressful and fearsome situation, he might have been hallucinating. He was a brave and skilled pilot, and his own subconscious mind guided him through the storm, helping him land safely.
