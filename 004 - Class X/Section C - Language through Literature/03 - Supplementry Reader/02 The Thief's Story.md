# The Thief's Story - Class 10 Comprehensive Notes

**Author:** Ruskin Bond
**(From the textbook *Footprints without Feet*)**

---

## Detailed Summary

**Phase 1: The Encounter**
*   **The Meeting:** Hari Singh approaches Anil at a wrestling match, identifying him as an easy target—someone "simple enough for my purpose."
*   **The Bargain:** Hari lies about being a cook. Anil agrees to feed him if he can cook. The first meal is "terrible," but Anil’s kindness prevents him from kicking Hari out.
*   **The Education:** Anil begins teaching Hari to write his name and promises to teach him to write whole sentences and add numbers.

**Phase 2: The Routine**
*   **Daily Life:** Hari makes tea in the morning and buys the day’s supplies. He makes a profit of about **one rupee a day** from the groceries, which Anil likely knows but ignores.
*   **Anil’s Finances:** Anil writes for magazines. One day he is rich, the next he is worried about his next check.

**Phase 3: The Theft**
*   **The Opportunity:** Anil brings home a bundle of notes (600 rupees in fifties) from selling a book. He tucks the money under the mattress.
*   **The Act:** Hari creeps to the bed, slides his hand under the mattress, and takes the money. He rushes to the railway station to catch the 10:30 Express to Lucknow.

**Phase 4: The Internal Conflict**
*   **The Hesitation:** At the platform, Hari’s "inner voice" stops him. He realizes that stealing from a greedy man is easy, but stealing from a **careless, trusting man** like Anil "takes all the pleasure out of the work."
*   **The Rain:** It begins to pour. Hari sits in a park, the notes damp in his pocket. He reflects on how Anil was teaching him to be a "big man, a clever and respected man"—something far more valuable than a few hundred rupees.

**Phase 5: The Return**
*   **The Risk:** Hari returns to the room, "very nervous," because it is much harder to return stolen money undetected than to steal it.
*   **The Restoration:** He slides the damp notes back under the mattress while Anil sleeps.

**Phase 6: The Silent Forgiveness**
*   **The Next Morning:** Anil wakes up and gives Hari a **fifty-rupee note**. It is still damp from the rain.
*   **The Revelation:** Anil says, "Now you’ll be paid regularly." Both men know what happened, but Anil says nothing. Hari’s smile this time is "genuine" and "of its own accord."

---

## Character Analysis

### A. Hari Singh (The Narrator)
*   **Experienced Thief:** Despite being only 15, he is an "experienced and fairly successful hand" at stealing.
*   **Manipulative:** He uses a fake name and a "most appealing smile" to gain the trust of his victims.
*   **Observant:** He studies human faces to understand their reactions to loss (greed, anger, or acceptance).
*   **Transformative:** Initially viewing education only as a tool to become a more "clever" thief, he eventually realizes its value for a dignified, honest life.

### B. Anil
*   **Trusting and Kind:** He gives a total stranger a key to his room and trusts him with his money and groceries.
*   **Easy-going and Carefree:** A struggling writer who earns "by fits and starts." He celebrates when he has money and borrows when he doesn’t.
*   **A Patient Teacher:** He doesn't get angry at Hari’s bad cooking; instead, he teaches him how to cook and, more importantly, how to read and write.
*   **Compassionate:** By the end, it is implied he knows about the theft but chooses to forgive Hari without a word, proving that kindness can reform a person.

---

## Central Themes

*   **The Power of Trust:** The story demonstrates that trust and kindness are more effective tools for reform than punishment or the law.
*   **The Value of Education:** Education is portrayed as a bridge from a life of crime to a life of dignity and self-respect.
*   **Human Relationships:** The bond between Anil and Hari shows how one person’s goodness can influence and change the moral compass of another.
*   **Redemption:** It is never too late to change. Even a "successful" thief can choose the path of honesty when shown genuine empathy.

---

## Exam-Style Value Points (6-Mark Questions)
1. **The Catalyst for Change:** It wasn't the fear of police, but the fear of losing an opportunity to be an educated, respected man that stopped Hari Singh.
2. **Anil's Tacit Forgiveness:** By accepting the wet note without scolding Hari, Anil ensures Hari doesn't regress out of shame. The forgiveness is complete because it is silent.
3. **The Irony of the "Smile":** Hari's first smile was fake and manipulative; his final smile was genuine, reflecting his internal transformation.

## Question & Answers (NCERT Solutions)

### I. Read and Find out

**1. Who does 'I' refer to in this story?**
**Ans:** In this story, 'I' refers to the thief.

**2. What is he "a fairly successful hand" at?**
**Ans:** He was "a fairly successful hand" at stealing and robbing people.

**3. What does he get from Anil in return for his work?**
**Ans:** When Hari asked Anil if he could work for him, Anil said that he could not pay him. Finally, the agreement was that if he cooked, then Anil would feed him. However, Anil soon found out that he did not know how to cook. Therefore, he taught him how to cook and later, how to write his name. He promised he would teach him how to write whole sentences and how to add numbers. Apart from this, when Hari went out to buy the day's supplies, he would make a profit of a rupee a day.

**4. How does the thief think Anil will react to the theft?**
**Ans:** The thief thought that on discovering the theft, Anil's face would show a touch of sadness. The sadness would not be for the loss of money, but for the loss of trust.

**5. What does he say about the different reactions of people when they are robbed?**
**Ans:** In his short career as a thief, he had made a study of men's faces when they lost their goods. He said that the greedy men showed fear; the rich men showed anger and the poor men showed acceptance.

**6. Does Anil realize that he has been robbed?**
**Ans:** Yes, Anil had realized that he had been robbed. He knew this probably because all the notes were wet and damp from the rain. However, he did not say anything to the thief and behaved normally.

### II. Think about It

**1. What are Hari Singh's reactions to the prospect of receiving an education? Do they change over time? (Hint: compare, for example, the thought: "I knew that once I could write like an educated man there would be no limit to what I could achieve" with these later thoughts: 'Whole sentences, I knew, could one day bring me more than a few hundred rupees. It was a simple matter to steal and sometimes just as simple to be caught. But to be a really big man, a clever and respected man, was something else.") What makes him return to Anil?**
**Ans:** Hari Singh's reactions to the prospect of receiving an education change over time. When he started living with Anil he wanted to be literate so that he could become a more cunning cheat. Later on there is a change in his heart. He wants to earn respect in life. Probably he is influenced by the calm in Anil's life. Although, both of them depend on irregular sources of income, but Anil seems to be more satisfied. Hari Singh develops a desire to be part of the civilized society.

**2. Why does not Anil hand the thief over to the police? Do you think most people would have done so? In what ways is Anil different from such employers?**
**Ans:** Anil does not hand the thief over to the police because he has put the money back at its place. It shows that he realized his fault. He wants to make amends for his ways. He must have felt guilty with himself. Anil wants to give him one more chance and therefore forgives him. No, in today's world most people would not have done so. Although it must be done but such sort of patience and compassion are rarely found now a days in this human world.

**3. Do you think people like Anil and Hari Singh are found only in fiction, or are there such people in real life?**
**Ans:** No, I don't think that people like Anil and Hari Singh are found only in fiction. There are such people in real life also. Although such people are rare in today's society. If one is caught stealing, people will first beat him severely. Then he will be handed over to the police. It should be realized that a human being is bound to commit errors. One chance for reforming oneself must be given. This is the only way to bring anyone to right path. Punishing severely is not the only solution. Strictness can make them an absolute criminal.

**4. Do you think it a significant detail in the story that Anil is a struggling writer? Does this explain his behaviour in any way?**
**Ans:** Hari Singh's observation about Anil getting irregular income and Anil's statement about his sale of a book to a publisher indicates that he is a struggling writer. He lives life according to the flow of money he gets at a given time. When he gets the money he enjoys eating out with his friends. When he is having less money then he even doesn't accept paying salary to Hari Singh.
