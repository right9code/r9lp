# The Midnight Visitor - Class 10 Comprehensive Notes

**Author:** Robert Arthur
**(From the textbook *Footprints without Feet*)**

---

## Detailed Summary

**Phase 1: The Disappointment**
*   **The Meeting:** Fowler follows Ausable to his sixth-floor room. He is let down by Ausable’s appearance and the dull, unromantic setting of the hotel.
*   **The Revelation:** Ausable tells Fowler that a very important "paper" regarding some missiles is coming to him that night—a paper that could change the course of history.

**Phase 2: The Midnight Surprise**
*   **The Intruder:** When they switch on the light, they find Max standing in the middle of the room with a small automatic pistol.
*   **The Demand:** Max demands the report on the missiles, claiming he wants it for "greater safety" in his own hands.

**Phase 3: The Fabrication (The Balcony Lie)**
*   **The Setup:** Ausable immediately moves to an armchair and grumbles about a "balcony" under his window. He claims this is the second time in a month someone has broken into his room through it.
*   **The Detailed Lie:** He explains that the balcony belongs to the next apartment and extends under his window. This detail makes the non-existent balcony feel real to the nervous Max.

**Phase 4: The Climax (The Knocking)**
*   **The Sound:** A sudden, loud knocking is heard at the door.
*   **The "Police" Trap:** Ausable tells Max that it must be the police. He claims he asked them to check on him because the missile report was so important.
*   **The Panic:** Max, fearing arrest, backs toward the window. He threatens to wait on the "balcony" and tells Ausable to send the police away.

**Phase 5: The Fatal Jump**
*   **The Leap:** As the knocking becomes louder and a voice calls "Mr. Ausable!", Max drops his legs over the windowsill and jumps into the night. 
*   **The Scream:** A shrill scream is heard as Max falls to the ground—there was no balcony; the room was on the sixth floor.

**Phase 6: The Resolution**
*   **The Reveal:** The door opens, and it is not the police, but **Henry, the waiter**, carrying the drinks Ausable had ordered earlier.
*   **The Final Twist:** A white-faced Fowler asks about the man on the balcony. Ausable calmly replies, "No... he won't return."

---

## Character Analysis

### A. Ausable (The Secret Agent)
*   **Deceptively Ordinary:** Unlike the "James Bond" stereotype, he is very fat, speaks with a slight American accent despite living in Paris for 20 years, and stays in a small, "musty" hotel room.
*   **Quick-Witted:** His greatest weapon is his mind. He improvises a complex lie about a balcony within seconds of seeing an intruder.
*   **Calm under Pressure:** Even with a pistol pointed at him, he doesn't panic. He sits down and complains about the "management" to make his story about the balcony more believable.
*   **Master of Psychology:** He knows exactly how to use Max’s fear and the sudden knocking at the door to manipulate him into jumping.

### B. Max (The Intruder/Antagonist)
*   **Slender and "Crafty":** Described as looking like a fox. He is a rival secret agent who is "not especially tall."
*   **Overconfident:** He believes that having a gun gives him total control over the situation, which makes him blind to Ausable’s mental traps.
*   **Gullible:** Despite being a spy, he lacks critical thinking. He fails to check for a balcony and immediately believes that the knocking at the door is the police.

### C. Fowler (The Romantic Writer)
*   **Disillusioned:** A young writer who expected secret agents to be mysterious, athletic men in "dark overcoats" dealing with "pistols and drugs." He is initially bored by Ausable.
*   **The Audience Proxy:** He represents the reader’s surprise. His "thrill" begins when he sees Max with a gun, and his "terror" peaks when he thinks the police are at the door.

---

## Central Themes

*   **Brain over Brawn:** Intelligence and presence of mind are more powerful than physical strength or weapons.
*   **Appearances are Deceptive:** A person’s outward appearance (like Ausable’s weight or dull room) does not reflect their inner capability or professional skill.
*   **Coolness in Crisis:** The ability to stay calm and think logically during a life-threatening situation is the mark of a true professional.

---

## Exam-Style Value Points (6-Mark Questions)
1. **The Power of Presence of Mind:** Ausable defeats an armed man simply by using his imagination and capitalizing on the environment.
2. **Subverting Stereotypes:** The story plays with the cliché of the glamorous spy, proving that real intelligence doesn't need to look heroic.
3. **The Interplay of Fear and Logic:** Max's fear of the police makes him abandon logic. He jumps without looking, proving that panic is a fatal weakness.

## Question & Answers (NCERT Solutions)

### I. Read and Find out

**1. How is Ausable different from other secret agents?**
**Ans:** Ausable is a secret agent but he is very fat and not much tall. His accent is also not proper. He appears to be completely different from other secret agents.

**2. Who is Fowler and what is his first authentic thrill of the day?**
**Ans:** Fowler is a young and romantic writer who wants to write a book on detectives. His first authentic thrill of the day was when he saw a man standing before them in Ausable's room holding a small automatic pistol in his hand.

**3. How has Max got in?**
**Ans:** Max has got in by using a passkey in Ausable's absence.

**4. How does Ausable say he got in?**
**Ans:** Ausable says Max has got in his room through an ordinary window. It was in the balcony.

### II. Think About It

**1. "Ausable did not fit any description of a secret agent Fowler had ever read." What do secret agents in books and films look like, in your opinion?**
**Ans:** Secret agents in fiction are projected like ideal men, 'tall dark and handsome'. They are usually well-built and have beautiful women for company. They would always smoke pipe or cigar and do death defying stunts. James Bond is a very famous character by Ian Fleming. Movies based on James Bond show hi-tech gizmos which assist the detective in countering villains. There are some exceptions as well. There is a character named Feluda which was created by Satyajit Ray, the famous Bangla Filmmaker. Feluda was typical bhadralok by appearance.

**2. How does Ausable manage to make Max believe that there is a balcony attached to his room? Look back at his detailed description of it. What makes it a convincing story?**
**Ans:** Ausable creates a detailed description of how his office was part of a bigger apartment and how the next room had direct connection with the balcony. His statement that somebody else also broke into his office through that balcony made it a convincing story.

**3. Looking back at the story, when do you think Ausable thought up his plan for getting rid of Max? Do you think he had worked out his plan in detail right from the beginning? Or did he make up a plan taking advantage of events as they happened?**
**Ans:** Ausable is very clever the way a detective should be. He made the plan to get rid of Max from the very beginning which is evident from the story of the imaginary balcony. He knew the waiter would come to deliver his drinks. He planned carefully to create an imaginary policeman which would have compelled Max to jump on the non-existent balcony.
